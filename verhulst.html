Verhulst

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Evan Verhulst (born November 27, 2002), known online by the mononym Verhulst, is an American professional Apex Legends player currently competing for Crazy Thieves. He is best known for his three-and-a-half year tenure with TSM, where alongside ImperialHal and Reps he formed the most decorated trio in ALGS history and won the 2023 ALGS Championship for a $600,000 first-place share.[1][2]

Verhulst joined TSM in December 2021 and remained on the roster through February 2025, before brief stints with 100 Thieves and his current team Crazy Thieves. With approximately $672,517 in tracked tournament earnings, he is one of the top three highest-earning Apex players of all time.[3]

Early life

Verhulst was born November 27, 2002 in the United States. He began playing Apex Legends competitively in 2021 at age 18, climbing the ranked ladder before being picked up by amateur and tier-two organizations later that year.[1]

Competitive career

Early teams (2021)

Verhulst's first competitive run was with Dudes Night Out in June 2021. He moved to Team Intel from June through October 2021, then to Esports Arena from October through December 2021. The trial periods established him as a top-tier rifler in the North American region and led to interest from larger orgs.

TSM era (2021-2025)

In December 2021, Verhulst joined TSM alongside ImperialHal and Reps. Under ImperialHal's in-game leadership, the trio quickly became the dominant force in NA. Together they won the 2022 ALGS Year 2 Split 1 Playoffs ($100,000) and the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 1 Playoffs ($300,000), and finished runner-up at the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs ($160,000).[2]

2023 ALGS Championship

The 2023 ALGS Championship at the Birmingham BEC in July 2023 was the high-water mark of Verhulst's career. As the trio's primary rifler, Verhulst accumulated multiple high-elimination games during the Match Point round and helped TSM secure the trophy and $600,000 first-place share.[2][4] The win cemented the TSM core as the most decorated ALGS lineup of all time.

100 Thieves and Crazy Thieves (2025-present)

After the TSM core dissolved in early 2025, Verhulst joined 100 Thieves in March 2025. He competed with the org for ten months including a 6th-place finish at the 2026 ALGS Championship in Sapporo ($100,000). On January 7, 2026, he moved to Crazy Thieves, where he plays alongside Falloutt and former NRG IGL Albralelie. The new lineup currently sits 8th in the AgentTV Power Rankings.[3]

Legacy and impact

Verhulst is regarded as one of the most consistent riflers in ALGS history. His ability to convert third-party engagements and lock down close-range fights was a defining trait of the TSM dynasty. As of May 2026, his $672,517 in tournament earnings places him third on the all-time Apex Legends earnings chart behind ImperialHal and Reps.

Personal life

Verhulst maintains a regular Twitch streaming schedule under his own name and is active on Twitter and YouTube. He is based in the United States and travels for international Apex LANs.
