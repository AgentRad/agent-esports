Shotzzy

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Anthony Cuevas-Castro (born July 4, 2001), known online as Shotzzy, is an American professional Call of Duty player currently signed to OpTic Texas. The roster's primary SMG slayer, he has captured three Call of Duty League Championships, was named MVP of the 2025 Esports World Cup, and has accumulated approximately $1,511,346 in tracked tournament earnings, ranking among the all-time top earners in the discipline.[1]

Cuevas-Castro turned professional with Dallas Empire on October 15, 2019, won the inaugural Call of Duty League Championship in 2020 in his rookie season, and has been with OpTic Texas since the franchise's relocation in November 2021. With OpTic he won back-to-back CDL Championships in 2024 and 2025, becoming the only player to be named CDL Championship Finals MVP in consecutive seasons.[1][2]

Early life and Halo background

Cuevas-Castro was born on July 4, 2001 and made his initial mark in competitive gaming in the Halo Championship Series, where he was widely regarded as one of the cleanest individual aimers in the title's late-2010s era. He transitioned to Call of Duty in late 2019 with the launch of Modern Warfare (2019), which introduced the franchised CDL and offered a roster-construction model that valued his pure mechanical ceiling.[1]

Dallas Empire (2019 to 2021)

Shotzzy signed with Dallas Empire on October 15, 2019. The squad of Crimsix, Clayster, Huke and iLLeY alongside him was the inaugural CDL Champion in 2020, defeating Atlanta FaZe in the final on Modern Warfare (2019). Cuevas-Castro was named the league's regular-season MVP and the SCUF Team of the Year SMG, an unusually decorated rookie season.[2]

The Dallas roster contracted in 2021 and finished outside the title hunt across the Black Ops Cold War calendar. The organisation rebranded into OpTic Texas for the 2022 season, with Cuevas-Castro retained as the cornerstone of the new project.

OpTic Texas (2021 to present)

OpTic Texas was unveiled on November 8, 2021. Across the Vanguard and Modern Warfare II seasons the team produced multiple Stage Major wins (Stage 4 Major 2022 among them) and consistent playoff appearances without converting a Championship. The 2024 reset around Dashy, Huke, Kenny and Shotzzy was the breakthrough: OpTic Texas won the CDL Championship 2024 in Allen, Texas, defeating New York Subliners 5-1 in the grand final. Cuevas-Castro was named Finals MVP.[1]

Texas defended successfully at CDL Championship 2025, becoming the first organisation to repeat as CDL Champion. They also captured the Esports World Cup 2025 Call of Duty bracket in Riyadh, where Cuevas-Castro was named the event MVP. He has now collected three CDL rings (2020, 2024, 2025), two consecutive Champs Finals MVP honours and an EWC MVP, a combination unmatched in the franchise era.[1]

Playstyle and analysis

Cuevas-Castro is the modern template for the SMG slayer in Call of Duty, the role Scump defined a generation earlier. His game is built around immaculate close-range gunfights, a reliably aggressive Hardpoint rotation and a willingness to take the first picture on Search and Destroy. Coaches who have worked with him point to his ability to extract value from rounds where his AR partners have been pinned by counter-utility, and his adjustments mid-series remain among the cleanest in the league. He is also unusually consistent across the franchise's revolving title cycle, with top-three K/D finishes across at least four distinct game engines, a structural durability that explains why every coach in the discipline ranks him in the top tier of all-time slayers.

Legacy and awards

  • 2020: CDL Champion (Dallas Empire), Regular Season MVP, SCUF Team of the Year
  • 2022: First All-Star Team (CDL)
  • 2023: Second All-Star Team (CDL)
  • 2024: CDL Champion (OpTic Texas), CDL Playoffs MVP, Second All-Star Team
  • 2025: CDL Champion (OpTic Texas), Esports World Cup MVP

Three CDL rings places him alongside Crimsix in the all-time list, and the back-to-back Finals MVP honours are an outlier in the discipline's modern history.[1][2]
