Ninja

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports.

Richard Tyler Blevins (born June 5, 1991), known online as Ninja, is an American Twitch streamer, YouTuber, and retired professional gamer. Best known for popularizing Fortnite on Twitch in 2018, he became the most-followed streamer on the platform and arguably the single most influential figure in mainstream esports recognition.[1]

Blevins competed professionally in Fortnite from 2018 to 2023, accumulating roughly $158,000 in tracked tournament earnings. His broader gaming career spans Halo (his original competitive title), H1Z1, PUBG, and Apex Legends. As of 2026 he focuses on streaming and content creation.

Early life

Blevins was born June 5, 1991 in Detroit, Michigan, the youngest of four siblings. His family relocated to Lemont, Illinois, where he attended Grayslake Central High School. He picked up Halo 3 competitively in his teens, eventually qualifying for MLG events through online team play.

Halo competitive career

From 2009 through 2017, Blevins competed in Halo professionally for organizations including Cloud9, Renegades, Team Liquid, and Luminosity Gaming. While never reaching the top tier of Halo competition, his consistent streaming presence built a following that would prove pivotal during his Fortnite transition.

Fortnite era and mainstream rise

Ninja began streaming Fortnite Battle Royale in late 2017, just as the game was emerging as a global phenomenon. By March 2018 he was breaking concurrent viewer records, including a then-historic stream with rapper Drake that peaked at over 600,000 concurrent viewers.

He competed in the 2018 Summer Skirmish series, the 2019 Fortnite World Cup as part of the Pro-Am side event, and various Twitch Rivals showcases. While never a top competitive placement player, his cultural footprint on Fortnite is unmatched.[2]

Mixer deal and return to Twitch

In August 2019, Blevins signed an exclusivity deal with Microsoft's Mixer streaming platform, reportedly worth between $20 and $30 million. Mixer shut down in July 2020, and after a brief period as a free agent, Blevins returned to streaming exclusively on Twitch in September 2020.

Legacy

By 2026, Ninja's combined social audience exceeds 50 million followers across platforms (18M+ Twitch, 24M YouTube, 14M Twitter, 13M Instagram). His 2018 ESPN The Magazine cover was the first to feature an esports figure. He hosted Fortnite events with athletes (Travis Scott, Drake, JuJu Smith-Schuster) that helped redefine what mainstream esports crossovers looked like.
