Matias Delipetro (born March 8, 1997), known online as saadhak, is an Argentine professional Valorant player currently signed to KRÜ Esports as in-game leader. He won VALORANT Champions 2022 in Istanbul with LOUD, becoming the first Latin American player to lift a VCT global trophy. He is widely regarded as one of the most accomplished controller-role IGLs in Valorant history.[1][2]

Delipetro played for LOUD from 2022 through 2024, captained the org's iconic Brazilian roster alongside aspas, Less, Sacy and pANcada, and signed with Karmine Corp for the 2025 VCT EMEA season. He returned to South America with KRÜ Esports in December 2025.[3]

Early life

Delipetro was born March 8, 1997, in Argentina. He competed in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive at a tier-two South American level for several years before transitioning to Valorant in late 2020.[1]

Competitive career

CS:GO origins (2014-2020)

Delipetro played CS:GO at a tier-two SA level under the same handle through the late 2010s, posting consistent in-game leader numbers on Argentine and regional rosters before pivoting to Valorant.

Team Vikings (2021)

He signed with Brazilian org Team Vikings in January 2021, captained the squad through VCT 2021 Brazil, and reached top-tier domestic placements. Vikings rebranded and dispersed in late 2021, with Delipetro becoming a free agent ahead of franchising.

LOUD and VCT Champions 2022

Delipetro signed with LOUD on February 3, 2022, joining a Brazilian core of pANcada, Sacy, aspas and Less. He spoke Portuguese fluently with the roster despite being Argentine and assumed the IGL role on Omen.

LOUD won VCT 2022 Brazil Stage 1, finished second at Masters Reykjavik 2022 after losing to OpTic Gaming, and entered Champions in Istanbul as a top-tier favorite. At VALORANT Champions 2022, LOUD ran through the lower bracket and defeated OpTic Gaming 3-1 in the Grand Final on September 18, 2022, with Delipetro calling on Omen.[2]

LOUD finished third at VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles after a top-2 placement in the regional VCT Americas League. Delipetro remained captain through 2024 before parting ways with the org on September 5, 2024.

Karmine Corp (2024-2025)

Delipetro signed with French org Karmine Corp on November 9, 2024, joining a partnered VCT EMEA franchise as the captain and Omen player for the 2025 season. Karmine reached the Stage 1 playoff bracket but missed international qualification, parting ways with Delipetro on November 17, 2025.[3]

KRÜ Esports (2025-present)

Delipetro returned to South America by signing with Argentine org KRÜ Esports on December 13, 2025, reuniting with former LOUD teammate Less as part of a Spanish-speaking VCT Americas Ascension project for the 2026 season.

Legacy and impact

Delipetro is widely credited with proving that a non-Brazilian could captain a Brazilian Valorant roster. He holds the Champions 2022 trophy as the first global VCT title for Latin America. As of May 2026, his $146,764 in tracked tournament earnings places him among the top-10 Argentine esports earners across all titles.

Personal life

Delipetro is fluent in Spanish, Portuguese and English. He has spoken publicly about adapting to a Brazilian roster as the lone Argentine, and he is known for his vocal Omen play and patient mid-round calls.
