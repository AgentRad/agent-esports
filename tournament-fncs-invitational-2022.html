About the Tournament
Raleigh Convention Center · November 12 to 13, 2022

The 2022 FNCS Invitational was Epic's first move back to a live stage since the 2019 World Cup. The format leaned closer to that World Cup blueprint than the FNCS Majors most pros had spent two years on. Fifty duos, all of them on direct invites earned through the year's regional FNCS placements, flew into North Carolina to play twelve games over two days.

The scoring matched the regional FNCS template. Placement carried the bulk of the points, eliminations got two each, and the leaderboard reset to nothing on the morning of day two. Six games per day, no matchmaking changes, 50 duos in every lobby. It was effectively a mini World Cup with a smaller field.

Setty and Kami had quietly been the most consistent EU duo across the year. Two FNCS Majors podiums for Become Legends had earned them their invite, but few brackets pegged them as outright favorites. The Polish pair never finished outside the top eight in any game across both days and built their lead game by game without ever needing the late-event Victory Royale.

Veno and Queasy ran the most aggressive event of the field. The UK-Serbian pairing grabbed the most game wins in the tournament but two early-game wipes on day two cost them the points needed to chase Setty and Kami down. Cented and Cold rounded out the podium in the strongest finish a North American duo posted at an in-person LAN since 2019.

Day-by-Day Snapshot
Six games each day · 50 duos in every lobby
Saturday · Day 1 · 6 games
Setty & Kami sit fourth at close · Veno & Queasy lead
Veno and Queasy banked the only multi-win day in the field. They closed two Victory Royales early and were 28 points clear of the trailing pack at the halfway break. Setty and Kami had no game wins but stacked four straight top-five finishes to keep the deficit manageable.
Sunday · Day 2 · 6 games
Setty & Kami flip the script · win three of last six
Day two was Polish from start to finish. Setty and Kami won game eight, cleared a 13-elim Victory Royale in game ten, and only needed top-fifteens in the last two to lock the trophy. Veno and Queasy got eliminated by storm in game nine and never recovered the gap.
