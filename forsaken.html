Jason Susanto (born March 25, 2004), known online as f0rsakeN, is an Indonesian professional Valorant player currently signed to Paper Rex. He has been with the org since February 2021 and is widely regarded as the most decorated APAC Valorant player and one of the top flex players in the game.[1][2]

Susanto won VALORANT Masters Toronto 2025 with Paper Rex, finished as runner-up at VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles, and was named 2025 APAC Player of the Year. He is the highest-earning Pacific Valorant player as of May 2026.[3]

Early life

Susanto was born March 25, 2004, in Indonesia. He is the younger brother of former Paper Rex coach and player mindfreak, who pioneered the early Indonesian Valorant scene. Susanto entered competitive Valorant in late 2020, drawing scouting attention from regional rosters before age 17.[1]

Competitive career

Paper Rex (2021-present)

Susanto signed with Paper Rex in February 2021 as one of the original Indonesian-Singaporean roster builds. He has remained with the org continuously, the longest-tenured tenure of any active VCT Pacific player.

Paper Rex has built its identity around Susanto's flex play across controllers, sentinels and duelists. He notably plays Omen, Neon and Brimstone at top international level, and has been recognized as one of the most versatile players in Valorant history.

International runs (2022-2024)

Susanto helped Paper Rex finish 2nd at VCT Masters Copenhagen 2022, 3rd at VALORANT Champions 2022, and reach the Grand Final of VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles, losing 1-3 to Evil Geniuses.

He won VCT Pacific Stage 1 2024 with the org, defeating Gen.G 3-2, before a fourth-place finish at Masters Madrid 2024 and a top-8 finish at VALORANT Champions 2024.[2]

Masters Toronto 2025

Paper Rex won VCT Pacific Stage 1 2025 and VCT Pacific Stage 2 2025 with Susanto continuing as the team's flex anchor. The team then traveled to VCT Masters Toronto 2025 in May 2025 and defeated FNATIC 3-1 in the Grand Final, with Susanto named tournament MVP for a 1.31 average rating across the bracket.[3]

Susanto was named 2025 APAC Player of the Year by VLR.gg in December 2025 and a third consecutive APAC team-of-the-year selection.

Legacy and impact

Susanto is widely considered the best APAC Valorant player of all time. As of May 2026, his $389,898 in tracked tournament earnings is the highest of any VCT Pacific player and ranks him in the top-3 globally. He holds the record for most appearances at international VCT events for any Pacific player.

Personal life

Susanto is from Jakarta, Indonesia. He is the younger brother of mindfreak, a former Paper Rex player and coach. He streams in Indonesian and English under the handle PRXf0rsakeN.
