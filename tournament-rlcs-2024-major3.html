About the Tournament
Fort Worth · Texas · September 10 to 15, 2024

The 2024 RLCS World Championship was the thirteenth edition of the world championship and the second under the post-Psyonix Epic-published format. The tournament returned to North American soil after a year in Europe, with the Dickies Arena in Fort Worth hosting all six days of play and the $1.165M prize pool the largest at any RLCS event since the original Psyonix-era flagship.

Sixteen teams qualified through the regional ladder over the 2024 calendar year, with seeding via the season-long RLCS points table. The group stage used a Swiss System format, with teams playing best-of-five matches across five rounds. The top eight advanced to the playoffs and contested an AFL Final Eight bracket in best-of-seven, with the lower-seeded losers re-entering through a tiebreaker round.

North American interest was anchored by G2 Stride, the trio of Daniel, Atomic and BeastMode, who entered as the freshest Major winners after their Major 2: London title in June. Their Worlds bracket was framed as a coronation run from the third week of August onwards, after they cleared the regional ladder without dropping a series.

Europe sent Team BDS, Karmine Corp and Team Vitality as the deepest regional contingent of the bracket, each with realistic title cases entering the event. Karmine Corp reached their fourth straight Worlds top-four. Team Vitality, the defending champions, fell short of the medal stage on the lower side of the bracket.

Game seven of the grand final was a half-court tactical match that BDS closed out late through a wide-rotation read off a midfield contest. The MVP of the playoffs was widely identified as the BDS striker M0nkey M00n, who finished the bracket with the highest individual rating across all eight playoff teams. The off-screen story of the bracket was the post-event G2 release announcement on November 7, which moved Daniel to NRG within seven weeks.
