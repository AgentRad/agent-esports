About the Tournament
Berlin · Paris · London · 2024

The 14th edition of the world championship was the first to spread the entire tournament across three different European cities. The Riot Games Arena in Berlin handled the play-in and Swiss stages from late September into mid-October, the brand-new adidas arena in Paris hosted quarter and semifinals, and The O2 in London staged the grand final on November 2.

Twenty teams from the four major regions plus the LCS, LCO, LLA, CBLOL, PCS, LJL, and VCS were drawn into a Swiss bracket after the play-ins. T1 stumbled in their opening match before reeling off four straight wins to advance, while regional rival Gen.G entered as the LCK first seed and the consensus favourite. China's Bilibili Gaming arrived as the second-seeded LPL representative.

The bracket stage produced two LCK and two LPL semifinalists. T1 ended Top Esports in the quarters, then dispatched LPL champion BLG already once in the semis, while BLG worked through the lower side via Weibo Gaming and Gen.G. By the time the two clubs reached London they had already played a marathon series, setting up a rematch with the title on the line.

Game five came down to a scrappy late-game teamfight in the Baron pit. Keria baited a flank, Oner cleaned up the engagement, and T1 closed the Nexus to seal a fifth Summoner's Cup. Faker was named Finals MVP, becoming the only player in history to win the award twice. The off-screen story line was just as compelling: Riot tied a Worlds-themed in-game skin bundle to the prize pool, lifting total winnings well above the $2.225M base figure.
