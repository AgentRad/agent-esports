About the Tournament
Madrid Arena · March 14 to 24, 2024

Madrid was the first Masters event under Riot's revised 2024 calendar. Each of the four international leagues qualified two teams off Stage 1 of the regional season, and the eight got 11 days to settle who took the global title and the first qualification slot for Champions.

The bracket opened with a Swiss group on patch 8.04. Two-and-out advancement and two-and-out elimination cut the field from eight to four in three rounds, then the survivors crossed into a double-elimination playoff bracket played out at Madrid Arena. The arena seats roughly 12,000 and sold out the entire weekend.

Sentinels arrived as outsiders. The roster had finished sixth in Americas Stage 1 and only made the event because the league's next two seeds had stumbled. Once on the international stage they ran the table from the lower bracket and built the longest playoff run by any team in the bracket, reverse-sweeping Paper Rex in the lower final to set up the rematch with Gen.G.

Gen.G had been the cleanest team in the bracket all event. The Korean side dropped exactly one map across their first six matches and looked the obvious favorite walking into the final. Sentinels took map one on Bind, dropped two in a row on Breeze and Ascent, then won Split and Icebox to close out a five-map series that ran past midnight local time.

Sentinels had to win three on the bounce after dropping two on Breeze and Ascent. Split was the swing map: zekken dropped a 24/12 line as the duelist anchor on B and the score line never closed inside four rounds.

Icebox was the closer. TenZ produced the moment of the tournament with a 1v2 retake on B in round 19 that gave Sentinels match point. Two rounds later zekken planted, defended, and the building came down with the trophy lift.
