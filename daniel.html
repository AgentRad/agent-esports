Daniel

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Daniel Piecenski (born December 5, 2006), known online simply as Daniel, is an American professional Rocket League player currently signed to NRG. A high-mechanical-ceiling striker who has been a regular fixture on global power rankings since the back half of 2023, he is the reigning RLCS World Champion after winning the RLCS 2025 World Championship with NRG in September 2025.[1][2]

Piecenski began his salaried career with Spacestation Gaming in early 2022 and reached his first RLCS World Championship with the org. After a brief stint at Version1, he joined G2 Esports in December 2023 and won the RLCS 2024 Major 2: London on June 23, 2024 with the trio of Atomic, BeastMode and himself. He has accumulated approximately $680,120 in tracked tournament earnings, the highest career total of any North American Rocket League player in his cohort.[1]

Early life

Piecenski was born on December 5, 2006 in the United States. He came up through the North American open ladder as a young teenager and was identified as one of the most aggressive top-of-the-bracket free-agent prospects in the region by his fifteenth birthday, the youngest age band that historically translates to a tier-one signing.[1]

Competitive career

Spacestation Gaming (2022 to 2023)

Piecenski signed his first salaried contract with Spacestation Gaming on January 12, 2022, joining the all-NA roster as the youngest player on the books. The team qualified for the RLCS 2021-22 World Championship, the first of his Worlds appearances, and he stayed on the roster through to April 2023.[1]

Version1 (2023)

He moved to Version1 on April 8, 2023 in a mid-season switch, alongside other roster shuffles in the North American region. The eight-month stint produced regional placings but no major silverware, ending on December 4, 2023.

G2 Esports (2023 to 2024)

On December 7, 2023, Piecenski joined G2 Esports, where the team rebranded its Rocket League roster as G2 Stride for the RLCS 2024 season. The trio of Piecenski, Atomic and BeastMode won the RLCS 2024 Major 2: London on June 23, 2024, defeating Team Falcons 4-1 in the grand final at the Copper Box Arena. They followed up with a runner-up finish at the RLCS 2024 World Championship in Fort Worth in September, where they were defeated by Team BDS. The G2 division was released by the organisation on November 7, 2024.[1][2]

NRG (2024 to present)

Piecenski signed with NRG on December 20, 2024, anchoring the rebuild around himself, justin. and Atomic. The roster won the RLCS 2025 World Championship in London the following September, the first Worlds title for an NRG-branded RL roster since 2019, and they have continued to anchor the NA region into the RLCS 2026 calendar.[1]

Playstyle and analysis

Piecenski is a primary striker in the modern definition of the role. He plays first man on most defaults and operates at one of the highest aerial pressure rates in the game. The structural cost is that he absorbs the largest share of opposition reads, which is how his teams convert him into the highest opening-shot rate among non-defenders, but it also means his off-rounds are the most visible reason a series swings. Coaches who have worked with him describe his replay review as the most disciplined among teenage stars, and his comfort taking the third-man position on slow-rotation defaults is the structural separator that converted G2's London run into the 2025 NRG title. He plays primarily on a controller with an aggressive deadzone and is a vocal defender of the modern straight-flick meta in coaching content.
