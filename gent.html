Gent

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Rigoberto Padilla (born January 11, 1999), known online as Gent, is an American professional Apex Legends player and in-game leader currently signed to GodLike Esports. He is best known for his tenures as IGL for G2 Esports and TSM, and for finishing 7th at the 2026 ALGS Championship in Sapporo for $80,000.[1]

Padilla has competed continuously since 2020, with stints at AimAssist, SolaFide, G2, Tripods, Pioneers and TSM. As of May 2026 his tracked tournament earnings total $112,325.[2]

Early life and gaming background

Rigoberto Padilla was born on January 11, 1999, in the United States. He entered competitive Apex Legends in early 2020 during the explosion of the North American grassroots scene that followed the game's first ALGS season. The North American competitive ecosystem at the time was unusually accessible to new IGL profiles, since the role's early structural conventions were still being figured out across rosters, and Padilla used the volume of online qualifier play to refine his decision-making framework over the course of his rookie year.[1]

His earliest pro appearances came with rosters where he was already the de-facto shotcaller despite having less LAN experience than several teammates. The pattern of being recruited specifically for his macro voice rather than his individual rating has continued through every roster of his career, and it shaped both the structural fit and the public framing of his subsequent moves.

Competitive career

Grassroots beginnings (2020 to 2021)

Padilla joined AimAssist in January 2020 as one of his earliest competitive contracts and bounced through the early-NA grassroots circuit during the run-up to the first formal ALGS Pro League cycle. His IGL identity was largely built across this period, when the role itself was still being defined inside the broader Apex competitive vocabulary.

G2 Esports (2021 to 2022)

Padilla joined G2 Esports on June 1, 2021 and shotcalled the organisation's North American Apex roster through October 2022. The signing was one of the higher-profile structural moves at the IGL position during that calendar year. He placed fourth at the 2022 Year 2 Split 2 Pro League NA for a $10,500 share, the team's signature finish during his tenure.

Tripods and Pioneers (2022 to 2024)

Padilla played for Tripods across two tenures, in 2022 to 2024 and again in late 2024, with a brief stint for Pioneers in between in 2024. The Tripods project was widely respected as a coach-led, structurally tight roster, and Padilla's work as the team's voice during that window built his reputation as a steady IGL who could squeeze high placements out of underdog rosters that lacked the individual peak fragging of more star-driven trios.

TSM (2025 to 2026)

Padilla signed with TSM on April 28, 2025 alongside Dooplex and Panic, taking on the IGL spot for one of the most storied org names in competitive shooters. He led TSM to a seventh-place finish at the 2026 ALGS Championship in Sapporo for an $80,000 share, the largest single-event finish of his career. The roster also placed fourteenth at the 2025 Riyadh Midseason Playoffs for $22,000.[3]

TSM disbanded its Apex division on February 1, 2026, ending one of the highest-profile org runs in the title. Padilla briefly joined Team Burger before signing with GodLike Esports on April 29, 2026.

Recent moves (2026 to present)

The GodLike Esports project marked a return to a development-style IGL role for Padilla, with the organisation building around his shotcalling voice in a way that mirrored his earlier Tripods era. Through the early-2026 calendar, the roster has remained in the Pro League conversation as a regular qualifier finisher.

Playstyle and analysis

Padilla is regarded as one of the more analytical IGLs in North American Apex Legends. His shotcalling tends to be methodical rather than highlight-driven, with an emphasis on rotation timings, ring-edge zone control and conservative third-party windows in the late-game. Coaches who have worked with him point to his pre-match preparation as the most disciplined among the working IGL pool, and his rosters have consistently produced higher placement averages than their individual fragging numbers would predict. The trade-off in his style has been that his rosters' ceiling is often capped by their individual fragging output, since his structure is built around minimising risk rather than maximising the kill differential per match. He is most effective on rosters where the other two slots can supply consistent close-range wins without requiring his macro plan to compensate for poor opening fights.

Reputation and impact

Within NA Apex, Gent is regarded as one of the more analytical IGLs in the region, a profile that has kept him in steady demand across multiple ALGS calendar cycles. His 2026 Championship run with TSM was the swan song of one of the most storied org names in Apex Legends, and his association with the organisation's final competitive roster has become part of the broader narrative around the post-2025 ALGS franchise landscape.

His tracked tournament earnings of $112,325 as of May 2026 reflect a career built on consistency rather than headline finishes, but his structural impact on rosters has positioned him as a recurring figure in the conversation about NA's developing IGL pipeline.[2]
