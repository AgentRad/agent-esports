Danil Kryshkovets (born January 25, 2007), known online as donk, is a Russian professional Counter-Strike 2 player and entry fragger for Team Spirit. He was named HLTV's best player of 2024 at age 17 · the youngest player ever to win the award · and is widely regarded as one of the most explosive individual talents to enter Counter-Strike since s1mple and ZywOo.[1][2]

Kryshkovets joined Team Spirit's main roster in July 2023 from the organization's academy team and within twelve months had become the most-talked-about player in the world. Across 2024 he led Spirit to titles at IEM Katowice 2024, BLAST Premier Spring Final 2024 and BetBoom Dacha Dubai, and to a runner-up finish at the Perfect World Shanghai Major 2024. He has accumulated approximately $912,000 in tournament earnings, the highest figure ever for a player under the age of 20.[3]

Early life

Kryshkovets was born January 25, 2007 in Russia. He was identified as a top-tier prospect on Russian-language matchmaking and FACEIT services as a young teenager. He signed with Team Spirit's academy program in late 2021 at age 14, one of several promising youth signings the org made in that period as part of its broader infrastructure investment.[1]

Career

Spirit Academy (2021-2023)

Kryshkovets played for Spirit's academy roster from December 2021 to July 2023, putting up the highest individual rating in the WePlay Academy League across multiple seasons. By the spring of 2023 his promotion to the main team was already widely rumored.[2]

Team Spirit main roster (2023-present)

Spirit promoted Kryshkovets to the main roster on July 5, 2023, replacing degster. He was 16 years old at the time. The team finished 2023 with a championship at BetBoom Dacha Dubai · Kryshkovets's first big-event MVP · and was already widely tipped as a HLTV top-five candidate.[3]

2024 became one of the most decorated calendar years for any individual player in CS history. Spirit, with Kryshkovets centerpiecing the team's T-side, captured IEM Katowice 2024, BLAST Premier Spring Final 2024 and several smaller events, and reached the grand final of the Perfect World Shanghai Major 2024, finishing runner-up to MOUZ. Kryshkovets earned the MVP at the Major despite the loss · the first runner-up MVP since 2017.[3][4]

Best Player of 2024

HLTV named Kryshkovets the best player of 2024 in January 2025, citing the highest event rating average ever recorded for a calendar year by a player under 20. He finished runner-up for the 2025 award (won by ZywOo) and entered 2026 as the consensus top individual talent to watch.[5]

Playing style

Kryshkovets is best known for his pace and aggression as Spirit's primary entry. He typically opens rounds for the team but is also trusted with key clutch responsibilities, an unusual combination among entries. Coaches and analysts have repeatedly described his decision-making and reaction time as outliers · the latter being a frequent topic on community broadcasts.[5]

Legacy and impact

At 19, Kryshkovets has already accumulated 10 HLTV MVPs and is on pace to break records at the very top of the all-time MVP list if his current trajectory holds. His commercial appeal has driven Team Spirit's brand recognition outside of CIS markets and he is regularly cited as the face of the next generation of Counter-Strike, alongside G2's m0NESY and NaVi's b1t.[5]
