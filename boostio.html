Boostio

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Kelden Elric Pupello (born November 14, 2000), known online as Boostio, is an American professional Valorant player. He served as in-game leader for Evil Geniuses at VALORANT Champions 2023, lifting the trophy in Los Angeles on August 26, 2023, alongside teammate Demon1. He is widely regarded as one of the most accomplished initiator players and IGLs in North American Valorant.[1][2]

Pupello was named Sentinel of the Year at the 2023 VCT Americas League awards and led EG's Cinderella run from the bottom of the regular season standings to the largest payday in Valorant history. He played for 100 Thieves in 2024 and 2025 before becoming a free agent in October 2025.[3]

Early life

Pupello was born November 14, 2000, in the United States. He came up through the tier-three Valorant scene in 2020, posting standout numbers as an initiator and emerging IGL on rosters such as Triumph and Second Wind during the first year of competitive Valorant.[1]

Competitive career

Tier-three NA (2020-2021)

Pupello played for Triumph, Second Wind, Spacestation Gaming and OTW Esports through 2020 and 2021, building a reputation as a vocal in-game leader on initiator agents Sova and Skye. He signed with Evil Geniuses in November 2021 ahead of VCT NA Challengers 2022.

Evil Geniuses and Champions 2023

Pupello was named IGL of an EG roster widely projected to finish bottom of the inaugural VCT Americas League 2023 season. After a third-place playoff finish, EG qualified for Masters Tokyo, where they finished second to Fnatic, and then ran the table at VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles.

EG defeated Paper Rex 3-1 in the Grand Final on August 26, 2023, with Pupello calling from initiator agents Skye and KAY/O. The team's $1,000,000 winner share was the largest single payout in Valorant history at the time. Pupello received the 2023 VCT Americas Sentinel of the Year award later that year.[2][3]

100 Thieves (2024-2025)

Pupello signed a multi-year deal with 100 Thieves in November 2023 amid EG's public roster dispute that saw multiple Champions winners depart the franchise. He served as IGL for 100 Thieves through the 2024 and 2025 VCT seasons, reaching the playoffs at Masters Shanghai in 2024 and posting consistent middle-tier finishes in VCT Americas.

Free agency (2025-present)

Pupello and 100 Thieves parted ways in October 2025. As of the start of the 2026 VCT season, he is unsigned and has been linked with multiple VCT Americas franchises in the offseason.[4]

Legacy and impact

Pupello is the only North American IGL to win VCT Champions, and his Skye/KAY/O calls during EG's 2023 grand-final run are widely studied. As of May 2026, his $263,567 in tracked tournament earnings places him third on the all-time American Valorant earnings list, behind only Demon1 and TenZ.

Personal life

Pupello is from the Tampa Bay area of Florida. He has spoken publicly about his role as a vocal IGL and his approach to mid-round adjustments, and he streams regularly on Twitch under the Boostio handle.
