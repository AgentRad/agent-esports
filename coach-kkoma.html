About
The dean of LoL coaching

Kim Jeong-gyun, known universally as kkOma, is the longest-tenured top-tier head coach in League of Legends history. He stepped off the player pathway and into a SK Telecom support role in 2012, taking the lead chair the following year and immediately steering SKT T1 K to the 2013 World Championship. The roster he built around a 17-year-old mid laner named Faker became the template every Korean organization has tried to replicate since.

After back-to-back Worlds titles in 2015 and 2016 plus consecutive MSI crowns, kkOma briefly stepped away in 2019, taking head-coach roles first at China's Vici Gaming and then at DAMWON Gaming during the 2020-2022 stretch. He returned to T1 ahead of the 2023 season as a senior advisor and quickly slid back into the head coach seat. Under his second T1 tenure the org has reclaimed the Summoner's Cup in both 2024 and 2025 and added the inaugural Esports World Cup gold in Riyadh.

What separates kkOma is process discipline rather than draft fireworks. Players past and present describe a film room that looks more like a thesis defense than a pep talk, every wave timing and ward path is questioned in plain Korean, and decisions only ship to the stage once the room can defend them out loud. He delegates pick-ban prep to assistants but personally owns mid-game macro language, which is why so many of his teams snowball cleanly between minutes 14 and 22.
