About
Coaching career & philosophy

Brandon Ortega, who plays under the tag Raven, started in Apex Legends as a staff analyst with NRG in 2021, working on zone-pull modelling and end-game positional review. He moved to TSM in 2022 as the team's first dedicated full-time coach, replacing the player-led prep model the org had run since the days of Albralelie. The hire was widely seen at the time as a low-stakes experiment. By the end of his first calendar year it was the most-discussed staff move in NA Apex.

The headline run is the 2023 ALGS Championship in Birmingham. Going in, TSM had the highest cumulative match score across the year on paper but had stumbled on every previous LAN. Raven rebuilt the prep cadence around late-circuit stress drills · forcing trio drops onto edge zones in scrim, then forcing the same drops again with a ring-three already shrinking. ImperialHal, Verhulst, and Reps lifted the trophy in the most lopsided final-game performance the format had ever seen, and Reps publicly credited the coach on stage.

The Raven philosophy is end-game first, mid-game built backwards from there. He famously runs his scrim blocks in reverse order · the trio first plays the final ring, then ring three, then ring two, only landing on early-game prep at the end of the day. The logic is that mid-game decisions only have value if you already know which positions matter at the close, and players who train the close first stop giving up free splits at minute fifteen. It is a simple framework, but it requires more scrim discipline than most rosters can sustain · which is part of why TSM keep finishing on the podium year over year.
