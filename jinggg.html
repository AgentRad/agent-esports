Jinggg

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Wang Jing Jie (born July 29, 2003), known online as Jinggg, is a Singaporean professional Valorant player currently signed to Paper Rex as a duelist. He has been with Paper Rex since 2021, won VALORANT Masters Toronto 2025 alongside teammate f0rsakeN, and finished as runner-up at VALORANT Champions 2023.[1][2]

Jing is widely regarded as one of the most aggressive duelists in Valorant, posting some of the highest first-blood percentages in VCT Pacific over five seasons. He was named APAC Best Player by VALO2ASIA in both 2022 and 2023.[3]

Early life

Wang was born July 29, 2003, in Singapore. He started competing in Valorant in 2020 during the closed beta in Southeast Asia, drawing attention through high-elim ladder runs on Raze and Phoenix.[1]

Competitive career

Reality Rift and Team SMG (2020-2021)

Wang played for Reality Rift through 2020 and 2021 before a brief stint with Team SMG in mid-2021. He was scouted by Paper Rex ahead of VCT 2022 and signed in late 2021 as the team's primary duelist.

Paper Rex (2021-present)

Wang debuted with Paper Rex at VCT 2022 APAC Stage 1 Challengers and quickly established himself alongside f0rsakeN as the team's offensive engine. Paper Rex finished second at Masters Copenhagen 2022, third at VALORANT Champions 2022, and reached the Grand Final of VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles, losing 1-3 to Evil Geniuses.

Wang was a substitute for portions of the 2023 and 2024 VCT seasons due to Singapore's National Service obligations but returned to the active roster full-time for the 2025 season.

Masters Toronto 2025

Paper Rex won VCT Pacific Stage 1 2025 and VCT Pacific Stage 2 2025 with Wang as the duelist. The team then traveled to VCT Masters Toronto 2025 in May 2025 and defeated FNATIC 3-1 in the Grand Final, with Wang putting up a 1.27 average rating across the bracket.[2][3]

It was Paper Rex's first international VCT title and the first Masters trophy for VCT Pacific since 2022. The team came into VALORANT Champions 2025 as title favorites, finishing third after a semifinal loss.

Legacy and impact

Wang is widely regarded as the best Singaporean Valorant player of all time and one of the top three duelists in VCT Pacific history. As of May 2026, his $290,018 in tracked tournament earnings places him second on the all-time Pacific earnings list, behind only f0rsakeN.

Personal life

Wang lives in Singapore. He completed Singapore's mandatory National Service obligations across 2023 and 2024, briefly stepping down from the active Paper Rex roster for portions of those seasons. He streams under the handle jingggxd.
