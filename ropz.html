ropz

Robin Kool (born December 22, 1999), known online as ropz, is an Estonian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to Team Vitality. A primary lurker known for his unhurried pace and elite trade-killing instincts, he has been ranked HLTV top 10 in five different years and has reached five Major grand finals, the latter tied with a small group of all-time veterans.[1][2]

Kool spent four-and-a-half years with mousesports / MOUZ, where he was discovered through Faceit Hub matchmaking before being legally signed at age 17. He won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 with FaZe Clan in 2022 and reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final the following year. Since January 2025 he has been the third star alongside ZywOo on Vitality, the team that has won three of the last four Majors.[1][3]

Early life and gaming background

Robin Kool was born on December 22, 1999, in Tallinn, Estonia. He picked up Counter-Strike: Global Offensive in his early teens and built his game almost entirely through Faceit Pro League and online matchmaking from the age of fourteen, an unusual route for a player who would eventually be ranked among the top three in the world. The Estonian competitive infrastructure was effectively non-existent at the time, and there were no domestic LAN events for him to enter, so Kool grew up as a "Faceit player" in the most literal sense.[1]

His reputation outpaced his profile by a wide margin during 2015 and 2016. Older European professionals would routinely lobby for him in friendly pugs, and his demos were widely shared in coaching circles, but he could not legally compete at offline events until he turned seventeen. He has discussed in later interviews how those years shaped a calmly self-reliant playstyle, since most of his learning happened against unfamiliar opponents in solo queue rather than inside a structured roster.

Competitive career

mousesports / MOUZ (2017 to 2021)

Kool joined mousesports on April 12, 2017, only weeks after he had aged into LAN eligibility. The transition into a top-tier roster was unusually smooth. He posted top-twenty HLTV finishes in his first full season, helped the team to the StarSeries Season 4 trophy in 2018, and earned MVP honours at multiple tier-one events as the lurker on a roster that also featured karrigan, chrisJ, oskar and later frozen.[2]

His individual numbers held up across the next three seasons, with HLTV top-ten finishes in 2019 and 2020. He stayed through the organisation's rebrand to MOUZ in 2021, but the roster around him cycled rapidly during that period, and the long-term ceiling of the project shrank as the international project at FaZe began to stabilise. By the end of 2021 he had become the most sought-after free agent in European Counter-Strike.

FaZe Clan (2022 to 2024)

On January 3, 2022, Kool signed with FaZe Clan, completing the karrigan, rain, Twistzz, broky and ropz roster that defined the international era. The fit was almost immediately complete. The team won IEM Katowice 2022 in February, lifted the BLAST Premier Spring Final the same season, and capped the year by winning the PGL Major Antwerp 2022. The lineup added a second Intel Grand Slam season later in the run.[3]

Kool reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final the following year and finished HLTV #3 for 2023, his career-best ranking at that point. He remained one of the most consistent fraggers in the world through 2024 before parting ways with FaZe at the end of that year, in a move widely framed as the end of one international era and the start of another.

Team Vitality (2025 to present)

On January 10, 2025, Kool signed with Team Vitality, the French organisation built around ZywOo, apEX, flameZ, and mezii. He arrived as the third star alongside ZywOo and apEX, and the move quickly became one of the most successful free-agent signings of the CS2 era. Vitality's 2025 was historically dominant: they won the BLAST.tv Austin Major 2025 over The MongolZ and the StarLadder Budapest Major 2025 over FaZe in the same calendar year, becoming the first roster to win two CS2 Majors back-to-back.[4]

Kool finished HLTV #3 again in 2025, equalling his 2023 mark and earning his second top-three HLTV ranking with two different organisations. Within the Vitality system he was deployed as the disciplined lurker, with ZywOo's AWP role taking the headline minutes and apEX shaping defaults around the structure he and Kool had agreed in pre-game review.

Recent form (2026)

The opening months of 2026 saw Vitality continue as the world's number one ranked roster, with Kool collecting another tournament MVP medal at BLAST Open Spring 2026. The team's defining series of the early season came against FaZe Clan, where Kool's lurk patterns on Mirage and Inferno produced two of the most-discussed individual performances of his career.

Playstyle and analysis

Kool is the platonic modern lurker. His role on every roster he has played for has been to operate behind the main pack, applying selective pressure to the rotational map of the opposing CT side and converting late-round duels into trade kills that swing the round in his team's favour. His unhurried pace is the defining quality of his film: he is willing to spend the entire economy on a single round if the read on the map demands it, and his discipline on the trigger lets him take fights at the precise moment the trade is favourable. Coaches frequently cite his economy management as the best of any non-IGL on the European circuit, and his ability to read what an opposing team will do with information is what separates him from peers who can match his raw aim. The tradeoff, well documented across his FaZe and Vitality runs, is that his impact is hidden in series where his teammates fail to convert opening duels: when a team is forced to play from behind, his patience cannot save the round on its own.

Notable moments

  • Won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 with FaZe Clan, defeating Natus Vincere in the grand final.[3]
  • Reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final with FaZe and finished HLTV #3 for the year.[3]
  • Won the BLAST.tv Austin Major 2025 with Team Vitality, his second Major title.[4]
  • Won the StarLadder Budapest Major 2025 with Vitality, completing the first back-to-back Major sweep of the CS2 era.[4]
  • Tied the all-time record with five Major grand-final appearances, joining a small group of veteran legends.[2]
  • Finished HLTV top-three in 2023 and 2025, with two different organisations.[2]

Legacy and awards

Kool's five Major grand-final appearances tie him with GeT_RiGhT, f0rest, friberg, Xizt, dupreeh, apEX and karrigan for the most all-time, and his six tournament MVPs include four at Big Events. He is widely regarded as the most complete lurker ever to play the game, and his career trajectory from Faceit anonymity to two-time top-three HLTV finisher is one of the most-cited modern path-to-pro examples in coaching content.[2][3]

Among his Estonian compatriots, he remains the country's most successful esports professional in any title by a wide margin, and he has used his platform to advocate for English-language streaming as a developmental tool for players from countries without domestic LAN scenes. He is regarded as one of the more thoughtful media performers among active stars, and his post-match interviews are routinely cited by analysts for their candour about preparation and economy decisions.

Personal life

Kool resides in Tallinn and bootcamps in Berlin or Paris during Vitality's competitive cycles. He has been a vocal advocate for English-language streaming as a developmental tool for younger players from non-traditional regions, and he often discusses his Faceit-only path to professional play in interviews and in coach-led VOD review sessions.[1]
