Robin Kool (born December 22, 1999), known online as ropz, is an Estonian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to Team Vitality. A primary lurker known for his unhurried pace and elite trade-killing instincts, he has been ranked HLTV top 10 in five different years and has reached five Major grand finals, the latter tied with a small group of all-time veterans.[1][2]

Kool spent four-and-a-half years with mousesports / MOUZ, where he was discovered through Faceit Hub matchmaking before being legally signed at age 17. He won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 with FaZe Clan in 2022 and reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final the following year. Since January 2025 he has been the third star alongside ZywOo on Vitality, the team that has won three of the last four Majors.[1][3]

Early life

Kool was born December 22, 1999 in Tallinn, Estonia. He developed his Counter-Strike: Global Offensive game almost entirely through Faceit Pro League and matchmaking from age 14, building a reputation as a lurker with elite raw aim despite never attending offline qualifiers.[1]

Career

mousesports / MOUZ (2017 to 2021)

Kool joined mousesports on April 12, 2017 once he became old enough to compete at LAN events. Across his tenure he played alongside karrigan, chrisJ, oskar and frozen, posting top-ten HLTV finishes in 2019 and 2020. He stayed through the team's rebrand to MOUZ in 2021.[2]

FaZe Clan (2022 to 2024)

On January 3, 2022, Kool signed with FaZe Clan, completing the karrigan/rain/Twistzz/broky/ropz roster. They won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 together and lifted IEM Katowice 2022 and BLAST Premier Spring Final 2022. He reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final and finished HLTV #3 in 2023. He left FaZe at the end of 2024.[3]

Team Vitality (2025 to present)

On January 10, 2025, Kool signed with Team Vitality, the French organization built around ZywOo, apEX, flameZ and mezii. The roster has been historically dominant in 2025 to 2026, winning the BLAST.tv Austin Major 2025 over The MongolZ and the StarLadder Budapest Major 2025 over FaZe. Kool finished HLTV #3 again in 2025.[4]

Legacy and awards

Kool's five Major grand-final appearances tie him with GeT_RiGhT, f0rest, friberg, Xizt, dupreeh, apEX, and karrigan for the most all-time. His six tournament MVPs include four at Big Events. He is widely regarded as one of the best lurkers ever to play the game, and his career trajectory from Faceit anonymity to two-time top-three HLTV finisher is a frequently cited modern path-to-pro example.[2][3]

Personal life

Kool resides in Tallinn and Berlin during competitive cycles. Among his Estonian compatriots, he remains the country's most successful esports professional in any title and has been a vocal advocate for English-language streaming as a developmental tool.[1]
