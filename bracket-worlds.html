How the Bracket Played
Eight teams, single elimination, three weekends across two cities

T1 opened the knockout stage in the cleanest possible fashion. Faker, Zeus and Keria swept Top Esports 3-0 in the QF, then took down LCK rival Gen.G 3-1 in the semi behind a now-iconic Keria flank flip on game four. The defending champions arrived at The O2 with the most efficient bracket run of any team.

Bilibili Gaming took the long road. After surviving a 3-2 thriller against LNG in the QF, BLG cleaned up Weibo Gaming 3-1 to set up the all-Chinese semi result that did not stop them from making the final. Bin's Camille and knight's Aurelion Sol carried games in the semi, leaving BLG one win short of breaking the LCK's grip.

The final swung four times. T1 took game one behind a Faker Galio masterclass, BLG snatched the next two on Bin's Camille and a knight clutch, then T1 reeled off games four and five to lift the cup. Final score 3-2, fifth Worlds title for the franchise, second straight for this core.
