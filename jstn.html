★ RLCS Season 8 World Champion · 8x S-Tier winner · NA earnings leader of his era

jstn

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Justin Morales (born August 5, 2002), better known online as jstn., is an American professional Rocket League player currently signed to FUT Esports. A right-handed playmaker with one of the most distinct passing reads in North American Rocket League, he has competed at every RLCS World Championship from Season 6 onward and is widely regarded as the most decorated active North American player of the post-Season-X era.[1]

Morales spent six full seasons with NRG Esports between 2018 and 2023, the longest tenure of any player at a single organisation in his region during that span. He is a one-time World Champion, having lifted the trophy at the RLCS Season 8 World Championship in Madrid in 2019, and has accumulated approximately $540,976 in tracked tournament earnings over his career.[1][2]

Early life and entry to competition

Morales was born on August 5, 2002. He came up through the North American ladder as a teenager during the early popularity boom of the game, and was identified by amateur tournament organisers as a top free-agent prospect well before he was old enough to compete in the salaried tier of the RLCS. His handle is a contraction of his given name and the trailing period was added during his early online play, where it has remained as a stylistic signature.[1]

Competitive career

Out of Style (2017 to 2018)

Morales' first salaried roster was Out of Style, the amateur stack that he joined in 2017 alongside future stars including GarrettG. The team progressed through the ranks of the open ladder and earned RLCS qualification, where their performance attracted the attention of NRG Esports, who signed the entire trio in 2018.[1]

NRG Esports (2018 to 2023)

Joining NRG was the breakthrough moment of his career. The trio of Morales, GarrettG and Turbopolsa became the dominant North American roster of the late RLCS Season 6, 7 and 8 era, winning both the Season 6 and Season 7 NA Regionals before lifting the Season 8 World Championship in Madrid in mid-2019 with a 4-2 grand-final win over Vitality. The performance was a structural turning point for the North American region.[2]

The roster underwent multiple rebuilds across Season X and the Epic-published RLCS 2021-22 through 2022-23 seasons, with Morales the only constant. He was on the roster that won three of the four NA Spring/Winter Majors of Season X and remained inside the global top 10 by individual rating across most of his NRG tenure.

Shopify Rebellion (2023 to 2025)

Morales joined Shopify Rebellion after his NRG departure in August 2023. The new Canadian-American organisation built its Rocket League roster around him as the centrepiece signing, and the team posted multiple top-eight finishes at North American Regionals and an appearance at the RLCS 2024 World Championship in Fort Worth. The fit was less title-driven than his NRG era but reaffirmed his durability as a tier-one anchor late into the post-Epic format.[1]

Gen.G Mobil1 Racing (2025 to 2026)

He moved to Gen.G Mobil1 Racing for the RLCS 2025 season, joining a roster designed to push the team into the World Championship picture. Gen.G qualified for both the Birmingham Major and the Worlds in London, where they finished outside the top eight, before the org restructured its esports portfolio in early 2026.

FUT Esports (2026 to present)

On March 6, 2026, jstn signed with FUT Esports, a Turkish-American organisation expanding its Rocket League footprint. The roster targets a return to the closed regional in 2026 and a Worlds appearance in RLCS 2026.[1]

Playstyle and analysis

Morales is best understood as a connector. He is rarely the highest-impact player in a series on his own raw mechanical ceiling, but his ability to read teammate position and to deliver passes into shooting windows has made him the spine of every roster he has anchored. His long NRG tenure produced one of the cleanest first-touch profiles in North America, and analysts who break down NRG's Season X tape will single out his offensive defaulting as the through-line that separated the team from peer rosters who relied more heavily on individual carry. He has also been one of the most reliable kickoff cheat readers of his generation, particularly out of the diagonal kickoff which has historically rewarded riskier first-touch reads. In ranked queue he plays primarily on a controller with a compressed deadzone, and he has been an open advocate for default keybinds in the Epic-published era of the game.

Legacy

jstn is the third-highest-earning American Rocket League player by tracked prize money behind the long-tenured GarrettG and the streamer-pivoted Turbopolsa, and his Season 8 trophy remains the only Worlds title held by an American-passport player from the Madrid era. He maintains a steady presence on Twitch, primarily as a casual ranked broadcaster on practice days, and has been candid in interviews about the personal toll of the long tenure approach to professional play.[1]
