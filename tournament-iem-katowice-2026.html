About the Tournament
Spodek Arena · Katowice, Poland

Few stops on the calendar carry the weight of Katowice. ESL has run a flagship event inside the saucer-shaped Spodek every winter since 2014, and the tournament has hosted six straight Counter-Strike Majors or equivalents. The 2026 edition opens the season with a $1,000,000 prize pool, 24 invited and qualified squads, and a single-week playoff push to a Sunday Bo5 final.

The eight directly seeded teams skipped the play-in entirely. Team Vitality arrived as the defending Katowice champion after their 3-1 dismantling of Spirit a year ago, and they brought the same core that closed 2025 ranked first in the world. Team Spirit, NaVi, FaZe Clan, MOUZ, G2, Astralis and Polish home favourites BIG rounded out the top eight, with HEROIC and Team Liquid entering through the closed qualifier.

Group stage played out at Studio EXPO across four days. ZywOo was the breakout performer, posting a 1.34 rating across nine maps as Vitality cruised through Group A undefeated. Spirit's donk delivered a 1.41 rating against weaker opposition before stumbling to MOUZ, sending the Russian-speaking side into the lower bracket. The two boldest results of the round were FaZe being mathematically eliminated by 3DMAX in the play-in and Liquid pulling off a 2-1 Bo3 sweep over G2 to advance.

Playoffs began on the Spodek main stage Friday morning. The defending champions face MOUZ in their first match, while Spirit drew Liquid in what looks like the most lopsided opening fixture of the round. Quarterfinals run through the evening session, semis on Saturday, and the Bo5 grand final closes the event Sunday in front of a sold-out arena. Live coverage and reactions are running on AgentTV's hub.
