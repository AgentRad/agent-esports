Scump

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Seth Abner (born June 30, 1995), known online as Scump, is a retired American professional Call of Duty player and full-time content creator currently signed to OpTic Gaming as a streamer. Across a 12-year competitive career he became the most recognisable face in the discipline, captured the 2017 CWL Championship with OpTic, and accumulated approximately $1,135,448 in tracked tournament earnings.[1]

Abner played for OpTic in some form across nearly the entirety of his career, including the original OpTic Gaming roster, the franchise-era Chicago Huntsmen, OpTic Chicago and finally OpTic Texas. He retired from active competition on January 17, 2023 and now operates as a streamer and on-camera personality for the organisation, where his audience remains the single largest individual draw in the Call of Duty broadcast ecosystem.[1][2]

Early life

Seth Abner was born on June 30, 1995 in the United States. He came up through the Major League Gaming amateur ladder during the early Modern Warfare 2 and Black Ops eras, when MLG events were the primary route from public matchmaking into a paid roster slot. By the back end of 2010 he had already drawn local attention as one of the cleanest individual gunfighters in the U.S. amateur pool.[1]

Competitive career

Obey, LeveraGe and ApeX (2011 to 2014)

Abner signed his first professional contract with Team Obey on January 1, 2011 at the age of fifteen, then moved to LeveraGe in February of the same year and rebranded with the team to Quantic LeveraGe before the year was out. He joined OpTic Gaming for the first time on December 1, 2011, transferred briefly to ApeX eSports in March 2012, and returned to OpTic full-time in May 2014. The four-roster shuffle through his late teen years was characteristic of the pre-franchise CoD ecosystem, where small structural decisions at minor LANs decided which house a top amateur landed in.[1]

OpTic Gaming (2014 to 2019)

The 2014 to 2019 OpTic Gaming era was the period that defined the modern Call of Duty audience. Abner played alongside Crimsix, FormaL, Karma and others in lineups that won multiple X Games gold medals and established the Greenwall as the genre's most-followed brand. The crown jewel was the Call of Duty World League Championship 2017, played on Infinite Warfare, which Scump captured with OpTic in front of a sold-out venue and a stream peak that remains a benchmark for CoD broadcast.[1][3]

The MLG-and-CWL years also produced a consistent set of S-Tier finishes and individual MVP nominations across Black Ops III, Infinite Warfare, WWII and Black Ops 4. Scump's pure aim and his 1v1 reputation kept him at or near the top of every published statistical leaderboard between 2015 and 2019.

Chicago Huntsmen and OpTic Chicago (2019 to 2021)

The arrival of the franchised Call of Duty League in 2020 reorganised the global ecosystem around twelve city-based teams. Abner stayed with the Greenwall through the brand's transition into Chicago Huntsmen on October 24, 2019, then to OpTic Chicago on November 11, 2020. The Chicago era produced the franchise's first CDL Major win in 2020, although the team's regular-season form fluctuated in line with the wider CoD title cycle and the team did not capture the 2020 Championship.

OpTic Texas and retirement (2021 to 2023)

OpTic Chicago was retired and rebranded as OpTic Texas on November 8, 2021 in an organisational realignment built around founder Hector "H3CZ" Rodriguez. Abner remained on the roster for the inaugural OpTic Texas season but stepped away from active competition on January 17, 2023, citing the cumulative cost of a twelve-year LAN schedule.[2]

His retirement was confirmed on stream and broadly covered across the discipline. He immediately transitioned into a streamer-and-personality role inside the OpTic Gaming organisation, where his Sunday co-streams and behind-the-scenes content have continued to drive the league's largest individual audience.

Playstyle and analysis

Scump was the modern template for the SMG slayer in Call of Duty, anchored by an unusually controlled crosshair on the franchise's mid-recoil weapons and a precise, repetitive bounce-off-corners movement loop. His aggression was always disciplined: he picked the same routes through the same rooms with measurable timing, which gave teammates predictable rotations to play around. The structural cost of the role was familiar across his career, where stretches when his entry timing slipped were also stretches when whichever roster he was anchoring fell out of the title hunt. He was also one of the rare slayers whose game travelled cleanly across the SMG-to-AR adjustment of the 2020 franchise reset, an asset that kept the OpTic core competitive into his final season.

Legacy and influence

Abner's broader cultural footprint in Call of Duty is unrivalled. He is the only player in the discipline whose content business stayed at scale through every title cycle from Modern Warfare 2 (2009) to Modern Warfare III (2023), and the only one whose individual stream audience consistently rivalled the league broadcast itself. His rivalry with Crimsix through the OpTic-versus-Faze era and his place inside the Greenwall pyramid of Hector "H3CZ" Rodriguez, FormaL, Karma and Crimsix are core to the franchise's mythology.[3]

He was elected to the inaugural Call of Duty Hall of Fame class associated with the league, and remains a vocal advocate for grassroots Call of Duty events and the Challengers ecosystem.

Streaming and content

Abner streams full-time on Twitch and produces a steady cadence of long-form Sunday vlogs, ranked grinds and CDL co-streams. His YouTube channel remains one of the highest-subscribed Call of Duty channels and his Twitter (now X) account at @scump is a default reference point for franchise news. He has been a recurring on-camera member of OpTic's broadcast and creator stable since 2023.
