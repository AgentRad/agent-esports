TenZ

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Tyson Van Ngo (born May 5, 2001), known online as TenZ, is a Canadian streamer and former professional Valorant player currently signed to Sentinels in a content creator role. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential players in the early history of competitive Valorant, headlining Sentinels' victory at the inaugural VCT Masters Reykjavik in 2021 and adding a second Masters title at Masters Madrid in 2024.[1][2]

Ngo retired from active competitive play in September 2024, transitioning to a full-time content creator role with Sentinels. He has since remained one of the most-watched Valorant streamers globally, with more than 4 million Twitch followers and a combined social audience exceeding 8 million.[3]

Early life

Ngo was born May 5, 2001 in British Columbia, Canada, to a Vietnamese-Canadian family. He grew up in the Greater Vancouver area and began playing first-person shooters competitively in his early teens, focusing on Counter-Strike: Global Offensive before transitioning to Valorant in the game's closed beta in April 2020.[1]

Competitive career

CS:GO origins (2018-2020)

Ngo competed in CS:GO at a tier-three level under the same handle, briefly playing for Bad News Bears and signing with Cloud9 in late 2019 ahead of a planned roster build. The CS:GO project never fully materialized, and Ngo pivoted to Valorant in early 2020 once Riot's closed beta opened in North America.

Cloud9 Blue (2020-2021)

Ngo joined Cloud9 Blue in May 2020 alongside Skadoodle, mitch and Tenny. The roster placed top-2 at multiple early NA Ignition Series events but failed to win a major. In March 2021, Ngo was loaned to Sentinels as a temporary fifth ahead of VCT NA Stage 1 Masters.

Sentinels and Reykjavik (2021)

Sentinels won VCT NA Stage 1 Masters in March 2021 with Ngo as their primary duelist. The loan was made permanent in April 2021. Sentinels then won VCT NA Stage 2 Challengers Finals and traveled to Iceland for the first international event, VCT Masters Reykjavik 2021.

The Reykjavik run is widely considered one of the dominant performances in Valorant history. Sentinels swept Fnatic 3-0 in the Grand Final on May 30, 2021, and Ngo was named Day 7 MVP for his Jett play across the bracket. The team's $200,000 prize check kicked off a fourteen-month run in which Sentinels were the most-followed esports brand in North America.[2]

The Madrid run (2024)

After a three-year drought of international titles, Sentinels rebuilt around Ngo, Zekken and a returning johnqt for VCT 2024. The team won VCT 2024 Americas Kickoff in February 2024, with Ngo named Finals MVP, and went on to win Masters Madrid in March 2024 with a 3-1 grand-final victory over Gen.G.[3]

Sentinels finished fourth at VALORANT Champions 2024 in Seoul that August, falling to EDward Gaming in the lower bracket.

Content creator era (2024-present)

On September 14, 2024, Sentinels announced that Ngo would step down from the active competitive roster and transition to a full-time content creator role with the organization. Ngo cited burnout, the demands of a year-round VCT calendar, and his interest in non-competitive content as reasons for the move.[4]

He has remained on the Sentinels analyst desk for selected events including the 2025 Masters and 2026 VCT Americas Kickoff broadcast, and he has co-streamed Masters Toronto in May 2026.

Legacy and impact

Ngo is the only North American player to win two VCT Masters trophies. His Reykjavik run in 2021 is broadly credited with setting the template for the duelist role in the early game and for establishing Valorant as a viable global esport. As of May 2026, his combined social media reach across Twitch, YouTube, Instagram and TikTok exceeds twelve million followers.

Personal life

Ngo lives in Los Angeles, California, where Sentinels is headquartered. He has been in a long-term relationship with content creator Kyedae Shymko, who was also signed to Sentinels and publicly disclosed a Hodgkin lymphoma diagnosis in 2023. Ngo has spoken publicly about his role as her caregiver during her treatment.
