Organization Overview
German institution · longest-running CS org

MOUZ (formerly known as mousesports) is a German esports organization headquartered in Hamburg. Founded March 24, 2002, MOUZ is the longest continuously operating tier-one Counter-Strike organization in the world, predating ESL and the formal CGS by several years.

MOUZ is best known in the modern era as the development home of ropz, who joined the org's Faceit pickup project in 2017 and went on to top-three HLTV finishes. Other notable alumni include NiKo, karrigan, frozen, and oskar. MOUZ won the StarLadder Berlin Major 2019 playoffs as a top-four team but have not yet won a Major.

The current roster is built around a Hungarian and Israeli core with Lithuanian standout jL on loan from NaVi, and remains one of the most reliable top-five HLTV teams in the CS2 era.
