Mero

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Matthew Faitel (born September 18, 2004), known online as Mero, is a Canadian professional Fortnite Battle Royale player currently competing for Team Liquid. He is a two-time FNCS Global Champion, having won the 2023 FNCS Global Championship with Cooper and the 2025 FNCS Global Championship with European partner Andilex. With approximately $1.11M in lifetime tournament earnings, he is among the highest-earning active North American competitors.[1]

Faitel previously played for Sentinels alongside Bugha and Reet in the early-2020s NA East trio scene before transitioning to NA-based duos and, in 2025, the cross-region pairing with Andilex that defined the new FNCS format. He joined Team Liquid in mid-2025 after a brief stint with Xen.

Early life

Faitel was born September 18, 2004 in Canada. He began competing in Fortnite during the Chapter 2 era, building reputation through Cash Cup placements before earning his first FNCS qualification.

Competitive career

Sentinels era (2020-2022)

Mero signed with Sentinels in the early Chapter 2 era, where he played in trios alongside Bugha and Reet. The trio took 1st at the C2S8 FNCS Grand Finals (2021, $45,000 each) and 1st at the C3S1 FNCS Grand Finals (2022, $65,000 each), establishing Mero as a primary IGL.[1]

2023 Global Championship with Cooper

In 2023, after the FNCS pivoted to a duos-only format, Mero paired with then-16-year-old American Cooper. The duo won the FNCS 2023 Global Championship in Copenhagen, taking home $500,000 each (over $1 million total) · the largest single payout for a Fortnite duo to that point.[2]

Team Liquid and the 2025 Global Championship

Mero joined Team Liquid in mid-2025 after parting ways with Xen. Paired with French veteran Andilex in a cross-region duo, the pair won the 2025 FNCS Global Championship · making Mero the first player to win two FNCS Global Championships with different partners.

Legacy

With back-to-back GC wins in 2023 and 2025 alongside two different partners, Mero is widely regarded as one of the most adaptable IGLs in competitive Fortnite. He routinely appears on top-3 lists alongside Peterbot, Khanada, and TaySon.
