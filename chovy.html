Chovy

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Jeong Ji-hoon (Korean: 정지훈; born March 3, 2001), better known as Chovy, is a South Korean professional League of Legends player who plays mid lane for Gen.G Esports in the LCK. Widely regarded as the most consistent regular-season player of his era, Chovy is a multi-time LCK Champion, a back-to-back Mid-Season Invitational Champion in 2024 and 2025, and the recipient of multiple LCK MVP awards.[1][2]

Chovy is known for his exceptional laning, mechanical precision, and his statistical dominance in regular-season play. Despite a long list of domestic and international trophies, a World Championship title remains the most discussed missing piece of his career as of May 2026.[2]

Early life

Jeong Ji-hoon was born on March 3, 2001 in South Korea. He began playing League of Legends competitively in the Korean amateur scene before joining the Griffin Challenger roster in 2017 at age 16. He impressed scouts in the Korean amateur circuit with extremely high laning numbers and was promoted to Griffin's main LCK roster ahead of the 2018 Summer split.[1]

Competitive career

Griffin debut (2018–2019)

Chovy debuted with Griffin during the 2018 LCK Summer split as the team's mid laner. Griffin reached three consecutive LCK finals during 2018 and 2019 but lost all three to SKT T1 and Kingzone DragonX. Chovy was named to multiple LCK All-Pro teams during the period and was widely viewed as the most promising young mid laner in the world.[2]

Griffin qualified for the 2019 World Championship, where the team was eliminated in the quarterfinals by Invictus Gaming. Following internal team turmoil at Griffin, Chovy departed at the end of 2019.

DRX, DK and Hanwha Life (2020–2021)

Chovy joined DragonX (DRX) for the 2020 season, qualifying for Worlds 2020 where the team was eliminated in the quarterfinals by Damwon Gaming. He moved to Hanwha Life Esports for the 2021 season, missing Worlds for the first time, before signing with Gen.G ahead of the 2022 LCK season.[1][2]

Gen.G era (2022–present)

Joining Gen.G alongside Ruler, Peanut, Doran and Lehends, Chovy won his first LCK title in 2022 Spring and went on to anchor a Gen.G dynasty that has won multiple consecutive LCK splits through 2025. The team won the 2024 Mid-Season Invitational in Chengdu, defeating Bilibili Gaming 3-1 in the final, and successfully defended the title at MSI 2025 in Vancouver, defeating T1 3-2.[2][3]

At the World Championship, Chovy and Gen.G have repeatedly fallen short of the final. The team was eliminated in the semifinals at Worlds 2025 by KT Rolster, with Chovy named LCK Regular Season MVP for that year and the 2025 MSI Finals MVP.[3]

Legacy and impact

Chovy is widely regarded as the most statistically dominant LCK regular-season mid laner since Faker. He holds the highest career CSPM among LCK mid laners with at least 200 games played and is one of only a handful of mid laners to win back-to-back MSI titles. As of May 2026 he is generally considered the second-best active mid laner in the world.[2]

Personal life

Chovy maintains a relatively private lifestyle and is based in Seoul. He is known to stream solo queue irregularly on Twitch and is active on X. His family lives in South Korea and has been featured occasionally in Gen.G content during championship celebrations.[1]
