★ 2023 ALGS Champion · longtime TSM anchor

Reps

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Jordan Wolfe (born April 23, 1995), known online as Reps, is an American former professional Apex Legends player and one of the most recognizable anchors in the history of competitive Apex. He spent nearly six years on TSM from June 2019 to March 2025 and won the 2023 ALGS Championship alongside ImperialHal and Verhulst, taking home a $600,000 first-place share.[1][2]

With approximately $684,913 in tracked tournament earnings, Wolfe is the second-highest-earning Apex Legends player of all time. He has been a free agent since departing TSM in March 2025 and currently maintains an active streaming presence rather than a competitive contract.[3]

Early life

Wolfe was born April 23, 1995 in the United States. He is one of the older members of the top tier of Apex Legends competition and brought a background in older shooter titles to the early Apex scene.[1]

Competitive career

LFOffers and early Apex (2019)

Wolfe's first competitive Apex stint was with the team LFOffers from February to June 2019. The trio earned strong online results and was acquired by TSM on June 26, 2019, alongside ImperialHal.

TSM era (2019-2025)

The original TSM Apex roster, with Wolfe as the trio's anchor, won the X Games Minneapolis 2019 Apex Pro-Am ($30,000) and the Apex Legends Preseason Invitational 2019 in Krakow ($105,000). Wolfe remained on TSM through every roster iteration of the original lineup, becoming the longest-tenured member of the org's Apex program.[2]

From 2022 onward, the TSM trio of ImperialHal, Verhulst and Wolfe became the dominant force in NA, winning two ALGS Playoffs titles before their crowning achievement at the 2023 Championship. Wolfe also placed 2nd at the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs ($160,000) and 4th at the 2024 ALGS Year 4 Split 2 Playoffs ($85,000).[3]

2023 ALGS Championship

The 2023 ALGS Championship at the Birmingham BEC in July 2023 ended a multi-year wait for an ALGS Championship trophy for Wolfe and TSM. As the trio's anchor, his role was to hold late-game zones and trade for ImperialHal in third-party scenarios. The win was widely covered as a career-defining moment for the entire roster.[2][4]

Free agency (2025-present)

Wolfe departed TSM on March 6, 2025, marking the end of the historic core. He has remained without a competitive team since, declining multiple reported offers in favor of part-time streaming and content. As of May 2026, he has been linked in reporting to potential analyst and coaching roles, but has not signed a competitive contract.[5]

Legacy and impact

Wolfe is regarded as one of the defining anchor players of the early ALGS era. His longevity on a single roster, his role in the 2023 Championship win, and his consistent late-game decision-making influenced a generation of Apex anchors. As of May 2026, his $684,913 in tournament earnings places him second on the all-time Apex Legends earnings chart behind ImperialHal.

Personal life

Wolfe is based in the United States and has spoken publicly about the strain of long-term competitive play. He has remained close with his former TSM teammates and is a regular co-streamer of major ALGS events.
