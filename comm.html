Comm

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Robert Kyser (born October 1, 2003), better known as Comm, is an American professional Rocket League player currently between organisations. A defensive specialist with one of the longest single-org tenures of his generation, he played for Version1 from November 2020 through August 2023, the bulk of the post-Psyonix transition into the Epic-published era.[1]

Comm is a multi-time Worlds qualifier and a top-eight finisher at the RLCS 2021-22 World Championship and the RLCS 2022-23 Fall Major. He has not yet won a Major, but he has accumulated approximately $334,838 in tracked tournament earnings across his career, making him one of the most consistent middle-of-the-stack North American competitors of his cohort.[1]

Early career

Kyser was born on October 1, 2003 and entered the North American competitive scene as a teenager during the RLCS Season 8 era. He cycled through a series of amateur stacks (Bull Send, Monkey Business, Adept) over the back half of 2019 and most of 2020 before his breakthrough signing.[1]

Competitive career

Version1 (2020 to 2023)

On November 4, 2020, Kyser signed with Version1, the Minnesota Vikings' esports brand. He spent nearly three full seasons with the organisation, the longest single-org tenure of his career, and the team became one of the more consistent regional sides in North America. He delivered a top-eight finish at the RLCS 2021-22 World Championship in Dallas and a top-eight at the RLCS 2022-23 Fall Major in Rotterdam, the highest individual placings of his career.[1]

OG (2024)

He moved to OG on January 18, 2024 in a structural rebuild around a younger NA roster. The OG year produced regional qualification but did not match the platform of the Version1 stretch, and the deal ended on October 2, 2024.

Free-agent stretch (2024 to 2026)

From late 2024 onwards Kyser cycled through a series of short-term rosters (Tech, CARL, Top Leh, and finally Apple Slices), the typical pattern of a North American veteran navigating a shrinking salaried-tier free-agent market. The Apple Slices run ended on March 12, 2026, leaving him on the open market again ahead of the Paris Major qualification cycle.[1]

Playstyle and analysis

Comm is a back-post specialist. He plays the third-man role about as conservatively as anyone in modern North American Rocket League, and the durability of his Version1 era is largely down to his ability to neutralise opposition aerial entries before they become shot windows. The structural cost of the role is that it is the hardest position to translate into headline numbers, and his individual rating profile understates the per-game impact more than peers' do. He also handles team boost economy with unusual precision; analysts who break down Version1 demos will single out his wide-pad routing as the foundation of the team's defensive shape. He prefers a controller with a low deadzone and has been a vocal advocate for back-post coverage in the early-game review content posted to his community channels.
