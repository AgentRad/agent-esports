Mongraal

From AGENT, the serious source for esports.

Kyle Jackson (born August 13, 2004), known online as Mongraal, is a British professional Fortnite player and Twitch streamer. He competed for FaZe Clan from July 7, 2019 onward, becoming one of the most recognizable competitive Fortnite players globally. With 7.2 million Twitch followers and 5.33 million YouTube subscribers, he is among the most-followed Fortnite content creators of all time.[1][2]

Jackson qualified for the 2019 Fortnite World Cup at age 14 · among the youngest competitors at the historic event. His mechanical skill and rapid build pace defined an early era of competitive Fortnite play, and his style influenced a generation of European pros including Mero, Andilex, and MariusCOW.

Early life

Jackson was born August 13, 2004 in the United Kingdom. He began playing Fortnite at age 13 and was identified early as a mechanical prodigy by other UK competitive players. He launched his YouTube channel on December 25, 2015, originally focused on FIFA content before transitioning to Fortnite in 2018.[3]

Career

Summer Skirmish era (2018)

Jackson rose to prominence during the Summer Skirmish series of 2018, the first major prize-pool Fortnite competition. He competed under the Team Secret banner and quickly became a top European name through online events.

World Cup 2019

At age 14, Jackson qualified for the 2019 Fortnite World Cup Solo Finals, finishing 19th with 17 points. He also competed in the Duo finals with partner Mitr0, where they finished 3rd, taking home $1.5 million ($750K each). At the time he was among the youngest players at the event.[4]

FaZe Clan years (2019-2024)

FaZe Clan signed Jackson on July 7, 2019, immediately following his World Cup performance. He became one of the most prominent figures in the org's content arm and competed regularly in EU FNCS finals throughout Chapters 2, 3, and 4. While he never won an FNCS Major during his FaZe tenure, multiple top-10 finishes contributed to his $1.53M lifetime earnings.[5]

Red Bull and free agency

Jackson signed an individual sponsorship deal with Red Bull eSports during his FaZe tenure, which continued after his eventual departure from competitive play. As of 2026 he is a free agent focusing on streaming, but has occasionally competed in Cash Cup events.

Streaming and content

Jackson's Twitch channel reached 7.2 million followers by 2024, placing him among the top-followed Fortnite streamers historically. His Instagram account has 4.5 million followers, and he was named the most talked-about esports player on Twitter in early 2022 according to Esports News UK.[2]
