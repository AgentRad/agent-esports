About
Architect of the Vitality system

Rémy Quoniam played top-flight French Counter-Strike across the 1.6 and Source eras, retiring as a competitor in 2014 and immediately turning to staff work with several smaller French projects · webSPELL, Melty, the early Epsilon shells. The reps mattered more than the names. By the time Vitality launched its CS:GO project in 2018 he was the obvious hire to give it shape, and the org has been built around his structure ever since.

He stepped away briefly in 2022 for a stint with G2, then a short consulting period at TSM, before returning to Vitality in 2023. The second tenure has been the most successful of his career. Vitality have lifted BLAST World Final 2023, IEM Katowice 2025, the BLAST Austin Major and the StarLadder Budapest Major back-to-back, IEM Kraków 2026 and PGL Cluj-Napoca 2026, with ZywOo collecting his fourth and fifth HLTV MVPs along the way.

What keeps the system humming is XTQZZZ's bilingual room. He runs scrim debriefs in French with apEX as IGL but pushes utility timing notes to mezii and ropz in English, then reconciles the two threads himself. He is also unusually willing to sub map veto authority to the players when the calendar gets dense, which is uncommon among coaches with this many trophies. Opposing analysts describe Vitality's mid-round adaptation as the cleanest in the world right now, and most of them put XTQZZZ's calm radio voice in the back end of the demo as the reason why.
