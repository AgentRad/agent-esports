★ 2021 CDL Champion · 2019 CWL Champs MVP · 4x Stage Major MVP

Simp

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Chris Lehr (born February 6, 2001), known online as Simp, is an American professional Call of Duty player currently signed to FaZe Vegas. The roster's primary SMG slayer, Lehr has captured the 2021 CDL Championship, was named MVP of the 2019 CWL Championship, and has accumulated approximately $1,971,277 in tracked tournament earnings, the second-highest figure in Call of Duty history.[1]

Lehr first signed with eUnited Cadets at age sixteen on November 30, 2017, was promoted to the senior eUnited roster in March 2019 and won the 2019 CWL Championship in his rookie senior year, lifting the Black Ops 4 trophy and the Finals MVP. He joined Atlanta FaZe on October 26, 2019 and remained with the franchise through its September 2025 relocation to Las Vegas as FaZe Vegas.[1][2]

Early life and amateur path

Chris Lehr was born on February 6, 2001 in the United States. He came up through the amateur Call of Duty ladder at the tail end of the MLG era and the start of the Call of Duty World League, drawing scouts' attention as a teen for his raw aim numbers and unusually disciplined Search and Destroy decision-making for his age group.[1]

Competitive career

eUnited Cadets and senior team (2017 to 2019)

Lehr signed with eUnited Cadets, the development side, on November 30, 2017. He was promoted to the senior eUnited roster on March 19, 2019 and immediately became the standout slayer on a lineup also containing Clayster, Arcitys, Prestinni and aBeZy. The team won the CWL Championship 2019 on Black Ops 4, the last MLG-branded CoD World Championship before the franchised CDL launched, and Lehr was named the event's MVP at the age of eighteen.[2]

Atlanta FaZe (2019 to 2025)

Lehr joined Atlanta FaZe on October 26, 2019 alongside aBeZy, Cellium and Arcitys. The roster became the dominant team of the early CDL franchise era, winning the CDL Championship 2021 on Cold War in a sweep over Toronto Ultra, plus the Esports World Cup 2024 Call of Duty bracket and a string of Stage Majors across the 2021 to 2025 calendar. Lehr was the league's Regular Season MVP in 2021 and the Stage Major MVP four times (2021, 2023, 2024 and 2025).[1]

The Atlanta FaZe roster across this stretch is widely cited as the cleanest SMG-AR pairing of the franchise era, with Simp and aBeZy in the slayer roles and Cellium in the AR anchor. Atlanta repeatedly came up short of the Championship between 2021 and 2025, finishing top three on multiple occasions but losing in three separate Champs grand finals.

FaZe Vegas (2025 to present)

FaZe Esports relocated the franchise to Las Vegas as FaZe Vegas on September 19, 2025. Lehr was retained as the cornerstone of the new project alongside Drazah and offseason additions 04 and Abuzah. The 2026 season is the first under the new branding, and Lehr enters with the second-highest career earnings in Call of Duty history.[1]

Playstyle and analysis

Lehr is the discipline's reference SMG slayer alongside Shotzzy, anchored by an unusually high engagement rate on Hardpoint and a top-tier pre-aim profile on Search and Destroy openers. His Sub-machine-gun setup tends to bias toward the longest-range option in the loadout pool, which keeps him alive in mid-range trades that most pure slayers would lose. Coaches who have worked with him point to his late-round Search and Destroy decision-making as the differentiator: he calls retake timings more aggressively than most, and the FaZe sites are routinely structured around his preferred entry angle. He has been a fixture inside the Power Index top three across every CDL season from 2021 onward.

Legacy and awards

  • 2019: CWL Championship winner (eUnited), Finals MVP
  • 2021: CDL Champion (Atlanta FaZe), Regular Season MVP, Stage Major MVP, Team of the Year
  • 2023: Stage Major MVP
  • 2024: Stage Major MVP, Esports World Cup champion (Atlanta FaZe), Team of the Year
  • 2025: Stage Major MVP, Team of the Year

His $1.97M career earnings rank second all-time in the discipline and the most for any active player as of the 2026 season. The combination of a CWL Champs MVP and a CDL ring is a structural marker only a handful of players in any era share.[1][2]
