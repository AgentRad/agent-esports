Zeus

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Choi Woo-je (Korean: 최우제; born March 27, 2004), better known as Zeus, is a South Korean professional League of Legends player who plays top lane for Hanwha Life Esports in the LCK. He is a two-time League of Legends World Champion (2023, 2024) with T1 and was named Worlds 2024 Finals MVP after T1 defeated Bilibili Gaming 3-2 in London.[1][2]

Zeus debuted with T1 in 2022 at age 17 and quickly became one of the most highly rated young top laners in the LCK. After winning back-to-back World Championships in 2023 and 2024, he departed T1 in November 2024 in a high-profile contract negotiation that saw him sign with rival Hanwha Life Esports for the 2025 season.[2][3]

Early life

Choi Woo-je was born on March 27, 2004 in South Korea. He was promoted from the T1 Academy roster to the main T1 LCK roster ahead of the 2022 LCK Spring split, joining Faker, Oner, Gumayusi and Keria.[1]

Competitive career

T1 era (2022–2024)

Zeus was the rookie top laner on the T1 roster that went 18-0 in the 2022 LCK Spring regular season, the first undefeated split in LCK history. T1 reached the Worlds 2022 final but lost to DRX 2-3.[2]

In 2023 T1 won the World Championship in Seoul, defeating Weibo Gaming 3-0. T1 successfully defended the title in 2024 in London against Bilibili Gaming 3-2, with Zeus named Worlds 2024 Finals MVP after a dominant Jax performance in game 5.[2][3]

2024 contract dispute

In November 2024, days after Worlds 2024, T1 announced that contract negotiations with Zeus had broken down. The dispute was the most-discussed roster controversy in LCK history at the time. Zeus signed with Hanwha Life Esports the following week for a reported salary that placed him among the top three highest-paid LCK players for 2025.[3]

Hanwha Life Esports (2025–present)

Zeus joined a strong Hanwha Life Esports roster for 2025 and reached multiple LCK playoff finals. The team qualified for Worlds 2025 but was eliminated by KT Rolster in the semifinals. Zeus remained with Hanwha Life for the 2026 season.[3]

Legacy and impact

Zeus is widely regarded as one of the best top laners in League of Legends history, despite having only four full LCK seasons of professional play as of 2026. He is one of only a handful of top laners to win consecutive Worlds titles and is the youngest Worlds Finals MVP since Faker in 2013.[2]

Personal life

Zeus is based in Seoul. He maintains a private life away from streaming and is publicly close with former T1 teammates Faker, Oner, Gumayusi and Keria despite his 2024 departure. He maintains an active presence on Instagram.[1]
