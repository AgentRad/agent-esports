Zeus

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Choi Woo-je (Korean: 최우제; born March 27, 2004), better known as Zeus, is a South Korean professional League of Legends player who plays top lane for Hanwha Life Esports in the LCK. He is a two-time League of Legends World Champion (2023, 2024) with T1 and was named Worlds 2024 Finals MVP after T1 defeated Bilibili Gaming 3-2 in London.[1][2]

Zeus debuted with T1 in 2022 at age 17 and quickly became one of the most highly rated young top laners in the LCK. After winning back-to-back World Championships in 2023 and 2024, he departed T1 in November 2024 in a high-profile contract negotiation that saw him sign with rival Hanwha Life Esports for the 2025 season.[2][3]

Early life and gaming background

Choi Woo-je was born on March 27, 2004, in South Korea. He came up through the Korean amateur League of Legends ecosystem of the late 2010s, joining the T1 youth pipeline as a teenager and rising quickly through the academy ranks. By his mid-teens he had developed a wide top-lane champion pool, with comfort across both carry-orientated and tank-style picks, and was identified as the next top-lane prospect destined for the main T1 roster.[1]

His scouting profile combined the unusual mechanical ceiling of a high-elo Korean solo-queue grinder with the mental composure typically associated with veteran starters. T1's coaching staff and front office made the call ahead of the 2022 season to promote him directly from the academy roster to the LCK main team, joining a generational lineup built around Faker, Oner, Gumayusi and Keria.

Competitive career

T1 Academy and promotion (2021)

Zeus spent the 2021 season inside the T1 Academy ecosystem, splitting time between Challengers Korea and intra-organisational scrim work. The academy environment paired him with the same staff he would later work alongside on the main team, accelerating the structural transition into the LCK rotation. He was promoted to the main roster ahead of the 2022 Spring split.[1]

T1 era (2022 to 2024)

Zeus was the rookie top laner on the T1 roster that went 18-0 in the 2022 LCK Spring regular season, the first undefeated split in league history. The team reached the Worlds 2022 final in San Francisco but lost 2-3 to DRX in a five-game series widely regarded as one of the greatest finals ever played. The defeat shaped the next chapter of his career: rather than rebuild, T1 kept the entire starting five together, and Zeus matured into one of the most-watched top laners in the world.[2]

In 2023 T1 won the World Championship in Seoul, defeating Weibo Gaming 3-0 in front of a home crowd at the Gocheok Sky Dome. T1 successfully defended the title in 2024 in London against Bilibili Gaming 3-2, with Zeus named Worlds 2024 Finals MVP after a dominant Jax performance in game five. The MVP placed him among the youngest top-lane Finals MVPs in League of Legends history and sealed his reputation as the cleanest mechanical top laner of his generation.[2][3]

2024 contract dispute and transfer

In November 2024, days after Worlds 2024, T1 announced that contract negotiations with Zeus had broken down. The dispute was the most-discussed roster controversy in LCK history at the time, with extensive public commentary from current and former pros, agents and league analysts. Zeus signed with Hanwha Life Esports the following week for a reported salary that placed him among the top three highest-paid LCK players for the 2025 season.[3]

The departure ended one of the more romantic continuity stories in modern League of Legends, and it reframed Hanwha Life Esports's pitch to other free agents in the same market. T1 went on to win Worlds 2025 with a different top laner, completing the three-peat without him.

Hanwha Life Esports (2025 to present)

Zeus joined a strong Hanwha Life Esports roster for 2025 and reached multiple LCK playoff finals. The team qualified for Worlds 2025 but was eliminated by KT Rolster in the semifinals, denying him the chance at a third consecutive title with a new organisation. Zeus remained with Hanwha Life for the 2026 season as the structural anchor of the lineup, and his individual numbers across the early 2026 split have continued to track among the best in the LCK.[3]

Playstyle and analysis

Zeus is the modern carry-top archetype in its purest form. His champion pool covers nearly the entire top-lane meta, from Aatrox and Jax in carry windows to Renekton and Ksante in tank flexes, and his lane-phase efficiency on those picks is the most-discussed structural advantage on every roster he has played for. He is comfortable taking the first dive, comfortable scaling on a counter pick, and one of very few top laners willing to teleport away from a winning lane state to control side-lane priority for his team. Coaches who have worked with him point to his ward placement and his early-game pathing as quietly elite, and his side-lane one-versus-two priority numbers across the 2023 and 2024 international cycles are some of the highest tracked at the position. The trade-off is that his role as a self-sufficient lane carry can leave him out of position when the roster's macro shotcalling falls behind a series, a tension that has been visible in his Hanwha Life run more than during the T1 era.

Legacy and impact

Zeus is widely regarded as one of the best top laners in League of Legends history, despite having only four full LCK seasons of professional play as of 2026. He is one of only a handful of top laners to win consecutive Worlds titles, and his Worlds 2024 Finals MVP placed him among the youngest non-mid-lane Finals MVPs the tournament has produced. His status as a generational lane-phase top laner has changed how LCK organisations evaluate teenage prospects at the role, with several teams using his promotion timeline as a template for academy-to-main-stage decisions.[2]

The 2024 contract dispute also reshaped the public conversation about LCK player negotiations, accelerating discussions about transparency, agent representation and the structural power of star players inside Korean organisations. Within Hanwha Life Esports he remains the most marketable face of the roster and a natural focal point of the team's international tournament campaigns.

Personal life

Zeus is based in Seoul. He maintains a private life away from streaming and is publicly close with former T1 teammates Faker, Oner, Gumayusi and Keria despite his 2024 departure, with several of those former teammates appearing in his social media content during off-season periods. He maintains an active presence on Instagram and X.[1]
