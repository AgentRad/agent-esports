Vatira

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Axel Touret (born May 14, 2006), known online as Vatira, is a French professional Rocket League player currently signed to Karmine Corp. A primary striker with one of the highest-mechanical ceilings on the international tour, he has been the franchise face of the Karmine RL division since the organisation built its competitive roster around him in late 2022.[1]

Touret is a three-time RLCS Major champion, having lifted the RLCS 2021-22 Spring Major with Team Queso, the RLCS 2022-23 Winter Major with Karmine Corp, and the RLCS 2025 Birmingham Major with Karmine Corp. He has reached the top four of the World Championship in four straight seasons (2022, 2023, 2024, 2025) and has accumulated approximately $698,169 in tracked tournament earnings, the highest career total of any active Rocket League player.[1][2]

Early life

Touret was born on May 14, 2006 in France. He was identified as a top-of-cohort prospect on the European amateur ladder before he was old enough to draft into a salaried roster, and the early stretch of his career involved a series of short-term substitute and trial deals as the European tier-one ecosystem rotated him into a permanent slot. He was fifteen at the time of his first salaried roster.[1]

Competitive career

Magnolia and Team Queso (2021 to 2022)

Touret signed with Magnolia on July 7, 2021, then moved to Team Queso on September 24, 2021 for the back end of the RLCS 2021-22 season. The Queso roster won the RLCS 2021-22 Spring Major, his first piece of major silverware, before the org transitioned out of the Rocket League scene later that summer.[1][2]

Moist Esports (2022)

He moved to Moist Esports on May 5, 2022 in a brief stint that produced regional placings but no Major silverware. The deal ended on September 8, 2022.

Karmine Corp (2022 to present)

On October 2, 2022, Touret signed with Karmine Corp, the French organisation that had launched its Rocket League division a year earlier. The roster around Atow. and juicy with Extra coaching has stayed largely intact since, becoming the longest-running tier-one trio of the post-Epic era. They lifted the RLCS 2022-23 Winter Major on April 9, 2023 and the RLCS 2025 Birmingham Major on March 30, 2025, the two highest-profile titles in the organisation's history at the time of writing. They have also reached the top four of every RLCS World Championship from 2022 onwards.[1][3]

Playstyle and analysis

Touret is the most-watched striker on the international tour. He plays a high-aerial, demand-shot-driven style with one of the cleanest first-touch profiles in the game's history. The structural cost of the role is well-known: he absorbs the largest share of opposition reads in series, which is why his off-rounds tend to read as the visible reason a final game tilts. The compensating quality is that on his on-rounds the team can build a default around shot windows that nobody else in modern Rocket League opens at the same rate. Coaches who have worked with him describe his replay review as the most disciplined among Karmine players, and his comfort taking the third-man slot on slow-rotation defaults is the structural separator that keeps the team in top-four Worlds territory year after year. He plays primarily on a controller with a steeply ramped deadzone and is a vocal proponent of the European competitive ecosystem in interviews and content appearances.

Legacy

Touret is widely regarded as the strongest striker of the post-Epic era and the player most likely to convert to the Worlds title still missing from his cabinet. His three Major wins place him in the top tier of European Rocket League individual achievement, and his $698,169 in tracked earnings makes him the highest-earning active player in the game at twenty years old. He maintains a steady presence on Twitch for ranked sessions and on his YouTube channel for highlight content.[1]
