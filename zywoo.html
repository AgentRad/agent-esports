Mathieu Herbaut (born November 9, 2000), known online as ZywOo, is a French professional Counter-Strike 2 player and the franchise AWPer for Team Vitality, the organization he has represented continuously since 2018. He is a four-time HLTV Player of the Year (2019, 2020, 2023 and 2025), tied with the all-time record, and a three-time Major champion. He holds the all-time record for HLTV MVP medals, with 32 awards including three Major MVPs.[1][2]

Herbaut is widely considered, alongside s1mple, the defining individual talent of the modern Counter-Strike era. With approximately $2.17 million in tracked tournament earnings he ranks within the top three of all-time CS earnings. Vitality have, under his leadership, captured the Intel Grand Slam multiple times and entered 2026 as the world's number-one ranked side.[3]

Early life

Herbaut was born November 9, 2000 in France. He was a quiet, low-profile teenager who built his early reputation on French FACEIT ladders and small online cups before professional offers came. Unlike many top-tier prospects, he did not enter the limelight as a junior phenom but instead emerged seemingly fully formed in 2018 when he signed with Team Vitality at age 18.[1]

Career

Early career (2014-2018)

Herbaut passed through several tier-3 French rosters between 2014 and 2018 including dizLown, Nevermind, E-Corp Bumpers, WySix and aAa (against All authority). His prodigious talent was an open secret in French CS circles long before his Vitality signing.[2]

Team Vitality (2018-present)

Team Vitality signed Herbaut in October 2018 to lead a new French project alongside captain apEX and veteran NBK-. The team rose immediately. By the spring of 2019 ZywOo was already in HLTV's top-five player rankings, and by year's end he had been named HLTV's #1 player of 2019 · the youngest player in history to win the title.[3]

He repeated as HLTV #1 in 2020 in spite of Vitality not lifting a major trophy that year, on the strength of an unprecedented individual rating. Vitality rebuilt around him in 2022 with a multinational core (apEX, dupreeh, Magisk, Spinx) and would soon claim their first Major.[1]

Major championships

Vitality won their first Counter-Strike Major at BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023, defeating GamerLegion 2-0 in the grand final at the Accor Arena. Herbaut was named MVP of the event. Two further Major titles followed under the post-CS2 era at PGL Copenhagen Major 2024 and the Perfect World Shanghai Major 2024, with Herbaut also earning MVP honours at both events.[3][4]

He was named HLTV's best player of 2023 and again of 2025, becoming the second player ever (after s1mple) to win the title four times.[5]

Legacy and awards

With 32 HLTV MVP medals, Herbaut surpassed s1mple as the all-time MVP leader during the 2024 BLAST World Final. His 25 big-event MVPs and three Major MVPs are both records. Analysts regularly cite his peak rating, consistency at the highest stakes and his eight-year run at the very top of the player rankings as arguments for him being the greatest player in Counter-Strike history.[1][3]

Outside of his individual record, ZywOo is celebrated for his understated public persona. He has rarely given English-language interviews and almost never streams, in stark contrast with most of his peers · a choice that has only added to his mystique.[2]

Personal life

Herbaut continues to live in France between events. He has spoken in interviews about his enthusiasm for football and his support of Lille OSC. He is reportedly one of the highest-paid Counter-Strike players globally on a salary basis, though specific figures have never been publicly confirmed.[1]
