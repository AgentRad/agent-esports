Erick Santos (born June 15, 2003), known online as aspas, is a Brazilian professional Valorant player currently signed to MIBR as a duelist. He won the VALORANT Champions 2022 Grand Final with LOUD in Istanbul, was named tournament MVP, and has since collected back-to-back-to-back Duelist of the Year awards in 2023, 2024 and 2025, plus the inaugural Player of the Year award in 2025.[1][2]

Santos competed for LOUD from 2021 through 2023, won VCT Champions 2022 with the org, then signed with Leviatán for the 2024 season before returning to Brazil with MIBR in late 2025. He is widely regarded as the best Brazilian Valorant player of his generation and one of the top duelists in the world.[3]

Early life

Santos was born June 15, 2003 in São Paulo, Brazil. He grew up playing Counter-Strike: Global Offensive at a tier-three level in Brazil before transitioning to Valorant in 2020 during the closed beta. The nickname "aspas" derives from the Portuguese word for quotation marks.[1]

Competitive career

Early career (2020-2021)

Santos played for Gamelanders and Black Dragons E-sports during the early Brazilian Valorant scene in 2020 and 2021, posting standout duelist numbers in the VCT 2021 Brazil circuit. He signed with LOUD in November 2021 alongside Sacy and pANcada as part of the org's first roster build.

LOUD and VCT Champions 2022

LOUD broke through in 2022, winning VCT 2022 Brazil Stage 1, finishing second at Masters Reykjavik 2022 after losing to OpTic Gaming, and entering Champions in Istanbul as a top-tier favorite.

At VALORANT Champions 2022, LOUD swept through the lower bracket and defeated OpTic Gaming 3-1 in the Grand Final on September 18, 2022. Santos was named tournament MVP for a campaign in which he led the event in average combat score and posted multiple thirty-kill maps. The team's $300,000 winner share was the largest VCT prize at the time.[2]

LOUD finished third at VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles after a top-2 placement at the regional VCT Americas League, where Santos was named regular season MVP.

Leviatán (2024-2025)

Santos signed with Leviatán in November 2023 in a multi-million-dollar move that consolidated the Latin American Spanish-speaking franchise around a Brazilian core. He won VCT 2024 Americas Stage 2 with Leviatán in 2024, was named stage MVP, and reached a third Champions semifinal at VALORANT Champions 2024 in Seoul.[3]

Return to Brazil with MIBR (2025-present)

Santos signed with MIBR in November 2025 in a homecoming move that reunited him with several Brazilian veterans. MIBR opened the VCT 2026 Americas Kickoff with a 7-2 record and qualified for VCT Masters Toronto as the second seed from the Americas.

Santos was named 2025 Player of the Year by VLR.gg and a third consecutive Duelist of the Year, the first player in Valorant history to win the duelist award three years in a row.[4]

Legacy and impact

Santos is widely regarded as the most successful Brazilian Valorant player in history. His MVP performance at Champions 2022 was the defining moment for Latin American Valorant, helping LOUD become the most-followed Valorant brand outside of North America. As of May 2026, his $254,789 in tracked tournament earnings places him fourth on the all-time Valorant earnings list, and he has collected an unmatched four MVP awards across regional and international play.

Personal life

Santos lives in São Paulo, Brazil. He is fluent in Portuguese and English. He is widely active on Brazilian Twitch, where he streams under the handle aspaszin.
