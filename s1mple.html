Oleksandr Olehovych Kostyliev (born October 2, 1997), known online as s1mple, is a Ukrainian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to BC.Game Esports. A primary AWPer who also rifles at an elite level, he is widely regarded as one of the greatest individual talents in the history of Counter-Strike. He has been ranked HLTV's number-one player of the year on three occasions (2018, 2021 and 2022) and holds 21 HLTV MVP medals, including the MVP of the PGL Major Stockholm 2021.[1][2]

Kostyliev spent the bulk of his career, from 2016 to 2023, with Natus Vincere (NaVi), winning the Intel Grand Slam Season 3 alongside Major Stockholm in a single season. After loan stints with Team Falcons and FaZe Clan in 2024 and 2025, he joined BC.Game Esports in late 2025. With approximately $1.75 million in tracked tournament earnings, he ranks among the highest-earning Counter-Strike players of all time.[3]

Early life

Kostyliev was born October 2, 1997 in Kyiv, Ukraine. He began playing Counter-Strike 1.6 as a child and was widely identified as a prodigy on Ukrainian and Russian-language matchmaking servers in his early teens. He moved to Counter-Strike: Global Offensive in 2013 and within a year was competing in tier-2 events.[1]

Career

Early career (2013-2016)

Kostyliev's first notable team was the Ukrainian roster LAN DODGERS in 2013-2014. He passed through Courage Gaming, Hashtag, Amazing Gaming and HellRaisers before a brief stint with Team Liquid in 2016, where he reached the grand final of the ESL One Cologne 2016 Major as a 19-year-old · the first time he played a Major final.[2]

Natus Vincere (2016-2023)

In August 2016, Kostyliev signed with Natus Vincere, the Ukrainian organization he would represent for nearly seven years. NaVi reached multiple grand finals through 2017-2020 but were repeatedly denied at the final hurdle. He was nonetheless named HLTV's best player of 2018 on the strength of one of the most statistically dominant individual seasons ever recorded, finishing top-three in player rating at virtually every event he attended.[2]

The breakthrough came in 2021. NaVi won the PGL Major Stockholm 21-game unbeaten run, defeating G2 Esports 2-0 in the grand final. Kostyliev was named MVP. The team also captured the Intel Grand Slam Season 3 in the same calendar year. He was again named HLTV #1 in 2021 and 2022, becoming the second player ever (after device) to win the title three times.[3][4]

Falcons and FaZe loans (2024-2025)

Following NaVi's roster restructure in mid-2023, Kostyliev took an extended break from competition. He returned in 2024 on loan to Team Falcons for several events including IEM Cologne and ESL Pro League S20, and then to FaZe Clan in early 2025 as a stand-in for broky during medical leave.[5]

BC.Game Esports (2025-present)

In late 2025, Kostyliev signed permanently with the multi-region organization BC.Game Esports, anchoring a young Ukrainian core. The team qualified for IEM Katowice 2026 through the European RMR cycle and have been a consistent top-eight presence at tier-1 events through the spring 2026 season.[6]

Legacy and awards

Kostyliev's 21 HLTV MVP medals are the most of any player in Counter-Strike history at the time of writing. He held the record outright until ZywOo passed him with the 2024 BLAST World Final, after which the two have alternated at the top. His 2018 individual season is widely considered the high-water mark for AWPing in CS:GO and is regularly cited in best-of-all-time discussions.[2][4]

Beyond competition, Kostyliev has been a major streaming personality on Twitch, where his channel has over 2.4 million followers. He has been a vocal commentator on roster moves, coaching, and the state of professional Counter-Strike.

Personal life

Kostyliev grew up in Kyiv and continues to maintain residency in Ukraine. Following the 2022 Russian invasion, he and NaVi were prominent in fundraising for Ukrainian humanitarian causes during their PGL Antwerp 2022 run. He has spoken in interviews about plans to transition to streaming and content creation full-time at the end of his current competitive cycle.[1]
