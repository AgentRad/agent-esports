About the Tournament
Sportpaleis Antwerp · May 9 to 22, 2022

PGL ran the first CS:GO Major outside of an empty studio in over two years. The Champions Stage filled the Antwerps Sportpaleis for two weekends, the first crowd a CS:GO Major had played in front of since IEM Katowice 2020 closed its doors as the pandemic took hold.

The bracket worked in three sequential gates. The Challengers Stage and Legends Stage each ran a Swiss format, trimming the field on a familiar 3-0 advance, 0-3 eliminate ladder. Eight survivors crossed into a single-elimination Champions Stage played out on the arena floor.

FaZe arrived in Antwerp on a heater. The lineup with karrigan back at the helm and ropz folded in on rifle had already taken IEM Katowice in February and ESL Pro League in April. Antwerp was the missing piece. The squad cleared the Legends Stage 3-0 without dropping a map and powered through the playoffs without ever facing a deciding map until the bracket ran out of teams.

Natus Vincere came in as defending Major champions and the consensus best team on the planet for the prior nine months. The grand final was the rematch the broadcast wanted. FaZe got it on Inferno, took NaVi to overtime on the EU favorites' own pick, and closed it on Nuke without giving the world's best AWPer a chance to swing the series back.

FaZe opened on the T side of Inferno, banked an 8-7 half, and turned what should have been a comfortable close-out into a chaotic OT. NaVi ran the apartments-into-banana double pressure all the way to a 15-15 board before broky and ropz iced the extra rounds.

Map two was over by the second-half timeout. FaZe's CT Nuke read every NaVi setup, electronic and s1mple were forced into bottom-of-site one-twos that broky picked clean, and the trophy ceremony was set up before the final round was even called.
