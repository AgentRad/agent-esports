★ FNCS 2026 Major 1 NA West Champion · Dignitas

Khanada

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports.

Leon Khim Woobin (born October 15, 2004), known online as Khanada, is a Korean-American professional Fortnite Battle Royale player currently competing for Dignitas in the NA West region. With over $1.28 million in career tournament earnings across 131 events, he is among the longest-tenured active competitive Fortnite players.[1]

In April 2026, Khim and his duo partner VicterV won the FNCS 2026 Major 1 NA West Grand Finals by a 160-point margin, taking home $30,000 each and securing qualification to the Major 1 Summit. He is currently competing in the Summit alongside Rapid as part of the Dignitas trio at 710 points (3rd place).

Early life

Khim was born October 15, 2004 to Korean parents living in the United States. He began competing in Fortnite during the Chapter 1 Season X era and quickly became one of the most promising young North American talents.[2]

Career

Trio with Tfue (2019-2020)

In September 2019, at age 14, Khanada became widely known for joining a trio with Tfue · then the most-followed Fortnite streamer on Twitch. The pairing thrust Khim into mainstream Fortnite consciousness and positioned him as one of the next-generation NA East stars.[3]

TSM era (2020)

In March 2020, Khim signed with TSM, beginning his tenure as a sponsored professional. His career had a strong start with multiple top-10 FNCS finishes before the events of late 2020.

Twitch ban controversy

In September 2020, Khim was banned mid-stream from Twitch after a moderation team review of language used during a competitive broadcast. Twitch stated that the ban · issued for "threats of violence" · would not be reversed despite an appeals process. As a result, TSM parted ways with Khim that same month.[4]

Khim continued streaming on YouTube and other platforms during this period and gradually rebuilt his competitive presence through Cash Cup placements.

Dignitas era (2024-)

Dignitas signed Khim in 2024 as part of a renewed push into competitive Fortnite. Now partnered with VicterV for duos and as part of a trio with Rapid and Boltz, Khim has reached the highest competitive form of his career · winning the 2026 NA West Major 1 Grand Finals and currently sitting at #5 in global Power Rankings.
