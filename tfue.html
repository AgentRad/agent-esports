Tfue

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports.

Turner Ellis Tenney (born January 2, 1998), known online as Tfue, is an American retired professional Fortnite player and Twitch streamer. One of the foundational figures of competitive Fortnite, Tenney was a dominant force during the 2018 Skirmish era and remains among the most-followed Fortnite streamers in history with over 11 million Twitch followers.[1]

He competed for FaZe Clan from April 2018 through May 2020, a tenure that ended in a high-profile lawsuit which became a watershed moment for esports player contracts. He retired from competitive Fortnite in 2023 with approximately $689,000 in tracked tournament earnings.

Early life

Tenney was born January 2, 1998 in Indian Rocks Beach, Florida. He played Call of Duty competitively in his teens before transitioning to H1Z1 and eventually Fortnite Battle Royale at the game's competitive launch in 2018.

Summer Skirmish dominance

During the 2018 Summer Skirmish series, Tenney emerged as the most consistent top-placement player. He won the PAX West LAN event in September 2018, taking $30,000, and finished as the highest-earning Fortnite player of 2018. His mechanical skill, particularly box-fight ability, defined the early Fortnite competitive ceiling.

FaZe Clan and lawsuit

FaZe Clan signed Tenney in April 2018. In May 2019 he filed a lawsuit against the organization alleging that his contract was "grossly oppressive" and contained illegal clauses. The case was widely reported on and helped catalyze industry-wide reform of esports player contracts. The dispute settled in 2020 and he departed FaZe in May of that year.[2]

2019 World Cup

Tenney qualified for both Solo and Duo finals at the 2019 Fortnite World Cup. He partnered with Cloak in the Duo Finals and finished outside the top 10 in both events, a result that disappointed fans given his pre-event status as a heavy favorite.

Legacy

Despite never winning a major championship, Tenney's Twitch following peaked at over 11 million in 2020, making him the most-followed Fortnite streamer in history at the time. His departure from competitive play in 2023 marked the end of an era. He remains an active streamer and YouTuber.
