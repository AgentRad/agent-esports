Less

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Felipe de Loyola Basso (born February 24, 2005), known online as Less, is a Brazilian professional Valorant player currently signed to KRÜ Esports as a sentinel and Viper specialist. He won VALORANT Champions 2022 in Istanbul with LOUD at age 17, becoming one of the youngest world champions in Valorant history.[1][2]

Basso played for LOUD from 2022 through 2024, signed with French org Team Vitality for the 2025 VCT EMEA season, and returned to South America with KRÜ Esports in late 2025. He is widely regarded as one of the best Viper players in Valorant.[3]

Early life

Basso was born February 24, 2005, in Brazil. He started competitive Valorant in 2021 at age 16, drawing scouting attention through tier-three Brazilian rosters such as Jaguares Esports and Galaxy Carrots.[1]

Competitive career

Tier-three Brazil (2021)

Basso played for Jaguares Esports and then Galaxy Carrots through 2021, posting standout sentinel numbers in VCT 2021 Brazil's open qualifier circuit. He was scouted by LOUD ahead of the 2022 season as the team's primary sentinel.

LOUD and VCT Champions 2022

Basso signed with LOUD in early 2022 alongside aspas, saadhak, Sacy and pANcada. He played Viper, Cypher and Killjoy at top international level for the roster.

LOUD won VCT 2022 Brazil Stage 1, finished second at Masters Reykjavik 2022 after losing to OpTic Gaming, and ran through the lower bracket at VALORANT Champions 2022 in Istanbul. They defeated OpTic Gaming 3-1 in the Grand Final on September 18, 2022, with Basso lifting the trophy at age 17.[2]

LOUD finished third at VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles after a top-2 placement at the regional VCT Americas League. Basso parted ways with the org in late 2024 amid a roster rebuild.

Team Vitality (2024-2025)

Basso signed a multi-year contract with French org Team Vitality in November 2024 ahead of the 2025 VCT EMEA season as the team's primary sentinel. Vitality reached the playoffs of VCT EMEA Stage 1 but missed international qualification across both 2025 stages.[3]

KRÜ Esports (2025-present)

Basso returned to South America by signing with Argentine org KRÜ Esports in late 2025, reuniting with former LOUD teammate saadhak as part of a Spanish-speaking Latin American Ascension project for the 2026 VCT season.

Legacy and impact

Basso was the youngest member of LOUD's Champions-winning roster and is widely credited with redefining the sentinel role in the early professional Valorant meta. As of May 2026, his $174,365 in tracked tournament earnings places him among the top-10 Brazilian Valorant earners.

Personal life

Basso is from São Paulo, Brazil. He streams in Portuguese under the handle lessvlr and has been cited as a model for younger Brazilian sentinels.
