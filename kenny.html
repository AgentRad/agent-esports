Kenny

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Kenneth Williams (born September 2, 1999), known online as Kenny and previously as Kuavo, is an American-Filipino professional Call of Duty player currently signed to Los Angeles Thieves. The roster's primary AR anchor, Williams has captured two CDL Championships (2022 with LA Thieves, 2024 with OpTic Texas) and accumulated approximately $1,233,457 in tracked tournament earnings.[1]

Williams is one of only a handful of CoD players with rings on multiple franchises. He turned professional in 2015 with Team JusTus at the age of fifteen, broke through with 100 Thieves in 2018, won the 2022 Championship anchoring the LA Thieves and added a second ring with OpTic Texas in 2024 before returning to LA in late 2025.[1][2]

Early life and amateur path

Kenneth Williams was born on September 2, 1999. He came up through the late MLG amateur ladder during the Black Ops III and Infinite Warfare seasons, drawing scouts' attention as one of the cleanest pure aimers in his age group. He played publicly under the alias Kuavo for several years before settling on Kenny.[1]

JusTus and Team Kaliber (2015 to 2018)

Williams signed his first professional contract with Team JusTus on August 10, 2015, then joined Team Kaliber on October 24, 2017. He won the CWL Pro League 2018 Stage 2 MVP with Team Kaliber, the first individual award of his career.[1]

100 Thieves (2018 to 2019)

Williams joined 100 Thieves on August 28, 2018 alongside Octane, SlasheR, Enable and Spacely. The team won CWL Pro League 2018 and finished top-three at the 2019 CWL Championship, but the organisation declined to enter the inaugural CDL franchise and the roster dispersed.

OpTic Gaming LA and LA Thieves (2019 to 2023)

Williams transferred to OpTic Gaming Los Angeles on October 24, 2019, the franchise rebranded as Los Angeles Thieves on November 7, 2020. The Thieves won the CDL Championship 2022 on Vanguard, defeating Toronto Ultra in the final. Williams was named Stage 4 Major MVP and Playoffs MVP that season, his most decorated individual year. He left the Thieves on July 14, 2023 after a top-six 2023 finish.[1]

OpTic Texas (2023 to 2025)

Williams joined OpTic Texas on July 30, 2023, paired with Shotzzy, Dashy and Pred. The team won the Stage 4 Major in 2023 and the CDL Championship 2024, defeating New York Subliners 5-1 in the Allen, Texas grand final. Williams left OpTic Texas on March 17, 2025 in an offseason restructure.

Return to LA Thieves (2025 to present)

Williams played briefly for Minnesota RØKKR from April to July 2025, then returned to the Los Angeles Thieves on September 24, 2025 as the franchise's primary AR anchor for the 2026 season.[1]

Playstyle and analysis

Williams is the discipline's reference AR anchor across multiple eras of Call of Duty, characterised by an unusually disciplined Hardpoint rotation, conservative Search and Destroy entries and a willingness to spend the back end of his Hardpoint timer absorbing rotational gunfights so his SMG slayers can free-flow into the next contest. His pairing alongside Shotzzy at OpTic Texas in 2023-2024 was the cleanest AR-SMG fit of the franchise era; the partnership won the 2024 ring and the 2023 Stage 4 Major. Coaches who have worked with him point to his quiet, low-volume comms as a stylistic match for veteran IGL setups, which is why every team he has joined has built defaults around his preferred angle on the league's standard maps.

Legacy and awards

  • 2018: CWL Pro League Stage 2 MVP (Team Kaliber)
  • 2022: CDL Champion (LA Thieves), Stage 4 Major MVP, Playoffs MVP
  • 2023: Stage 4 Major winner (OpTic Texas)
  • 2024: CDL Champion (OpTic Texas), Stage 3 Major winner

Two CDL rings on two different franchises is a structural achievement only a small handful of players in the league's history have produced. His three Stage Major wins additionally rank him in the top tier of franchise-era trophy lifters.[1][2]
