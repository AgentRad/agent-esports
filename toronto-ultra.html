Organization Overview
The only Canadian CDL franchise · CleanX's home

Toronto Ultra was unveiled on May 2, 2019 as the only Canadian franchise in the inaugural Call of Duty League, owned by OverActive Media. The organisation is the most-followed esports outfit in Canada and has run a long-term content partnership with the Toronto Maple Leafs and the Coca-Cola Coliseum venue across multiple Stage Major hostings.

Toronto's signature performances came at the CDL '23 Stage 3 Major, where the team defeated OpTic Texas 4-2 in the grand final, and the CDL '24 Stage 1 Major, where the team defeated Atlanta FaZe 4-1 to claim the season opener. Both wins were anchored by CleanX, the Danish AR who has been the most consistent international player in CDL history.

The franchise rebranded as Toronto KOI on October 23, 2025 in a co-brand with the European multi-game KOI organisation. The 2026 roster of CleanX, Insight, JoeDeceives and Kips is led by head coach Flux.
