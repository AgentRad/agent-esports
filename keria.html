Keria

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Ryu Min-seok (Korean: 류민석; born February 9, 2003), better known as Keria, is a South Korean professional League of Legends player who plays support for T1 in the LCK. He is a three-time League of Legends World Champion (2023, 2024, 2025) and is widely considered the best support player of his generation.[1][2]

Keria debuted with DRX in 2020 at the age of 17 and joined T1 ahead of the 2021 season. Alongside teammates Faker, Zeus, Oner and Gumayusi, he formed the core of the T1 roster that won three consecutive World Championships from 2023 to 2025.[2]

Early life

Ryu Min-seok was born on February 9, 2003 in South Korea. He played League of Legends in the Korean amateur scene before being signed by DragonX (DRX) ahead of the 2020 LCK Spring split as one of the youngest players in the league at the time.[1]

Competitive career

DRX debut (2020)

Keria debuted on DRX as a 17-year-old support and immediately won All-Pro honors. DRX qualified for the 2020 World Championship, where the team was eliminated in the quarterfinals by Damwon Gaming. Following the season, T1 signed Keria to a long-term contract.[2]

T1 early years (2021–2022)

Keria joined T1 as the support next to Faker for the 2021 season. T1 won the LCK Spring 2022 split with a 18-0 regular-season record, the first undefeated split in LCK history. T1 reached the Worlds 2022 final in San Francisco but lost 2-3 to DRX in a five-game series widely regarded as one of the greatest finals in League of Legends history.[2]

2023–2025 three-peat

Keria was a foundational piece of T1's three consecutive World Championship victories. In 2023 in Seoul, T1 defeated Weibo Gaming 3-0 in the final at the Gocheok Sky Dome. In 2024 in London, T1 defeated Bilibili Gaming 3-2 at The O2 Arena. In 2025 T1 defeated KT Rolster 3-1 to complete the three-peat, the first since SKT's 2015–2016 back-to-back.[2][3]

Across the three-peat run Keria has been named to multiple LCK All-Pro teams and is regularly cited by analysts as the highest-skilled engage support of his era.

Legacy and impact

Keria is widely regarded as the best support player in the world and one of the greatest supports in League of Legends history. As of May 2026 he holds three Worlds titles, multiple LCK titles and is one of the youngest players in history with three Worlds rings.[2]

Personal life

Keria is based in Seoul and lives at the T1 player house. He is publicly close with Faker and frequently appears in T1 organizational content. He maintains an active presence on Instagram and X.[1]
