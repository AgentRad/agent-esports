How Match Point Works
No bracket, no elimination matches, just a 50-point ladder

EA introduced Match Point in 2022 and it remains the signature look of an ALGS final. Twenty trios begin the Sunday session at zero points. Placement and kill points carry forward across the day. Once a trio reaches 50 points cumulatively, they enter Match Point status, which means they can win the title only by also winning a match outright. A 50-point team that places second in the next ring still has to win one outright to lift the trophy.

The format keeps every match alive. A team can sit at 49 points, win their first ring, and the title is theirs. Alternatively, the field can stay scrambled until match 8 with five teams sitting on Match Point and the loser pool counting kills furiously. The ALGS Year 4 Championship in Sapporo went all the way to the seventh match before GoNext sealed it with a Storm Point ring win.

Trios reach the 20-team Sunday lobby through two distinct tracks. The Bracket Stage Winners side advances the top 13 from a six-match Saturday session. The Elimination side, fed by the lower group-stage finishers, keeps trying until match 6 and sends through its top 7. Combine both lists and you have your 20.

Match Point Mechanics

Trigger: Reach 50 cumulative points across the Sunday session.

Win condition: Once on Match Point, a trio must place 1st in any subsequent match.

Tiebreaker: Multiple teams on Match Point compete simultaneously. First to win an outright match takes the title.
