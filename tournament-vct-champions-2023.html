About the Tournament
Shrine Expo Hall and Kia Forum · Los Angeles, California

Champions 2023 was the first edition staged under the new partnered VCT structure introduced that season. Sixteen teams qualified, four from each of the three regional leagues plus the LCQ winners. Riot split the event into two stages and two venues. The group phase ran out of Shrine Expo Hall in downtown LA from August 6 to 13. Playoffs followed at the bigger Shrine main floor before the top four moved into the 17,000-capacity Kia Forum for closing weekend.

Evil Geniuses were not the favourites. They had finished sixth in regular-season Americas before catching fire in the post-season, qualifying for Champions on a tight points margin. Their reward was a brutal opening group with Fnatic, the Masters Tokyo champion. EG dropped their opener, then ripped through the lower bracket of the group, beating EDward Gaming and FunPlus Phoenix to advance.

Once into the playoffs they never lost again. Bilibili Gaming fell 2-0. Fnatic, the team that had beaten them in groups, was eliminated 2-1 in a rematch where Demon1 dropped a 31-frag Map 3 on Lotus that the entire room watched in silence. Paper Rex took the long path through the lower bracket, dispatching LOUD on home soil for the Brazilian fans, before falling to EG in their first meeting of the playoffs. PRX then ran the lower bracket back through DRX and Fnatic to rematch EG in the grand final.

Game one of the final on Lotus went 13-9 to EG, with Demon1 posting 27 kills. Paper Rex hit back on Split 13-7. From there EG locked in. Bind 13-10, then a 13-7 closeout on Ascent gave the United States its first Valorant world championship. Demon1 was named Tournament MVP after averaging the highest combat score of any player in the bracket.
