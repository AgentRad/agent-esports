Helvijs Saukants (born February 14, 2001), known online as broky and nicknamed "the Latvian Laser", is a Latvian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to FaZe Clan. A primary AWPer who has spent his entire tier-one career with FaZe since September 2019, he is one of the most consistent snipers of the post-2020 era.[1][2]

Saukants reached his career peak as a member of the FaZe Clan roster that won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022. He was also a key part of the team that lifted CS Asia Championships 2023 and reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final. He has accumulated approximately $1.32 million in career earnings and has been ranked HLTV top 10 in three consecutive years (2022 to 2024).[1][3]

Early life

Saukants was born February 14, 2001 in Riga, Latvia. He began competing in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive in 2018 and was identified as a top European AWPing prospect during a brief tier-two run with the Latvian-led Epsilon Esports roster.[1]

Career

Epsilon (2019)

Saukants joined Epsilon Esports on February 14, 2019 (his eighteenth birthday). The team operated as ex-Epsilon for several months in mid-2019 before disbanding ahead of his FaZe move.[1]

FaZe Clan (2019 to present)

On September 26, 2019, Saukants signed with FaZe Clan as the long-term replacement for GuardiaN. After a difficult first 18 months and several roster reshuffles, the breakthrough came in 2022. The roster of karrigan, ropz, rain, Twistzz, and broky won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 over Natus Vincere, with broky earning the IEM Katowice 2022 MVP and BLAST Premier Fall Finals 2022 MVP in the same calendar year.[2][3]

FaZe also reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final, falling 0-2 to Team Vitality. Saukants was awarded a third HLTV MVP medal at IEM Chengdu 2024. He briefly took medical leave in early 2025, during which s1mple stood in, before returning later that year.[4]

Legacy and awards

Saukants is the only Latvian player to have lifted a Major trophy and remains the country's most decorated CS player. His 2022 individual season placed him at HLTV #6, then his highest finish, and his three Big-Event MVPs put him among the most-decorated AWPers of the FaZe era. He is widely cited for his unusually clean opening picks on T-side and his economy management instincts.[2][3]

Personal life

Saukants resides in Riga, Latvia. He has spoken publicly about the personal difficulties of long-term FaZe bootcamps in Berlin and Belgrade, and has been involved in Latvian-language CS community grassroots events.[1]
