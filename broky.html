Helvijs Saukants (born February 14, 2001), known online as broky and nicknamed "the Latvian Laser", is a Latvian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to FaZe Clan. A primary AWPer who has spent his entire tier-one career with FaZe since September 2019, he is one of the most consistent snipers of the post-2020 era.[1][2]

Saukants reached his career peak as a member of the FaZe Clan roster that won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022. He was also a key part of the team that lifted CS Asia Championships 2023 and reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final. He has accumulated approximately $1.32 million in career earnings and has been ranked HLTV top 10 in three consecutive years (2022 to 2024).[1][3]

Early life and gaming background

Helvijs Saukants was born on February 14, 2001, in Riga, Latvia. He began competing seriously in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive in 2018, joining the small but well-regarded Latvian online ladder community that had previously produced players like ZOREE. The Latvian scene at the time funneled talented juniors into Russian-speaking online cups, where Saukants earned a reputation for unusually steady AWPing in tight rounds.[1]

His scouting profile through 2018 emphasised mechanical consistency rather than flash. Where many young AWPers built their highlight reels on aggressive opening peeks, Saukants drew attention from European tier-two coaches for his disciplined positioning and his ability to anchor a default with limited utility. Within a year of formal competition he had attracted offers from multiple Western European organisations, and he signed his first salaried contract on his eighteenth birthday.

Competitive career

Epsilon Esports (2019)

Saukants joined Epsilon Esports on February 14, 2019, as the centerpiece AWPer of a Baltic-led roster targeting Polish and ESEA Premier qualification cycles. The team operated as ex-Epsilon for several months in mid-2019 after parting ways with the organisation, and the lineup eventually disbanded as larger offers arrived for Saukants's signature.[1]

The Epsilon stint was short, but it was where he attached himself to the role he would carry for the rest of his career. Reviewing demos from that period, FaZe Clan's coaching staff identified him as the most polished young AWPer available outside the marquee CIS pipeline.

FaZe Clan rise (2019 to 2022)

On September 26, 2019, Saukants signed with FaZe Clan as the long-term replacement for GuardiaN. The first eighteen months of his tenure were uneven. FaZe was in flux around the in-game leader position, and several iterations of the roster attempted to figure out which players belonged to a long-term core. Saukants's individual numbers held up through that turbulence, and his run as a default AWPer through 2020 and 2021 quietly built a foundation that would pay off when the lineup finally stabilised.[2]

The arrival of Twistzz in early 2021 completed the karrigan, ropz, rain, broky, Twistzz formation widely considered the most complete European-international roster of the post-Astralis era. The chemistry was immediate, and Saukants slotted comfortably as the AWPing centerpiece, with his teammates building executes around the angles he was most likely to take first.

FaZe peak era (2022 to 2024)

FaZe's 2022 was the peak of Saukants's career to date. The team won IEM Katowice 2022 in February, with Saukants named MVP, and added the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 in May, defeating Natus Vincere in the grand final. He picked up a second HLTV MVP medal at the BLAST Premier Fall Finals later in the same calendar year and finished HLTV #6, then his career-best ranking.[2][3]

The roster reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final, falling 0-2 to Team Vitality in one of the era's most-watched series. Saukants added a third HLTV MVP medal at IEM Chengdu 2024, completing a three-year stretch in which he was ranked HLTV top-ten in every season. The 2024 cycle, however, also marked the start of personal-health complications that would shape his early-2025 absence.[4]

Recent form (2025 to present)

Saukants briefly took medical leave in early 2025, during which the legendary s1mple stood in for FaZe at several events. He returned later that year and slotted back into the AWPing role, and the reunion with Twistzz in September 2025 restored the most successful version of the lineup. Through early 2026, FaZe have continued to be a regular S-Tier semifinalist with Saukants as the primary information-gatherer in the team's attack patterns.

Playstyle and analysis

Saukants is a default AWPer in the structural sense rather than a highlight-reel sniper. His T-side game is built around picking up information for his teammates first, and turning that information into a frag second. He tends to take the second look at a piece of map, often after a teammate has flashed or thrown utility, and his reset patience is one of the highest among elite AWPers, which lets FaZe burn opposing economies without committing to bad rounds. On CT, his angles often look conservative on the surface, but they are stacked over the rotation lanes that karrigan's defaults are most worried about, an arrangement that lets him collect kills without ever truly being out of position. His clean opening-pick rate and the steadiness of his economy management have been the qualities most often cited by analysts when discussing why FaZe's win rates rebound so sharply when he is healthy and on form.

Notable moments

  • Won IEM Katowice 2022 with FaZe Clan and was named MVP, his first HLTV MVP medal.[2]
  • Won the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 over Natus Vincere, FaZe's only Major title to date.[2]
  • Named MVP of the BLAST Premier Fall Finals 2022 and finished HLTV #6 for the year.[3]
  • Reached the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 grand final with FaZe, falling to Team Vitality.[3]
  • Won a third HLTV MVP medal at IEM Chengdu 2024, his third in a three-year stretch.[3]
  • Returned to FaZe after a 2025 medical absence and stabilised the team during the lead-in to the StarLadder Budapest Major run.[4]

Legacy and awards

Saukants is the only Latvian player to have lifted a Counter-Strike Major trophy, and he remains the country's most decorated competitive CS player by a wide margin. His three Big-Event MVPs put him among the most-decorated AWPers of the FaZe era, and his three consecutive HLTV top-ten finishes from 2022 through 2024 are matched by very few snipers outside the Russian-speaking pipeline. Within FaZe Clan he is widely viewed as the structural anchor of the lineup, the player whose form is the leading indicator for the team's fortunes in any given season.[2][3]

He is also one of the more prominent ambassadors for Baltic Counter-Strike, and his Twitch presence under the handle brokycs has drawn a meaningful Latvian-language audience back to elite play after a quiet decade for the local scene. He has been involved in Latvian community grassroots events between competitive seasons.

Personal life

Saukants resides in Riga, Latvia. He has spoken publicly about the personal difficulties of long-term FaZe bootcamps in Berlin and Belgrade, particularly during the 2025 health absence, and he is one of the few elite Counter-Strike players to base his off-season life entirely outside the major Western European hubs. He has been involved in Latvian-language CS community events and supports several local junior tournaments through his FaZe affiliation.[1]
