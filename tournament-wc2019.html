About the Tournament
July 26-28, 2019 · New York City

The 2019 Fortnite World Cup was the only edition of the event ever held. Following the success of the Summer Skirmish ($8M) and Fall Skirmish ($10M) series, Epic Games announced a $30 million prize pool · the largest single-event esports prize pool in history at the time · and opened qualifiers to anyone aged 13+.

Across 10 weeks of online open qualifiers from April through June 2019, an estimated 40 million players competed. The top 100 solo and top 50 duos from each region advanced to the LAN finals at Arthur Ashe Stadium · the home of the U.S. Open in tennis, with a 23,000+ capacity that sold out three consecutive days.

Every solo finalist was guaranteed a minimum $50,000. Every duo team was guaranteed $100,000. The grand prize: $3 million for the Solo champion, and $3 million split between the winning Duo. Side events for Pro-Am (celebrities + pros) and Creative mode rounded out the weekend.

The event has not been repeated. Epic Games shifted to the FNCS structure in 2020, citing pandemic logistics and a desire for more frequent regional competition. The 2019 World Cup remains the high-water mark for prize money in Fortnite competitive history.
