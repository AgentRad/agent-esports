Ruler

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Park Jae-hyuk (Korean: 박재혁; born December 14, 1997), better known as Ruler, is a South Korean professional League of Legends player who plays bot lane (ADC) for Gen.G Esports in the LCK. He is a 2017 League of Legends World Champion with Samsung Galaxy and was named Worlds 2017 Finals MVP after Samsung defeated SK Telecom T1 3-0 in Beijing.[1][2]

Ruler's career has spanned both major Korean and Chinese leagues. After Samsung was acquired by KSV (which later became Gen.G), he played four more seasons with the organization before moving to JD Gaming in the LPL for 2023, where he won the LPL Spring, LPL Summer and the 2023 Mid-Season Invitational. He returned to Gen.G ahead of the 2024 LCK season.[2]

Early life

Park Jae-hyuk was born on December 14, 1997 in South Korea. He played in the Korean amateur scene before being signed to Samsung Galaxy ahead of the 2016 season as a substitute, debuting in the main roster the same year.[1]

Competitive career

Samsung Galaxy and 2017 Worlds

Ruler was the bot laner for Samsung Galaxy from 2016 to 2017. Samsung reached the 2016 World Championship final in Los Angeles but lost 2-3 to SK Telecom T1. Samsung returned to the 2017 Worlds final in Beijing the next year and defeated SK Telecom T1 3-0, ending Faker's three-peat bid. Ruler was named Worlds 2017 Finals MVP with a Tristana Pentakill on game 3.[2]

Gen.G era (2018–2022)

After Samsung was acquired by KSV and rebranded as Gen.G in 2018, Ruler remained as the ADC through five seasons. He was a foundational piece of the rebuild that culminated in the 2022 LCK Spring and Summer titles, with Gen.G reaching the Worlds 2022 semifinals before losing to T1.[2]

JDG and 2023 Golden Road run

For the 2023 season, Ruler signed with JD Gaming in the LPL. JDG won the 2023 LPL Spring, the 2023 Mid-Season Invitational in London (defeating Bilibili Gaming 3-1), and the 2023 LPL Summer split. The team came one game away from the so-called "Golden Road" before losing to T1 in the Worlds 2023 semifinals.[2][3]

Return to Gen.G (2024–present)

Ruler returned to Gen.G for the 2024 LCK season, reuniting with Chovy. Gen.G won the 2024 Mid-Season Invitational in Chengdu and the 2025 Mid-Season Invitational in Vancouver back-to-back. Ruler reached the Worlds 2025 semifinals with Gen.G before being eliminated by KT Rolster.[3]

Legacy and impact

Ruler is widely regarded as one of the greatest ADCs in League of Legends history. He is one of only a handful of Korean players to win an LPL title and an MSI, and his Worlds 2017 Pentakill on Tristana remains one of the most replayed moments in League of Legends history.[2]

Personal life

Ruler is based in Seoul as of 2026 and lives at the Gen.G facilities. He is publicly close with longtime support partner CoreJJ and Gen.G teammates Chovy and Canyon. He maintains an active presence on Instagram.[1]
