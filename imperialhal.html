ImperialHal

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Phillip Dosen (born May 31, 1999), known online as ImperialHal, is an American professional Apex Legends player and the in-game leader for Team Falcons. Widely regarded as the best IGL in the history of competitive Apex, he won the 2023 ALGS Championship alongside teammates Verhulst and Reps, taking home a $600,000 share of the prize pool. With approximately $1.17 million in lifetime tournament earnings, he is the highest-earning competitive Apex player in history.[1][2]

Dosen previously played Counter-Strike: Global Offensive before transitioning to Apex shortly after the game's launch. He spent more than five years with TSM from March 2019 to May 2024 before joining Team Falcons, where he is paired with Australian rifler Genburten and Canadian recon player Zer0.[3] He is one of the most-watched personal POV streamers in Apex, broadcasting almost every competitive day on Twitch.

Early life

Dosen was born May 31, 1999 in the United States. He began his competitive shooter career in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive in his late teens before pivoting to Apex Legends in early 2019, just weeks after the game launched.[1]

Competitive career

Counter-Strike origins

Before Apex, Dosen competed in semi-professional CS:GO circles. The shooter fundamentals carried over directly to Apex, where he was identified early as one of the most mechanically reliable players in the North American region.

TSM era (2019-2024)

Dosen joined TSM on March 6, 2019, less than two months after the launch of Apex Legends. He won the X Games Minneapolis 2019 Apex Pro-Am ($30,000) and the Apex Legends Preseason Invitational 2019 in Krakow ($105,000), establishing TSM as the top org in the early competitive scene.[2]

After Respawn and EA launched the Apex Legends Global Series in 2020, Dosen led TSM to multiple Playoffs titles including the 2022 ALGS Year 2 Split 1 Playoffs ($100,000) and the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 1 Playoffs ($300,000). For long stretches, the TSM trio of Dosen, Verhulst and Reps was considered the most decorated lineup in Apex.[3]

2023 ALGS Championship

The 2023 ALGS Championship took place at the Birmingham BEC in July 2023. TSM, with Dosen as in-game leader, took the championship after a Match Point run that ended a stretch of near-misses for the org at the most prestigious LAN of the season. The $600,000 first-place share was the largest single payout of his career and ended a nearly four-year wait for an ALGS Championship trophy.[4]

Team Falcons (2024-present)

On May 28, 2024, Dosen left TSM and joined Team Falcons, where he was paired with Genburten and Zer0. The new lineup won the 2025 ALGS Open ($300,000) and finished runner-up at the 2026 ALGS Championship in Sapporo, taking home an additional $300,000.[2] The Falcons trio currently sits at the top of the AgentTV Power Rankings entering the Year 5 Split 2 Playoffs.

Legacy and impact

Dosen is broadly regarded by analysts and players as the defining IGL of competitive Apex Legends. His teams have placed top-five at every ALGS LAN they have qualified for since 2021, and he has played in three ALGS Championship grand finals across two organizations. As of May 2026, his $1,169,633 in tracked tournament earnings makes him the all-time leader in Apex Legends prize money.

He is also one of the most influential personalities in the Apex broadcast ecosystem, regularly co-streaming international LANs to his Twitch audience and shaping community narratives about meta and roster moves.

Personal life

Dosen has spoken publicly about his preparation routines and frequent solo ranked grinding outside of scrims. He is based in the United States and travels for both Pro League online play and major LANs.
