Albralelie

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Mac Kenzie Beckwith (born September 13, 2000), known online as Albralelie, is an American professional Apex Legends player currently signed to Team Nemesis. He is best known as a foundational member of the original TSM Apex roster from June 2019 to October 2023, where he played alongside ImperialHal and Reps on the lineup that defined early NA dominance in Apex.[1]

Beckwith has played for more than ten organizations across his career, including stand-in stints at Team Liquid, Cloud9 and FaZe Clan, plus full tenures at FURIA Esports, Twisted Minds and Hotdog Mafia. As of May 2026 his tracked tournament earnings total $177,635.[2]

Early life and gaming background

Mac Kenzie Beckwith was born on September 13, 2000, in the United States. He came up through the broader American Battle Royale community of the late 2010s, transitioning to Apex Legends shortly after the title's surprise launch in February 2019. The early Apex Legends competitive scene had no formal league structure, so the path to a professional roster ran almost entirely through community-organised tournaments, online showmatches and streamer-led pickup teams. Beckwith was identified as one of the most-promising young fraggers in that ecosystem inside a few months of formal competition.[1]

His scouting profile through 2019 emphasised the unusual aim consistency that he had brought into Apex from earlier shooter experience, paired with the willingness to take aggressive opening fights that would later become a defining feature of the original TSM identity. He was one of the youngest players to be considered for a tier-one organisation when TSM came calling in mid-2019.

Competitive career

Early career (2019)

Beckwith competed briefly for MRKN Clan in April 2019, the first salaried roster of his career, before TSM secured his signature in June. The MRKN stint was short, but it provided enough structured scrim exposure to validate the scouting reports that had been circulating through the early Apex community.[1]

TSM era (2019 to 2023)

Beckwith joined TSM on June 2, 2019, completing the iconic original trio of ImperialHal, Reps and Albralelie. The lineup quickly became the defining North American roster of pre-ALGS Apex Legends competition. They won the 2019 Apex Legends Preseason Invitational in Krakow for a $105,000 first-place share, the largest prize purse the title had paid out at the time, and were the dominant force in North American online play through 2020.[1]

Beckwith remained tied to TSM through October 2023 across active and inactive periods, an unusually long single-organisation tenure for the era. The TSM run produced the early-phase identity for the entire North American competitive scene, and his place inside that lineup made him one of the most-discussed individual fraggers of the title's first three seasons.

Stand-in years (2020 to 2022)

During lulls with TSM he stood in for several major international rosters, including Team Liquid from October 2020 to June 2021, Cloud9 from June 2021 to July 2022, and FaZe Clan from October to December 2022. With Cloud9 he placed fifteenth at the 2022 ALGS Championship and tenth at the 2022 Year 2 Split 2 Playoffs, the more durable LAN finishes from his stand-in stretch.[2]

The breadth of the stand-in run is unusual for a player who had already established a primary roster identity, and it gave him exposure to several different shotcalling structures and IGL philosophies during a formative period of the ALGS structure.

Recent rosters (2023 to present)

From 2023 onwards Beckwith cycled through FURIA Esports, Stallions, Twisted Minds and Hotdog Mafia, a free-agent stretch that has nonetheless produced regular LAN qualification. With Hotdog Mafia he placed thirteenth at the 2026 ALGS Championship in Sapporo for a $26,000 share. He signed with Team Nemesis on April 30, 2026 to anchor the next phase of his career.

Playstyle and analysis

Beckwith is a fragger-first profile in the North American mould, with a long-standing reputation for taking the first contact in close-range fights and converting opening damage into pressure for his teammates. His strongest competitive periods have come on rosters built around aggressive trio-fighting compositions, and his comfort with shotgun-and-SMG legend kits has shaped the draft choices on every team he has anchored. The trade-off, well documented across his post-TSM rosters, is that his individual peak fragging has not always paired well with the more disciplined macro style that has come to dominate the late-ring environment of the modern ALGS, a tension that explains the breadth of his roster history. Coaches who have worked with him cite his demo review as among the more honest in the player pool, with a willingness to identify his own positioning errors that has earned him steady respect within the competitive community.

Legacy and impact

Beckwith is one of the longest-tenured Apex Legends professionals in the world, with seven years of continuous high-level competition since the title's launch. His tenure with TSM spanned the entire pre-ALGS competitive era and the early ALGS years, making him a touchstone for the broader evolution of North American Apex. The original TSM trio is often cited as the lineup that established the structural template for serious-title shooter competition inside the title, and Beckwith's place inside that history has remained part of the broadcast conversation through every subsequent roster move.[1]

His tracked tournament earnings of $177,635 as of May 2026 understate his cultural impact on the competitive scene, since his most influential period at TSM coincided with a community-tournament era that paid out a fraction of modern ALGS prize pools. He is regarded as one of the era's defining personalities and a recurring presence in retrospective coverage of the title's early competitive history.

Personal life

Beckwith maintains an active streaming presence on Twitch under the handle Albralelie. His content has been a steady recommendation for new viewers exploring early-cycle Apex Legends competition, and he has been one of the more visible long-tenure NA pros across both broadcast and social platforms.[1]
