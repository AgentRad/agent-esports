m0NESY

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Ilya Osipov (Илья Осипов; born May 1, 2005), known online as m0NESY, is a Russian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to Team Falcons. A primary AWPer regarded as the defining sniper of the post-s1mple generation, he was named HLTV's second-best player of 2024 at age 19, the youngest top-two finish in the rankings' history.[1][2]

Osipov rose through the Natus Vincere youth system before being acquired by G2 Esports in early 2022, where he spent more than three years as the centerpiece of the European roster. He moved to Team Falcons on April 14, 2025 in one of the largest CS2 transfer deals on record. He has accumulated approximately $957,000 in tracked earnings and holds 6 tournament MVPs.[1][3]

Early life and gaming background

Osipov was born on May 1, 2005, in Moscow, Russia. He picked up Counter-Strike: Global Offensive at the age of eight, and by the time he was twelve he had built a reputation in Russian-language matchmaking lobbies that punched well above his rank. Coaches and content creators began circulating his demos on community forums, and his rapid scoping accuracy on the AWP became the focal point of the discussion. By his early teens he was already routinely playing against, and beating, established adult professionals in Faceit pugs.[1]

His first formal contracts came in 2019, when he was just fourteen, with the small Russian organisations NewBALLS and S-Gaming. The sample size at that level was modest, but the trajectory was unmistakable: he was identified by scouts inside Natus Vincere as the most promising junior AWPer in the CIS region, and the comparisons to a young s1mple began to follow him into nearly every interview. The pressure of those comparisons would shape both his preparation and his public persona over the next several years.[1]

Competitive career

Junior circuit and NaVi academy (2019 to 2021)

After short stints with NewBALLS and S-Gaming, Osipov entered the Natus Vincere youth pipeline in January 2020. He was officially promoted to the academy team, NaVi Junior, on March 11, 2021. The academy environment paired him with structured anti-strat preparation, demo review and IGL coaching for the first time, and his individual numbers in WePlay Academy League and DreamHack Open events drew the attention of every major European organisation.[1]

Osipov departed the NaVi Junior roster on December 8, 2021, freeing himself to pursue a tier-one move shortly before his sixteenth birthday. NaVi's main team already had s1mple in the AWP role, which made it impossible for the parent club to retain him at full salary. The exit was a polite acknowledgement that the prodigy had outgrown the academy framework, and the bidding war for his signature began almost immediately.

G2 Esports (2022 to 2025)

On January 3, 2022, Osipov signed with G2 Esports, taking over the primary AWP role on a roster anchored by NiKo. The leap from academy CS to a top-five international roster was unusually aggressive for a sixteen-year-old, but the adjustment took only weeks. He finished HLTV #7 in his rookie year, won BLAST Premier World Final 2022, and was named MVP of that event, the youngest Big-Event MVP in Counter-Strike history at the time.[2][3]

The G2 era only escalated from there. Osipov climbed to HLTV #4 in 2023, lifting IEM Cologne 2023 alongside NiKo and Aleksib in what remains G2's signature S-Tier title under the international project. His 2024 season is generally regarded as one of the great single-year AWP campaigns of the modern era: he posted five tournament MVPs across IEM Dallas, BLAST Fall Final, BLAST World Final and other events, finishing HLTV #2 behind only donk. By the time the project entered the CS2 Major cycle, however, the team had begun to plateau in deep playoff series, and a 2025 transfer became a question of when rather than if.

Team Falcons (2025 to present)

On April 14, 2025, Osipov transferred to Team Falcons, joining a Saudi-backed roster that had been openly assembling a super-team around former top-ten HLTV finishers. AGENT reporting at the time placed the buyout in the multi-million-dollar range, and the deal stands among the largest single-player transfers in CS2 history. The Falcons project paired m0NESY with established stars across multiple regions and reframed the AWPer's role from clear-cut centerpiece to symbiotic anchor.[4]

Through the second half of 2025, Falcons settled into a regular S-Tier semifinalist pattern, breaking through with an ESL Pro League Season 22 trophy in which Osipov was again named MVP. He finished HLTV #4 for 2025, his fourth consecutive top-ten ranking, an unbroken streak that began in his rookie year at G2.[2]

Recent form (2026)

The opening months of 2026 saw Falcons cement their status as a tournament fixture, with Osipov picking up a sixth career MVP at BLAST Rivals Fall 2025 and producing several signature highlight pieces during BLAST Bounty Winter 2026. He continues to anchor Falcons' Mirage and Inferno setups in particular, where his ability to convert opening duels into map-economy swings has separated the roster from its peers.

Playstyle and analysis

Osipov is most often described as an aggressive opening AWPer, but the framing undersells the structural way he uses the rifle. His default is to win the first picture on a map, fall back into a pre-rehearsed re-peek lane, and force the opposing entry pack to commit information before the trade. The result is a sniper whose impact registers in opening-pick win rate, of course, but also in opponents' rotation timings, which slow noticeably across a series against him. Coaches who have studied the role describe him as one of the few AWPers comfortable holding both deep and crossfire angles inside the same round, and his quick-scope accuracy on retake angles has reset what younger snipers consider a viable shot. He plays loose with positioning by superstar standards, and the trade-off has been that he is one of the most punishable elite AWPers when his read is wrong, a tension that defines his film.

  • Lifted IEM Cologne 2023 with G2 Esports, the organisation's signature S-Tier trophy of the international era.[3]
  • Finished HLTV #2 for 2024 with five tournament MVPs, behind only donk in a season widely regarded as the most stacked top of the rankings since 2018.[2]

  • Won ESL Pro League Season 22 with Falcons and added a fifth career tournament MVP medal.[4]
  • Ranked HLTV top-ten in every full season since his debut, the longest active such streak among AWPers under 22.[2]

Legacy and awards

Osipov's six tournament MVP medals (BLAST World Final 2022, IEM Dallas 2024, BLAST Fall Final 2024, BLAST World Final 2024, ESL Pro League Season 22, BLAST Rivals Fall 2025) include four Big-Event MVPs, an unusually high concentration for a player who turned twenty-one only in 2026. He is widely viewed as the natural heir to s1mple and ZywOo in the AWP lineage, and his opening-pick win rate has been the highest among elite snipers in nearly every season tracked since 2023.[2][3]

Beyond competition, Osipov is one of the most-followed Russian-speaking CS2 streamers and maintains an active presence on Twitch under the handle m0nesyof. His broadcasts pull a meaningful crossover audience from VKontakte and Telegram CS communities, and he has used the platform to mentor several younger CIS prospects entering the academy circuit.

Personal life

Osipov is based in Moscow but has bootcamped extensively in Belgrade and Berlin during his G2 and Falcons tenures. He has spoken openly in interviews about the unusual pressure of being labelled a generational AWPer from the age of fifteen, and has credited the structured environment of the NaVi Junior programme with much of his tactical development. He is a longtime fan of FC Spartak Moscow and has occasionally appeared in club-related social content during international breaks.[1]
