NiKo

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Nikola Kovač (born February 16, 1997), known online as NiKo, is a Bosnian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to Team Falcons. A primary rifler renowned for his pure aim and consistency, he has been a top-tier presence on the HLTV individual rankings every full year since 2016, finishing inside the global top five on six occasions.[1][2]

Across more than a decade at the top of the game, Kovač has played for three of the most recognizable organizations in Counter-Strike: mousesports (2015 to 2017), FaZe Clan (2017 to 2020), and G2 Esports (2020 to 2025), before joining Team Falcons on January 3, 2025. He holds 10 HLTV MVP medals, has reached two Major grand finals, and has accumulated approximately $1.84 million in tracked tournament earnings.[1][3]

Early life

Kovač was born February 16, 1997 in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina. His older cousin Nemanja Kovač, who plays under the alias huNter-, also became a professional Counter-Strike player and the two have been longtime teammates at the top level. NiKo began competing in Counter-Strike 1.6 as a young teen and transitioned to Counter-Strike: Global Offensive in 2014.[1]

Career

mousesports (2015 to 2017)

Kovač signed with mousesports on August 30, 2015 and was almost immediately the focal point of the European multinational roster. He posted top-15 HLTV finishes in 2016 and a top-three finish in 2017 on the strength of one of the most statistically dominant individual half-seasons in CS:GO history, despite the team failing to clear deep brackets at premier events.[2]

FaZe Clan (2017 to 2020)

NiKo joined FaZe Clan on February 19, 2017 to anchor a star-studded international roster alongside olofmeister, karrigan, rain, and GuardiaN. He reached his first Major grand final at ELEAGUE Major Boston 2018, where FaZe lost a five-map series to Cloud9. He finished HLTV #2 in 2017 and #3 in 2018, leading FaZe through a highly successful run of IEM and ESL Pro League finals.[3]

G2 Esports (2020 to 2025)

On October 28, 2020, NiKo signed with G2 Esports, where he was reunited with cousin huNter-. Across his five-year tenure G2 became one of the most consistent top-five teams in the world, capturing IEM Cologne 2023 and the BLAST Premier World Final 2022 and 2024, and reaching the PGL Major Stockholm 2021 grand final, where they lost 0-2 to Natus Vincere. Kovač was again HLTV #2 in 2023 and a top-five finisher in 2020, 2021, and 2024.[1][4]

Team Falcons (2025 to present)

On January 3, 2025, NiKo joined Team Falcons in a high-profile move, completing a Saudi-backed lineup that has rotated several star players including m0NESY, TeSeS, kyousuke, and longtime teammate niko9 / m0NESY. The Falcons CS2 division has become a regular S-Tier finalist and a perennial threat to the dominant Vitality and Spirit rosters.[5]

Legacy and awards

Kovač's 10 HLTV MVP medals place him among the most decorated tournament MVPs of his generation, with 9 of them earned at Big Events. He is one of only a handful of players to be ranked HLTV top 5 across three different consecutive console generations of Counter-Strike (CS:GO 1.6 era, CS:GO Source 2 transition, and CS2). His rivalry with s1mple through the late 2010s and early 2020s defined the rifler-versus-AWPer best-of-his-era debate.[2][3]

Outside of competition Kovač streams on Twitch and produces tutorial-heavy content on his YouTube channel, with a strong base of practice-focused fans. He is widely respected by other professionals as one of the cleanest pure aimers in the game.

Personal life

Kovač splits time between Sarajevo and Belgrade, Serbia. His brother and his cousin huNter- have been recurring teammates throughout his career. He has spoken in interviews about wanting to remain competitive into his early thirties and has discussed eventual coaching or analyst aspirations beyond his playing career.[1]
