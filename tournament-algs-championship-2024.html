About the Tournament
Daiwa House PREMIST DOME · Sapporo, Japan

The Year 4 ALGS Championship pulled forty trios into Hokkaido for a five-day finale to the 2023-24 season. EA chose the Daiwa House PREMIST DOME, an indoor baseball venue used for Hokkaido Nippon-Ham Fighters home games, and built a five-stage rig at second base. Forty teams across the four ALGS regions arrived after Pro League play and the Last Chance Qualifier in late January.

The Group Stage on January 29 and 30 sorted the field into a Winners Bracket of 20 and a Losers Bracket of 20, with three rounds against rotating opponents. Team Falcons, with the long-running TSM core of ImperialHal, Verhulst, and Reps in fresh new colours, paced the Winners Bracket. GoNext Esports, an entirely unsigned roster five months earlier, settled in at fourth and quietly racked up consistent top-five finishes.

Bracket Stage thinned the field down to the 20 finalists. Falcons and Alliance looked dominant. Shopify Rebellion, NA's brightest hope, scraped through. FURIA represented LAS, while two Japanese teams kept the home crowd loud as the field shrunk. The biggest story going into Sunday was that no NA team had ever won an international ALGS LAN held in Asia, and the bracket numbers said it would take something special.

The Match-Point Finals played out exactly that way. Falcons clinched their match-point spot first but failed to close. Alliance hit match-point on map seven, fronted by Effect's 17-frag Loba pop-off, and seemed to have the title in hand. GoNext got there a game later. On the ninth and final map, GoNext rotated late from a remote ridge into a 3-team final ring, won the closing fight clean, and lifted the trophy. Effect was still named MVP for his individual stat line, but the team trophy went to Greece, Portugal, and France.
