About the Tournament
Wuliangye Chengdu Performing Arts Center · May 2024

MSI 2024 ran a fully double-elimination format for the first time since 2022. Eight teams entered, the LCK and LPL got two seeds each, and the LCS, LEC, and PCS each got one. The bracket was best-of-three until the semifinals turned to best-of-five and the grand final stayed at the traditional Bo5.

Riot's biggest structural change for the year was attached to the trophy. The 2024 MSI champion locked in a direct slot at Worlds, an extra qualifier for that team's region, and a meaningful international leg-up the prior format had never offered. Gen.G's win pushed the LCK to four total Worlds slots that year.

T1 entered as defending Worlds champions and as betting favorites. They beat G2 and Team Liquid to start, dropped the upper-bracket final 1-3 to Gen.G, and returned through the lower bracket. The lower-final rematch with BLG was the series of the tournament until the actual grand final settled it.

Gen.G arrived hot off an LCK Spring title and never dropped a series in the upper bracket. Chovy's Hwei pocket pick on map two of the final cracked open BLG's mid-game tempo and Canyon's Maokai punished knight's mid pressure repeatedly. The 3-1 close-out came at midnight Chengdu time on a 41-minute Nexus push that started with a clean Baron throw from BLG.

Game four ended on the cleanest naked Nexus defense any MSI grand final has ever produced. BLG had Baron, base advantage, and the inhibitor open at 38 minutes. Lehends' Rell engage on knight at the inner turret crack flipped the fight 4-for-1 and Chovy's Hwei push closed the game on a clean teleport rotation.

Across the series Lehends posted a 4-1 series record on roaming supports and went 13/8/52 on the bracket-stage Rell games. The Finals MVP vote was a one-name ballot.
