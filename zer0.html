Zer0

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Rhys Perry (born June 29, 2000), known online as Zer0, is an Australian professional Apex Legends player currently signed to Team Liquid. He is one of the most decorated players in Apex history, having won the 2022 ALGS Championship, the 2022 Year 2 Split 2 Playoffs, and the 2023 Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs with DarkZero Esports.[1]

Perry first broke through with Reignite in the APAC South region, then formed the dominant DarkZero trio with Genburten and Sikezz from July 2022 to May 2024. He moved with Genburten and ImperialHal to Team Falcons in 2024 before joining Team Liquid in November 2025. As of May 2026 his tracked tournament earnings total $849,846.[2]

Early career

Perry was born June 29, 2000 in Australia. He competed for Excellence, EBC and EXO Clan during 2019 and 2020 before joining Reignite South in January 2021.

Competitive career

Reignite era

With Reignite from January 2021 to July 2022, Perry developed into the recon/IGL anchor of the APAC South scene alongside Genburten.

DarkZero (2022-2024)

Perry joined DarkZero on July 5, 2022 with Genburten and Sikezz. The trio won the 2022 ALGS Championship in Raleigh ($500,000), the 2023 Y3 Split 2 Playoffs in London ($300,000), and finished 2nd at the 2024 Year 4 Split 1 Playoffs ($160,000).[3]

Team Falcons (2024-2025)

On May 28, 2024 he joined Team Falcons with ImperialHal and Genburten. The lineup placed 2nd at the Esports World Cup 2024 ($375,000) and 3rd at the 2025 ALGS Championship ($210,000). He was moved to inactive status in September 2025.

Team Liquid (2025-)

Perry signed with Team Liquid on November 5, 2025, returning to a roster he had previously stood in for during 2020-2021. He is the centerpiece of Liquid's APAC-led 2026 lineup.

Legacy

Perry is one of just three players to have won an ALGS Championship and multiple Split Playoffs titles. With $849K in tracked earnings he sits in the all-time top five of Apex prize money.
