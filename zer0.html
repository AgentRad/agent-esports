Zer0

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Rhys Perry (born June 29, 2000), known online as Zer0, is an Australian professional Apex Legends player currently signed to Team Liquid. He is one of the most decorated players in Apex history, having won the 2022 ALGS Championship, the 2022 Year 2 Split 2 Playoffs, and the 2023 Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs with DarkZero Esports.[1]

Perry first broke through with Reignite in the APAC South region, then formed the dominant DarkZero trio with Genburten and Sikezz from July 2022 to May 2024. He moved with Genburten and ImperialHal to Team Falcons in 2024 before joining Team Liquid in November 2025. As of May 2026 his tracked tournament earnings total $849,846.[2]

Early life and gaming background

Rhys Perry was born on June 29, 2000, in Australia. He came up through the Oceanic shooter scene of the late 2010s, the same regional ecosystem that would later produce Genburten and a wider wave of APAC South stars. The Oceanic competitive infrastructure at the time relied heavily on online cups and grassroots community organisation rather than franchise-led structure, and Perry's early development reflected the region's emphasis on disciplined macro play to compensate for high-ping disadvantages on international servers.[1]

His scouting profile combined unusually disciplined recon-and-rotation instincts with a willingness to take the IGL voice on rosters where shotcalling responsibilities had not been formally assigned. The combination, rare in any region, would shape every roster he played for from 2021 onwards.

Competitive career

Grassroots beginnings (2019 to 2020)

Perry competed for Excellence, EBC and EXO Clan during 2019 and 2020, the early-cycle Oceanic projects that operated as feeder rosters for what would later become the regional Pro League pool. He briefly stood in for Team Liquid during this period, the first contact with an organisation he would eventually return to in late 2025.[1]

Reignite era (2021 to 2022)

Perry joined Reignite in January 2021 and stayed through July 2022, partnering with Genburten on a roster that became the headline Oceanic-region trio of the post-launch ALGS era. Across the Reignite tenure he developed into the recon and shotcalling anchor of the APAC South scene, the structural counterweight to Genburten's pure-fragger profile, and the partnership built into one of the most-watched lineups in international qualifiers.

DarkZero (2022 to 2024)

Perry joined DarkZero Esports on July 5, 2022, completing the trio with Genburten and Sikezz. The lineup is widely regarded as one of the strongest in Apex Legends history, and produced the most-decorated single-roster trophy window the title has yet seen. They won the 2022 ALGS Championship in Raleigh for the $500,000 first-place share, the 2023 Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs in London for $300,000, and added a runner-up finish at the 2024 Year 4 Split 1 Playoffs for $160,000.[3]

Within the DarkZero structure, Perry was the rotation manager, the player whose voice set the trio's positioning before the ring closed and whose recon-legend draft choices shaped the team's late-game strength. The combination of his macro voice and Genburten's individual ceiling produced a team identity that opposing rosters consistently tried, and largely failed, to replicate.

Team Falcons (2024 to 2025)

On May 28, 2024 Perry joined Team Falcons with ImperialHal and Genburten in one of the highest-profile signings in Apex Legends history. The trio combined three of the game's most-decorated names and immediately became the most-watched roster in the competitive scene. The lineup placed second at the Esports World Cup 2024 for a $375,000 share and third at the 2025 ALGS Championship for $210,000. He was moved to inactive status by Falcons in September 2025.

Team Liquid (2025 to present)

Perry signed with Team Liquid on November 5, 2025, completing a return to an organisation he had stood in for years earlier. He has been the centerpiece of Liquid's APAC-led 2026 roster construction, with the project built around his recon and shotcalling voice. Through the early 2026 ALGS calendar Liquid have settled into a regular LAN-finalist pattern with him at the centre of the team's macro identity.

Playstyle and analysis

Perry is one of the most cerebral macro voices in competitive Apex Legends. His role across DarkZero and Falcons leaned heavily on recon legends and zone-edge rotation control, and his shotcalling is built around the principle that an extra round of clean rotation usually produces more total damage than a contested fight. Coaches who have worked with him describe his pre-match preparation as among the most disciplined in the competitive pool, and his ability to read the upcoming ring split before the broadcast even cuts to the map overlay is a recurring talking point in coaching content. The trade-off is that his rosters tend to look passive in the early game by some viewers' standards, since his macro pattern often delays the team's first contested fight until the trio's positioning is structurally optimal. The pattern produced multiple championship runs, which is why the trade-off is widely accepted as a correct one.

Legacy and impact

Perry is one of just three players to have won an ALGS Championship and multiple Split Playoffs titles, and his trophy density across the DarkZero years places him among the most-decorated trios in the title's competitive history. With $849,846 in tracked tournament earnings as of May 2026, he sits inside the all-time top-five of Apex Legends prize money, a remarkable position for a player whose individual fragging numbers are deliberately framed around team output rather than highlight reel.[2]

His role as one of the central faces of Oceanic Apex has been a steady promotional asset for the region's broader case for international representation, and the Reignite-to-DarkZero career arc remains one of the most-cited path-to-pro examples for Australian competitive shooter players.

Personal life

Perry is one of the most prominent Australian competitive shooter professionals on the international circuit, and he maintains an active streaming presence on Twitch under the handle zer0. He has been a longtime advocate for the Oceanic region's place in the broader competitive Apex calendar.[1]
