Demon1

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Maximilian Mazanov (born September 7, 2002), known online as Demon1, is an American professional Valorant player. He won VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles with Evil Geniuses on August 26, 2023, and was named tournament MVP. The result, plus Evil Geniuses' run through Champions as the lowest seed, is widely considered one of the deepest underdog stories in Valorant history.[1][2]

Mazanov holds dual American and Russian citizenship. He was a tier-three player in 2022 before being signed by Evil Geniuses in late 2022 ahead of VCT Americas League. After a season with EG, a stint with Dragon Ranger Gaming in 2024-2025, he returned to North America with ENVY for the VCT 2026 season.[3]

Early life

Mazanov was born September 7, 2002. He holds dual citizenship of the United States and Russia. He came up through the Knights Arena Monthly Gauntlet circuit in 2022, posting standout duelist numbers in tier-three North American Valorant before being scouted by Evil Geniuses.[1]

Competitive career

Tier-three (2022)

Mazanov played for Shopify Rebellion's tier-three roster in early 2022 before stints with Knights and Akrew. He won the Knights Arena Monthly Gauntlet in 2022 and the Moonrise Showdown #2, drawing scouting attention from VCT Americas franchises ahead of the league's first season.

Evil Geniuses and Champions 2023

Mazanov signed with Evil Geniuses in November 2022 alongside jawgemo, Boostio, C0M and Ethan, joining what was widely considered the lowest-projected roster of the inaugural VCT Americas League season. EG finished the regular season at the bottom of the standings before catching fire in the playoffs.

EG placed third at the VCT Americas League 2023 regular season but qualified for Masters Tokyo, where they finished second after losing the Grand Final to Fnatic. Mazanov was named to the tournament's all-star duelist line.[2]

At VALORANT Champions 2023 in Los Angeles, Evil Geniuses ran the table from the Group Stage to the Grand Final and defeated Paper Rex 3-1 on August 26, 2023, to take the $1,000,000 winner's share. Mazanov was named tournament MVP, the first American player to receive the honor at VCT Champions.[3]

Dragon Ranger Gaming (2024-2025)

After parting ways with Evil Geniuses in early 2024 amid public roster disputes, Mazanov signed with Dragon Ranger Gaming in the VCT China league for the 2025 season. The team qualified for VALORANT Champions 2025, where they were eliminated in the group stage with a 13th-16th finish.

ENVY return (2025-present)

Mazanov signed a multi-year deal with the rebuilt ENVY organization in November 2025 ahead of the 2026 VCT Americas season. The signing reunited him with NA-based coaching staff and was framed by ENVY as a project to recapture Mazanov's 2023 form.[4]

Legacy and impact

Mazanov's 2023 Champions performance is widely regarded as one of the best individual tournament runs in Valorant history. He posted a 1.31 rating across the playoff bracket and led the event in first-blood percentage. As of May 2026, his $252,067 in tracked tournament earnings places him fifth on the all-time Valorant earnings list.

Personal life

Mazanov has spoken publicly about navigating the demands of professional play and personal mental health, including a public dispute with Evil Geniuses in 2024 that preceded his departure from the org. He maintains an active streaming schedule on Twitch and YouTube under the Demon1 handle.
