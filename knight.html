Knight

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Zhuo Ding (Chinese: 卓定; born May 29, 2002), better known as Knight, is a Chinese professional League of Legends player who plays mid lane for Bilibili Gaming in the LPL. He is widely regarded as the best mid laner in the LPL of his generation and is a 2024 League of Legends Worlds finalist with Bilibili Gaming.[1][2]

Knight debuted with Suning Gaming in 2018 before becoming the franchise mid laner of TOP Esports from 2019 to 2022. He joined Bilibili Gaming for the 2023 season and has remained as the team's foundational mid laner since.[2]

Early life

Zhuo Ding was born on May 29, 2002 in China. He climbed the Chinese solo queue ladder under the handle "Knight" and was signed by Suning Gaming in 2018 as a 16-year-old prospect.[1]

Competitive career

Suning debut (2018)

Knight played briefly for Suning Gaming in the 2018 LPL Spring split before being signed by TOP Esports for the 2019 season, where he became the team's franchise mid laner.[2]

TOP Esports era (2019–2022)

Knight anchored TOP Esports through some of the most dominant LPL regular-season runs of the era. The team won the 2020 LPL Spring split with Knight named LPL Finals MVP. TOP Esports also won the 2020 Mid-Season Cup and reached the 2020 Worlds semifinals before being eliminated by Suning. The team did not win an MSI or Worlds during the period.[2]

BLG era (2023–present)

For the 2023 season Knight signed a record-breaking contract with Bilibili Gaming, making him the highest-paid player in the LPL at the time. BLG reached the 2023 MSI final (lost to JDG 1-3), the 2024 MSI final (lost to Gen.G 1-3), and the 2024 World Championship final in London, where they lost to T1 2-3 in a five-game series.[2][3]

BLG won the 2024 LPL Summer split and reached Worlds 2025, where they were eliminated in the quarterfinals. Knight remains under contract with BLG through the 2026 season.

Legacy and impact

Knight is widely regarded as one of the most mechanically gifted mid laners in League of Legends history. As of 2026 he has not won an international title (Worlds or MSI) but has reached the final of every major international event in 2023 and 2024. He holds multiple LPL Regular Season MVP awards and is the longest-tenured top-five LPL mid laner.[2]

Personal life

Knight is based in Shanghai. He maintains a public presence on Weibo and Bilibili. He is known for an extremely soft-spoken interview style and a near-singular focus on solo queue training.[1]
