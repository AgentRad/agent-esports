Knight

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Zhuo Ding (Chinese: 卓定; born May 29, 2002), better known as Knight, is a Chinese professional League of Legends player who plays mid lane for Bilibili Gaming in the LPL. He is widely regarded as the best mid laner in the LPL of his generation and is a 2024 League of Legends Worlds finalist with Bilibili Gaming.[1][2]

Knight debuted with Suning Gaming in 2018 before becoming the franchise mid laner of TOP Esports from 2019 to 2022. He joined Bilibili Gaming for the 2023 season and has remained as the team's foundational mid laner since.[2]

Early life and gaming background

Zhuo Ding was born on May 29, 2002, in China. He came up through the Chinese solo-queue ladder of the late 2010s, where the volume and competitiveness of high-elo Korean Master and Challenger games on Chinese servers gave young mid laners an unusually deep pool of repetitions in a single calendar year. He climbed under the handle "Knight" and was identified by professional staff well before he had aged into LPL eligibility.[1]

His amateur reputation was built almost entirely on lane-phase mechanics. Even as a young teenager, his ability to win two-versus-two skirmishes against jungle pressure stood out on solo-queue VOD review, and his champion pool spanned both assassin and control-mage profiles. By 2018, Suning Gaming had committed to giving him an LPL roster spot at sixteen.

Competitive career

Suning debut (2018)

Knight played briefly for Suning Gaming in the 2018 LPL Spring split, an unfussy debut that nonetheless drew attention from larger organisations. The roster did not finish among the LPL leaders that split, but his individual numbers were strong enough that TOP Esports made a play for his signature ahead of 2019. He left Suning at the end of the year.[2]

TOP Esports era (2019 to 2022)

Knight became TOP Esports's franchise mid laner from 2019 to 2022, a four-year window that produced some of the most dominant LPL regular-season runs of the era. The team won the 2020 LPL Spring split with Knight named LPL Finals MVP, and TOP also won the 2020 Mid-Season Cup, the LPL's pandemic-era replacement event for MSI. Knight reached the 2020 Worlds semifinals with TOP before the team was eliminated by eventual finalist Suning Gaming.[2]

The TOP Esports years are often discussed as a cautionary tale about LPL roster construction. The lineup was statistically dominant in regular-season play but never closed an international title, and Knight's profile through that period became the archetype of the mechanically generational LPL mid laner who could not get over the international final hurdle. He left TOP at the end of 2022 in one of the most-watched free-agent windows in LPL history.

Bilibili Gaming era (2023 to present)

For the 2023 season, Knight signed a record-breaking contract with Bilibili Gaming, making him the highest-paid player in the LPL at the time. BLG was built around him as the structural mid lane anchor of an aggressive, fight-first roster. The team immediately reached the 2023 MSI final, where they lost 1-3 to JDG, then reached the 2024 MSI final and lost 1-3 to Gen.G, two consecutive international finals in eighteen months without a title.[2]

BLG also reached the 2024 World Championship final in London, where they fell 2-3 to T1 in a five-game series defined by the matchup between Knight and Faker. The series put BLG on the cusp of the title that had eluded Knight at TOP, but the 2-3 defeat extended one of the more frustrating finalist careers in League of Legends history. BLG won the 2024 LPL Summer split as compensation, with Knight central to the team's pre-Worlds form, and reached Worlds 2025 before being eliminated in the quarterfinals.

Recent form (2026)

Knight remains under contract with Bilibili Gaming through the 2026 season as the structural anchor of the roster, and the team's early-2026 form has continued to feature him as one of the LPL's signature mid lane carries. The 2026 international cycle is widely framed in LPL coverage as the next chance for Knight to convert his finalist record into an international title.

Playstyle and analysis

Knight is the LPL's archetypal mechanically generational mid laner. His champion pool spans nearly the entire mid-lane meta, with comfort across both assassin and control-mage axes, and his lane-phase wave management is the most-cited structural advantage of nearly every roster he has played for. He has been a steady proponent of post-laning roams that look low-impact in isolation but compound across the mid game, and his vision-trade efficiency in the 8-to-14 minute window is regularly highlighted in coaching content. The trade-off across his career has been a roster-level tendency to over-index on his lane-phase advantage, which has produced LPL regular-season dominance but a recurring pattern of international-final losses in series where opposing teams compress the early-game window into a coin-flip skirmish. He is one of the most demanding mid laners in the world to play against, and his mechanical ceiling on classic LPL picks remains a benchmark for younger Chinese prospects.

Legacy and impact

Knight is widely regarded as one of the most mechanically gifted mid laners in League of Legends history. As of 2026 he has not won an international title at Worlds or MSI, but he has reached the final of every major international event across the 2023 and 2024 cycles, an unusual record for any player at the position. He holds multiple LPL Regular Season MVP awards and is the longest-tenured top-five LPL mid laner of the post-Rookie generation.[2]

Within the LPL ecosystem his contracts have repeatedly reset the market for the mid-lane position, and his free-agent windows are routinely cited as the inflection points for league-wide salary expectations. He is regarded as one of the central figures of Chinese League of Legends through the early-CS2 era of competitive gaming.

Personal life

Knight is based in Shanghai and maintains an unusually quiet public profile by LPL star standards. He posts irregularly on Weibo and Bilibili and is known for a soft-spoken interview style and a near-singular focus on solo-queue training. His off-stage personality has been part of the appeal that LPL brand partners have leaned on in marketing campaigns through the BLG era.[1]
