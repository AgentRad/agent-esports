Sweetdreams

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Christopher Sexton (born July 2, 1999), known online as Sweetdreams, is an American professional Apex Legends player. He spent the bulk of his career as the in-game leader for NRG, anchoring one of North America's most consistent ALGS finalist trios from 2021 to 2023. As of May 2026 he is between rosters after a stint with Luminosity Gaming, with $415,059 in tracked tournament earnings.[1]

Sexton broke through at Rogue in 2019 and joined NRG on January 7, 2021. The NRG trio of Sweetdreams, Gild and Nafen became a fixture of every major ALGS LAN, finishing 2nd at the 2023 Year 3 Split 1 Playoffs ($160,000) and reaching the grand finals stage of the 2022 Championship. After NRG parted ways with the roster in September 2023, he later moved to Luminosity Gaming from January 2024 to February 2025.[2]

Early career

Sexton was born July 2, 1999 in the United States. He entered the Apex Legends competitive scene during the game's first year, signing with Rogue on February 10, 2019.[1]

Competitive career

Rogue (2019-2021)

Sexton spent nearly two years with Rogue from February 2019 through January 2021, establishing himself as one of the most disciplined IGLs in North America during the pre-ALGS era of Apex.

NRG (2021-2023)

He joined NRG on January 7, 2021 and stayed for nearly three years. With NRG he finished 2nd at the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 1 Playoffs ($160,000), 9th at the 2022 ALGS Championship ($48,000), and reached multiple Pro League finals. He left the roster on September 15, 2023.[2]

Luminosity (2024-2025)

After a brief gap Sexton signed with Luminosity Gaming on January 11, 2024. With Luminosity he finished 7th at the 2025 ALGS Championship in Sapporo for $80,000 before parting ways on February 13, 2025.

Legacy

Sweetdreams is regarded as one of the longest-serving IGLs in NA Apex. He has appeared at every ALGS Championship LAN from 2021 through 2025 and is one of just a handful of players to clear $400K in lifetime earnings without an ALGS Championship trophy.
