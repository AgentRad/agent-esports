SquishyMuffinz

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Mariano Arruda (born November 29, 2000), better known online as SquishyMuffinz, is a Canadian professional Rocket League player and content creator. He currently competes for G.A.S. and has held a content-creator deal with Cloud9 since July 11, 2024. He is one of the most-watched personalities in the game's competitive history and the franchise face of the Cloud9 Rocket League brand.[1]

Arruda was the right-side striker on the Cloud9 roster that won the RLCS Season 6 World Championship in November 2018, and he is one of only a handful of players whose career has spanned the full Psyonix-to-Epic publishing transition. He has accumulated approximately $498,410 in tracked tournament earnings.[1][2]

Early life and gaming background

Arruda was born on November 29, 2000 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He came up through the early Rocket League ladder during the RLCS Season 1 era, where he was already a fixture in the Canadian-American open ladder by the time he was sixteen. The handle is a play on the soft-handling, deceptive style he was known for in early ranked play, and it has remained unchanged across his entire competitive career.[1]

Competitive career

Cloud9 (2017 to 2020)

Arruda joined Cloud9 in 2017 alongside Gimmick and Torment, the trio that would deliver the organisation its first Rocket League world title. Cloud9 won the RLCS Season 6 World Championship in November 2018 over Team Dignitas, with Arruda finishing as the highest-rated striker in the bracket. The Cloud9 era is widely considered the apex period for the cluster of North American freestyle-leaning rosters that defined the late-2010s competitive identity of the region.[2]

NRG and the rebuild years

He moved to NRG Esports after the Cloud9 trio split, joining the roster around jstn for the back end of the Psyonix-published era. The team won three Season X North American Majors (Winter, Spring and the NA Championship) in 2021, the most decorated regional run of his career outside of the Worlds title. He subsequently bounced through Rule One and the streamer-driven Lil Step Bros stack as the post-Epic format reorganised the North American region's free-agent ecosystem.[1]

G.A.S. (2024 to present)

On November 26, 2024 he signed with G.A.S., a North American organisation aiming to qualify for the RLCS 2025 closed regional. The signing dovetailed with his Cloud9 content-creator deal, struck five months earlier on July 11, 2024, which keeps him in the orange-and-black colours for stream-side branding while G.A.S. handles his competitive contract.

Playstyle and analysis

Arruda is the textbook North American freestyle striker. He plays a high-aerial, high-feint style with one of the cleanest air-roll-left ceilings in the game's history, and his Cloud9 era set the template for what amateur players still copy in modern ranked. The structural caveat in the more recent meta is that the same style of high-risk freestyle has been gradually written out of the top of the competitive scene as the game has become more demand-shot driven, and his ability to translate streamer mechanics into series wins has been the dominant analytical question around him since 2022. He still owns a top-tier first-touch profile and a rare two-handed flick repertoire, and the residual upside is the largest single-game ceiling in the North American region. He plays primarily on a controller with a low deadzone and has been a vocal advocate for raising sensitivity defaults in coaching content for newer players.

Streaming and content

Arruda is one of the most-watched Twitch streamers in Rocket League, with a steady mid-five-figure concurrent audience for ranked sessions and a long-running highlight pipeline on his YouTube channel. The content side of his profile has eclipsed the competitive side in concurrent eyeballs since 2022, and it is the through-line that informed the 2024 Cloud9 deal.[1]
