Caps

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Rasmus Borregaard Winther (born November 17, 1999), better known as Caps, is a Danish professional League of Legends player who plays mid lane for G2 Esports in the LEC. He is widely regarded as the most decorated and accomplished mid laner in Western League of Legends history, with multiple LEC titles, an MSI championship in 2019 and two World Championship finals appearances.[1][2]

Caps debuted with Fnatic in 2017 and joined G2 Esports for the 2019 season, where he has remained ever since. In 2019 he became the first Western player to reach a World Championship final on two different rosters, having lost the 2018 final with Fnatic before losing the 2019 final with G2 to FunPlus Phoenix.[2]

Early life

Rasmus Borregaard Winther was born on November 17, 1999 in Denmark. He began playing League of Legends competitively in solo queue under the handle "Caps" before being picked up by Dignitas EU in 2016 at the age of 16. He played briefly in the EU Challenger Series before moving to Fnatic Academy and then to the main Fnatic roster ahead of the 2017 EU LCS Spring Split.[1]

Competitive career

Fnatic era (2017–2018)

Caps replaced veteran mid laner Febiven on Fnatic for the 2017 season. He earned the nickname "Baby Faker" during 2018 thanks to a notable champion pool, an aggressive mechanical style and a string of LEC and international standout performances.[1]

In 2018 Caps led Fnatic to the EU LCS Spring and Summer titles and to the 2018 World Championship final in Incheon, where they lost 0-3 to Invictus Gaming. It was the first time a Western team had reached a Worlds final since 2011.

G2 and 2019 super-team

In November 2018 Caps signed with rival G2 Esports for the 2019 season, joining a roster of Wunder, Jankos, Perkz and Mikyx. The team won the 2019 LEC Spring and Summer titles, and won the 2019 Mid-Season Invitational in Vietnam, defeating Team Liquid 3-0 in the final.[2]

G2 reached the 2019 World Championship final in Paris, where they were swept 0-3 by FunPlus Phoenix. The defeat marked the second consecutive year a Western roster lost the Worlds final 0-3, and remains the closest a Western team has come to the Summoner's Cup since 2011.

2020–present

Caps has continued to anchor G2 Esports through multiple roster cycles, winning further LEC titles and qualifying for Worlds and MSI on a near-annual basis. The team has not returned to a World Championship final but reached the semifinals at Worlds 2020 and the quarterfinals at Worlds 2025, where they lost to T1.[3]

In April 2026 Caps signed a three-year contract extension with G2 Esports, ending speculation about a possible move to the LCS or LCK. The deal makes him the longest-tenured G2 player in the organization's League of Legends history.

Legacy and impact

Caps is widely regarded as the best mid laner the Western regions have ever produced. He holds the record for most LEC playoffs appearances and most LEC titles by a mid laner, and is one of only two Western players to reach a Worlds final on two different rosters. His 2019 MSI title remains the most recent international trophy won by a Western League team.[2]

Personal life

Caps maintains a public presence on Twitch and X. His older brother, Anders "Wunder" Winther, was his teammate on G2 Esports from 2019 through 2021. The two are the only sibling pair to win the LEC together.[1]
