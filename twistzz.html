Twistzz

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Russel David Kevin Van Dulken (born November 14, 1999), known online as Twistzz, is a Canadian professional Counter-Strike 2 player currently signed to FaZe Clan. A consistent rifler known for his entry presence and clutch capacity, he is the only player to have won the Intel Grand Slam with two different organizations: Team Liquid in 2019 and FaZe Clan in 2023.[1][2]

Van Dulken's career has alternated between Team Liquid and FaZe Clan across three separate signings, and he has appeared in two Major grand finals, winning the PGL Major Antwerp 2022 against Natus Vincere. He has accumulated approximately $1.84 million in tracked earnings, placing him among the top-earning North American CS players in history.[1][3]

Early life and gaming background

Russel Van Dulken was born on November 14, 1999, in Pinawa, a small town in southeastern Manitoba, Canada. He came up through the Canadian and North American mixed-team scene in 2015, the same wave of teenage Counter-Strike: Global Offensive players that included future stars like NAF and Stewie2K. The North American pipeline at the time was thin compared with Europe, and Van Dulken bounced between rosters as a fragger-for-hire while still attending high school.[1]

He signed his first amateur deal at sixteen and was playing more than full-time hours in online qualifiers within months. The early experience of grinding open qualifiers from a small Manitoba town, often on inconsistent ping, became part of the player's later reputation as a mechanically self-built rifler. He has cited the demanding online ladders of that era, particularly the shift from CEVP to ESEA Premier, as the formative environment for his entry pacing.

Competitive career

Early career (2015 to 2017)

Van Dulken's early circuit included Tectonic, SapphireKelownaDotCom, Team AGG, KKona, Team SoloMid, and Misfits Gaming. None of these stops were headline projects, but the cumulative experience of bouncing between rosters built his comfort across both T-side entry and CT-side anchoring. By late 2016 his Faceit and ESEA numbers had begun to draw the attention of established North American organisations.[1]

On April 14, 2017, he signed with Team Liquid, the move that would define the first half of his career. Liquid at the time was assembling a younger North American core around nitr0 and EliGE, and Van Dulken was brought in as the rifling spark to complement that veteran spine.

Team Liquid (2017 to 2020)

Van Dulken's first stint with Team Liquid produced what is still widely considered the most decorated North American Counter-Strike season in history. Across mid-2019 the roster of Stewie2K, NAF, EliGE, nitr0 and Twistzz won Intel Grand Slam Season 2 inside a 63-day stretch, lifting four S-Tier trophies in succession and briefly displacing Astralis at the top of the world rankings. The streak included BLAST Pro Series Los Angeles, ESL Pro League Season 9 Finals, ESL One Cologne 2019 and IEM Chicago 2019.[2]

Van Dulken was named HLTV MVP at ESL One New York 2018 and IEM Sydney 2019, finishing HLTV #9 in 2019 in a season top-loaded with European rifling stars. The team's run was eventually broken by the 2020 pandemic and a roster reshuffle that moved him out of his preferred tactical roles, and he departed Liquid in late 2020 amid significant speculation about his next step.

FaZe Clan (2021 to 2023)

On January 30, 2021, Van Dulken joined FaZe Clan, completing the karrigan, ropz, rain, broky roster that has since been described as the most complete European-international lineup of the post-Astralis era. The roster's chemistry was immediate. They won IEM Katowice 2022 in February and the BLAST Premier Spring Final the same season, and then took the centerpiece trophy of their era at the PGL Major Antwerp 2022, defeating Natus Vincere in the grand final.[3]

The lineup added a second Intel Grand Slam in 2023, completing Intel Grand Slam Season 4 with FaZe and making Van Dulken the only Counter-Strike player to win the Grand Slam with two different organisations. He was ranked HLTV top-twenty across every full season of the FaZe run, and the roster's heavy international pace, often described as "karrigan tempo," fit his entry style better than any rotation he had played for at Liquid.

Return to Liquid (2023 to 2025)

Van Dulken rejoined Team Liquid on December 7, 2023, anchoring a rebuild around NAF and YEKINDAR. The project produced multiple S-Tier semifinal runs and qualified for both the PGL Major Copenhagen 2024 and the BLAST.tv Austin Major 2025 playoffs, but never produced the trophy run the organisation had hoped for. His role on the roster shifted toward a more flexible support-rifle profile, and he posted his lowest ratings since 2017 across that two-year window.[1]

FaZe Clan return (2025 to present)

On September 20, 2025, Van Dulken rejoined FaZe Clan, reuniting with karrigan and broky in place of frozen. The reformed core slotted him back into his trusted roles almost immediately, and the team reached the grand final of the StarLadder Budapest Major 2025 in the fall, where they fell 1-3 to Team Vitality. Through early 2026 he has remained a fixture in FaZe's S-Tier lineups.[4]

Playstyle and analysis

Van Dulken is best described as a high-impact entry rifler who reads tempo as well as he aims. His T-side game is built around taking the second body through a piece of map, a half-beat behind the lurker or AWP, which lets him punish hesitating defenders without absorbing the first opening duel himself. On the CT side he favours aggressive forward holds and rotational anchoring, often trading himself into a numbers-down situation that his teammates can convert. Coaches who have worked with him highlight his comfort with mid-round resets, a relatively rare trait among elite riflers, and his clutch numbers in 1v1s have routinely placed him among the top of his role group. The trade-off is that his individual frag totals tend to compress in disciplined teams that ask him to play later off the bomb, which is part of why his peak years coincided with karrigan-led FaZe and the explosive 2019 Liquid roster.

Legacy and awards

Van Dulken's two Intel Grand Slams across two organisations is a record unique to him in Counter-Strike, and his PGL Antwerp 2022 victory remains FaZe Clan's only Major win and one of only three Majors won by a fully international roster. He has been ranked HLTV top-twenty in six different years, a level of longevity matched by very few non-superstar riflers and one that has anchored North American Counter-Strike's argument for representation throughout an era dominated by European and CIS players.[2][3]

He has been a vocal advocate for roster transparency and player-side contract reform, particularly during the transition periods between his Liquid and FaZe runs. His pre-game and post-game interview presence is a familiar fixture of the FaZe broadcast cycle, and he is regarded by the community as one of the more thoughtful media performers among active riflers.

Personal life

Van Dulken splits time between Manitoba and the FaZe Clan training facility in Berlin during competitive seasons, returning to Canada during off-windows. He has been a vocal commentator on roster moves and player welfare, particularly during transition periods between organisations, and has spoken in interviews about the challenges of maintaining North American social ties while bootcamping for European LANs eight months of the year.[1]
