Faker

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Lee Sang-hyeok (Korean: 이상혁; born May 7, 1996), better known by his in-game name Faker, is a South Korean professional League of Legends player and a part-owner of T1, the organization for which he plays mid lane. Widely regarded as the greatest League of Legends player of all time, Faker is the first and only player in history to win the League of Legends World Championship six times (2013, 2015, 2016, 2023, 2024, 2025).[1][2]

Faker has played his entire professional career for SK Telecom T1, later rebranded as T1 in 2019. As of May 2026 he is the longest-tenured active player in the LCK and is widely credited with elevating the popularity of competitive League of Legends in South Korea and globally. He is also a two-time Mid-Season Invitational champion (2016, 2017) and has won 10 LCK titles.[2]

Early life

Lee Sang-hyeok was born on May 7, 1996 in Seoul, South Korea. He was raised primarily by his father and grandparents and attended Mapo High School. He began playing League of Legends in 2011 and rapidly climbed the Korean solo queue ranking, where he was known under the name "GoJeonPa" before adopting "Faker".[1]

By late 2012 he was the top-ranked player on the Korean ladder and had drawn the attention of multiple Korean organizations. SK Telecom T1 signed him in February 2013 ahead of his graduation from high school.

Competitive career

2013 debut and first World title

Faker debuted with SK Telecom T1 #2 on April 7, 2013 against CJ Entus Blaze, defeating star mid laner Ambition in his first competitive game. SKT T1 #2 went on to win the OGN Champions Summer 2013 split and qualified for the 2013 World Championship in Los Angeles, where they swept Royal Club 3-0 in the final at the Staples Center. Faker, then 17, was the youngest World Champion at the time.[2]

2015–2017 SKT dynasty

After a 2014 in which SKT failed to qualify for Worlds, the team rebuilt around Faker and won the 2015 World Championship in Berlin against KOO Tigers (3-1) and the 2016 World Championship in Los Angeles against Samsung Galaxy (3-2).[2] SKT also won the Mid-Season Invitational in 2016 and 2017, with Faker named tournament MVP at MSI 2016.

SKT reached a third consecutive Worlds final in 2017 but lost 0-3 to Samsung Galaxy in Beijing. The image of Faker crying alone on stage afterward became one of the most circulated photographs in esports history.[3]

The drought (2018–2022)

Between 2018 and 2022, Faker did not win a World Championship despite multiple deep runs. SKT, rebranded as T1 in 2019, lost in the semifinals at Worlds 2019 to G2 Esports and did not reach the final at any Worlds during this stretch. He continued to win LCK titles regularly during the period and remained the league's most recognizable player.[2]

2023–2025 three-peat

Faker and a young T1 roster of Zeus, Oner, Gumayusi and Keria won the 2023 World Championship in Seoul, defeating Weibo Gaming 3-0 in front of a home crowd at the Gocheok Sky Dome. The team repeated at the 2024 World Championship in London, defeating Bilibili Gaming 3-2, and again at the 2025 World Championship, defeating KT Rolster 3-1.[2][4]

The 2025 victory made Faker the first six-time World Champion in League history and made T1 the first organization to three-peat the title since SKT's own near-three-peat in 2015–2016. Faker was named Finals MVP in 2023 and 2024.

Legacy and impact

Faker is widely considered the greatest League of Legends player in history and one of the most decorated competitors in any esports title. As of 2025 he is the only player in history with six World Championship titles. He has been featured in Time, ESPN, The New York Times, and was on the cover of the inaugural Korean edition of Forbes 30 Under 30 in 2019.[1][3]

In 2023 he was named the LCK MVP for the regular season, and he carries the all-time records for most LCK games played, most LCK wins and most pentakills in LCK history. He became a part-owner of T1 in 2020 as part of his contract extension.

Personal life

Faker is known for an exceptionally private personal life and disciplined training schedule. He lives in the T1 player house in Seoul and is widely reported by teammates to maintain a strict practice and sleep regimen. He has served as a global brand ambassador for Nike, Razer, BMW Korea and Red Bull, and was a torchbearer at the 2018 Pyeongchang Olympic torch relay.[1]
