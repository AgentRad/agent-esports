About
Coaching career & philosophy

Adam Bahriz, better known as OnSet, came up through the late CS:GO matchmaking ladder and was an early adopter of Valorant during the 2020 closed beta. After a brief stint trialing for tier-two North American rosters he leaned into the analyst path in 2022, joining a Sentinels academy project and then climbing into the senior support staff. He took the head coach chair midway through the rebuild that preceded the Masters Madrid run.

The Madrid 2024 win is still the headline. Sentinels arrived as a wildcard via the VCT Americas Kickoff bracket, dropped only one map across the playoffs, and lifted the trophy on Spanish stage with a roster nobody outside the building had picked to make it past quarters. OnSet's role in that run was to keep the rotations simple while leaning into TenZ and zekken on duelist combos that other teams had already shelved. Since then Sentinels have stayed in the regional title picture, taking the VCT Americas Stage 1 2025 bracket and posting consistent Top 6 finishes at LAN.

His coaching identity is built around utility audit. Where most VCT staff prep around comp, OnSet prep is reportedly built around grenade and ability spend per round, mapped against the average win-rate delta when those abilities are saved versus thrown early. It is unglamorous work, but the result is a team that almost never runs dry on smokes in the back half of a half. Players have publicly described his half-time talks as short and almost surgical · he targets one structural fix per pause rather than relitigating every round.
