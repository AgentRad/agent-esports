Genburten

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Noyan Ozkose (born September 20, 1999), known online as Genburten, is an Australian-Turkish professional Apex Legends player currently competing for Crazy Thieves. He is widely considered one of the most mechanically gifted players in Apex Legends history and is the defining rifler of the Oceanic competitive scene.[1]

Ozkose won the 2022 ALGS Championship with DarkZero Esports alongside Zer0 and Sikezz, a $500,000 first-place share, then added the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs ($300,000) with the same lineup. He moved to Team Falcons in May 2024 to play with ImperialHal and Zer0, then joined 100 Thieves later that year. As of May 2026 his tracked tournament earnings total $635,828.[2]

Early career

Ozkose was born September 20, 1999. He competed for HeadHunters and MiTH T2 in 2020 before joining Reignite South in January 2021, the move that put him on the international radar.

Competitive career

Reignite era

He played for Reignite from January 2021 to July 2022, becoming the headline rifler of the APAC South region. His clip reel and 1-vs-3 highlights from this era helped raise the profile of the Oceanic scene.

DarkZero (2022-2024)

Ozkose joined DarkZero Esports on July 5, 2022. With Zer0 and Sikezz, he won the 2022 ALGS Championship in Raleigh ($500,000) and the 2023 Y3 Split 2 Playoffs in London ($300,000). DarkZero also placed 2nd at the 2024 ALGS Year 4 Split 1 Playoffs ($160,000).[3]

Team Falcons (2024)

On May 28, 2024 he joined Team Falcons with ImperialHal and Zer0 in one of the highest-profile signings in Apex history. The trio finished 2nd at the Esports World Cup 2024 ($375,000) before Genburten departed in September 2024.

100 Thieves and Crazy Thieves

After a brief stint with The Dojo, he signed with 100 Thieves on November 11, 2024 and later moved with the trio to Crazy Thieves on January 7, 2026.

Legacy and play style

Genburten is the centerpiece of nearly every "best mechanical Apex player" debate. His tracking, recoil control, and ability to take 1-vs-many engagements at LAN are treated as benchmarks. His DarkZero stretch from 2022 to early 2024 is one of the most decorated runs by a single player in Apex history.
