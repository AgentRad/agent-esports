Genburten

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 7, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Noyan Ozkose (born September 20, 1999), known online as Genburten, is an Australian-Turkish professional Apex Legends player currently competing for Crazy Thieves. He is widely considered one of the most mechanically gifted players in Apex Legends history and is the defining rifler of the Oceanic competitive scene.[1]

Ozkose won the 2022 ALGS Championship with DarkZero Esports alongside Zer0 and Sikezz, a $500,000 first-place share, then added the 2023 ALGS Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs ($300,000) with the same lineup. He moved to Team Falcons in May 2024 to play with ImperialHal and Zer0, then joined 100 Thieves later that year. As of May 2026 his tracked tournament earnings total $635,828.[2]

Early life and gaming background

Noyan Ozkose was born on September 20, 1999. He grew up in Australia with Turkish family heritage, and built his early gaming reputation on the Oceanic competitive ladder of late-stage Battle Royale shooters before transitioning to Apex Legends shortly after the game's launch in 2019. The Oceanic region's high-ping disadvantage in international servers shaped much of his early development, and his preparation work emphasised mechanical precision in close-range fights as a way to compensate for the structural lag against North American opponents.[1]

His early ranked play attracted regional pickup-team invites well before any salaried offers. By the time he committed to formal competition in 2020, his tracking and recoil control on the R-99 had become the most-shared clips in Australian Apex content circles, and he was identified as the player most likely to break the Oceanic region's reputation for thin LAN representation.

Competitive career

Oceanic origins (2020 to 2021)

Ozkose began formal competition in 2020 with HeadHunters and MiTH T2, two early-cycle Australian rosters that played most of their fixtures online during the global pandemic restrictions. The fixture density was modest, but the rosters served as the entry into structured scrim blocks and gave him his first prolonged exposure to a coaching environment. By the end of 2020 he had established himself as the most coveted Oceanic free agent at the rifler position.[1]

Reignite era (2021 to 2022)

He joined Reignite in January 2021 and stayed with the project through July 2022. Reignite became the headline Oceanic-region roster of the post-launch ALGS era, and Ozkose's clip reel and one-versus-three highlights from this period became the spine of the international community's awareness of the APAC South region. The team's deep ALGS qualifier runs put Ozkose into direct comparison with the established North American and European stars, and the public consensus by 2022 was that the Oceanic region had produced one of the best raw fragger profiles in the world.

DarkZero (2022 to 2024)

Ozkose joined DarkZero Esports on July 5, 2022, completing the trio with Zer0 and Sikezz. The lineup is widely regarded as one of the strongest in Apex Legends history. Together they won the 2022 ALGS Championship in Raleigh, securing the $500,000 first-place share, and added the 2023 Year 3 Split 2 Playoffs title in London for a further $300,000 the following year.[3]

DarkZero also placed second at the 2024 ALGS Year 4 Split 1 Playoffs for $160,000, completing a run of finals appearances that produced one of the most decorated single-roster trophy windows in Apex history. The roster's identity was shaped by Ozkose's willingness to take aggressive opening fights from positions most rosters avoid, with Zer0 and Sikezz providing the structural support to convert his openings into ring-closing pushes.

Team Falcons (2024)

On May 28, 2024, he joined Team Falcons with ImperialHal and Zer0 in one of the highest-profile signings in Apex Legends history. The trio combined three of the most-decorated names in the game and quickly became the most-watched roster in the competitive scene. They finished second at the Esports World Cup 2024 for a $375,000 payout, the largest single-event finish of his Falcons stint, before he departed the project in September 2024.

100 Thieves and Crazy Thieves (2024 to present)

After a brief stint with The Dojo, Ozkose signed with 100 Thieves on November 11, 2024, joining the organisation's Apex revival project. He later moved with the trio to Crazy Thieves on January 7, 2026, in a re-branded continuation of the same competitive core. Through the early 2026 ALGS calendar he has remained a regular LAN finalist and one of the highest-rated individual fraggers in any region.

Playstyle and analysis

Genburten is the centerpiece of nearly every "best mechanical Apex player" debate, and the framing has held across multiple meta cycles and weapon-tuning patches. His game is built around tracking and recoil control on close-range automatics, and his comfort taking one-versus-many engagements at LAN is treated as a benchmark for the role rather than as a highlight novelty. He is most dangerous in mid-ring rotations, where his ability to convert a contested zone into an extra round of looting feeds his roster's late-game compositions. His preferred legend rotations have shaped DarkZero and Falcons's broader compositions, with both rosters favouring assault-focused legend lockins that maximise his time-on-target rather than scaling support utility. The trade-off in his style has been a tendency to over-commit to fights that are not strictly necessary, a tension that has been visible during smaller-event series where the roster's path to a top-three finish does not require high-variance pushes.

Legacy and impact

Genburten is the defining rifler of the Oceanic competitive scene and one of the most-cited mechanical benchmarks in Apex Legends history. His DarkZero stretch from 2022 to early 2024 is one of the most decorated runs by a single player in any era of the ALGS, and his trio movements with Zer0 across DarkZero, Falcons and Crazy Thieves have repeatedly reshaped the broader competitive market for top-tier rifler signings.[2]

His tracked tournament earnings of $635,828 as of May 2026 place him among the highest-earning Oceanic competitive shooter players in any title, and his presence at LAN has been one of the most consistent draws on the ALGS broadcast cycle.

Personal life

Ozkose is of Turkish heritage and has been one of the most visible Australian-Turkish esports professionals on the international circuit. He maintains an active streaming presence on Twitch under the handle genburten, where his content tends to focus on ranked solo-queue play during off-cycle weeks. He is regarded inside the competitive scene as one of the more approachable headline players in pre-game and post-game media.[1]
