Aydan

From AgentTV, the serious source for esports. Last updated May 8, 2026 by AGENT staff.

Aydan Conrad (born April 25, 1999), known online as Aydan, is an American professional Call of Duty Warzone player and content creator currently signed to Gen.G Esports. Conrad is one of the most prominent crossover competitors of his generation, having transitioned from a high-profile Fortnite career into Call of Duty's Warzone competitive ecosystem in 2021. He won the Esports World Cup 2025 Warzone championship, the discipline's biggest individual title, and has accumulated approximately $647,732 in tracked tournament earnings.[1]

Conrad began his professional Fortnite career as a controller pioneer at Ghost Gaming in the late Battle Royale era, where he was widely regarded as the strongest controller player in North America. He moved into the Warzone discipline in March 2021 with New York Subliners as a content creator, then signed competitively with Twisted Minds in June 2024 and Gen.G Esports on December 9, 2025.[1]

Early life and Fortnite career

Aydan Conrad was born on April 25, 1999 in the United States. He came to broad public attention as a Fortnite controller player during the 2018 to 2020 stretch, where he was repeatedly cited as the discipline's most accomplished controller competitor and one of the loudest community advocates for controller-PC parity in the title's settings. He represented Ghost Gaming across multiple Fortnite seasons before transitioning to Call of Duty in 2021.[1]

Warzone career

New York Subliners (2021 to 2024)

Conrad signed a content creator contract with New York Subliners on March 17, 2021. The role was primarily streaming-focused, but he also competed in early Warzone tournament invitationals and the first cycle of the World Series of Warzone, where the prize pools first justified full-time competitive practice. He left the Subliners on May 2, 2024 ahead of the EWC bracket.

Twisted Minds (2024 to 2025)

Conrad signed competitively with Twisted Minds on June 27, 2024 in the Saudi-backed organisation's expansion into Warzone. He won the World Series of Warzone Season 1 NA Trios with the org and finished third at both the 2024 Esports World Cup Warzone and the 2024 WSOW Global Final. The breakout moment was the 2025 Esports World Cup, where Conrad and his Twisted Minds trio won the discipline's flagship event in Riyadh and a $250,000 share of the prize pool. He was widely cited as the standout individual of the bracket.[1]

Gen.G Esports (2025 to present)

Conrad signed with Gen.G Esports on December 9, 2025 in the organisation's first headlining Warzone signing. The role is again hybrid, with content responsibilities anchored in the Gen.G creator stable and competitive responsibilities anchored in the WSOW and EWC brackets the team is now scheduling around.

Playstyle and analysis

Conrad is the discipline's reference controller competitor across both Fortnite and Warzone, anchored by the cleanest controller-aim profile of his generation and a willingness to take the longest gunfights in any rotation. His play style in Warzone is built around the kind of high-volume, mid-range engagements that the EWC and WSOW formats reward, where the cumulative kill count across three trios maps decides placement points alongside the final-circle wins. His Twisted Minds trio in 2025 was widely cited as the cleanest end-game decision-making unit of the EWC bracket, with Conrad doing the lion's share of the rotational shooting and his teammates handling the support and zone management. The transition away from a pure trios role into the Gen.G project will be the structural test of his 2026 season.

Legacy and content

  • 2024: 3rd · Esports World Cup Warzone (Twisted Minds)
  • 2024: 3rd · World Series of Warzone Global Final

Conrad streams full-time on Twitch, where his Warzone audience is consistently among the largest individual draws on the discipline's broadcast surface. His Twitter account at @aydan remains the default reference point for crossover Fortnite-and-Warzone roster news.[1]
